投資解讀

盤面定義

一、UST 收盤與 Curve 變化

6/1 不是 Duration 鬆動，而是油價衝擊帶動的前端主導偏空收斂。

官方 UST 收盤顯示 6/1 美債以前端上行為主：2Y 收 4.05%、日升 7bp，5Y 收 4.18%、日升 5bp，10Y 收 4.47%、日升 2bp，20Y 收 4.99%、日升 1bp，30Y 收 4.99% 持平。這組收盤讓 2s10s 收斂 5bp 至 42bp、5s30s 收斂 5bp 至 81bp，型態屬偏空收斂，而不是乾淨的偏多收斂。

週線仍保留回落，但月線壓力已經回來。2Y、5Y、10Y、30Y 相較 5/22 仍分別低 8bp、9bp、9bp、8bp，可是一拉長到 5/1，2Y、5Y、10Y 已分別高出 17bp、16bp、8bp。這代表市場不是只在交易單日油價，而是在把五月以來的成長韌性、AI 資本支出與能源風險重新放進高利率維持更久框架。

盤中的真正訊號是前端先被快速重定價，但尾盤還沒有演變成失序拋售。油價跳升後，市場材料顯示 10Y 一度逼近 4.5%，2Y 也被推向 4.08% 附近；但官方收盤仍落在 10Y 4.47%、2Y 4.05%，代表資金是在重估政策風險，而不是全面撤出 Duration。對組合來說，這比較像前端風險被重新喚醒，長端則繼續被 5% 附近的期限溢價上限壓住。

主驅動是能源與地緣政治，而且它直接作用在前端政策溢價。WTI 日內一度跳升 6.5% 至 93 美元，收盤約在 92.35 美元；伊朗停止透過中介交流、Hormuz 風險重新升溫，讓市場把油價上行同步翻譯成整體通膨溢價、Fed 再定價空間與美元避險需求。這不是單純商品風險，而是會沿著汽油、運輸、供應商交貨與通膨期待一路傳進利率定價；也就是說，2Y 被重新抬高不是意外，而是油價衝擊自然先落在前端。

成長數據沒有提供反彈保護。ISM 製造業 PMI 升至 54.0、為四年高位，4月是 52.7；價格分項雖從 84.6 降至 82.1、也低於 85.0 預估，但仍是非常高的成本壓力讀數。對 Curve 來說，PMI 偏強代表成長疑慮帶動的買盤不成立，價格分項偏高則代表前端仍要尊重通膨風險，兩者合在一起就是 2Y 上行幅度大於 10Y。

跨資產確認同樣偏向偏空收斂。S&P 500 收 7,599.96、日升 0.26%，Nasdaq 收 27,086.81、日升 0.42%，AI 領漲讓股指維持高位；WTI 約升 4.96%、黃金約跌 1.41%，顯示市場交易的是油價與實質利率，而不是全面避險。當股票風險沒有崩、信用技術面仍強、美元維持偏強，美債長端很難得到完整衰退避險買盤。

定價邏輯

二、美債走勢解讀

前端重新定價 Fed 政策選擇權，長端守在 5% 門檻附近但沒有真正轉多。

本輪走勢應解讀為油價衝擊帶動的前端重定價。2Y 日升 7bp、10Y 日升 2bp、30Y 持平，說明市場先處理 Fed 反應函數，而不是直接放大期限溢價。若投資人把 30Y 沒有同步上行視為長端安全訊號，會忽略 30Y 仍收在 4.99%、距離 5.00%-5.10% 壓力區很近。

Fed swaps 的定價已經很明確。Fed swaps 已完全反映 2027 年 3 月前升息，10 月仍給約一半機率，12 月 FOMC 日期附近約有 20bp 的升息幅度。把這組定價和 WTI 92.35、ISM 54.0、價格分項 82.1 放在一起看，代表市場已重新承認能源衝擊與成長韌性還能把前端政策溢價往上推；只要這三個訊號不退，2Y 就仍比 10Y 更容易先被抬高。

通膨持續性不只來自油價本身。ISM 價格分項 82.1 雖低於預估，但供應商交貨、提前拉貨訂單、運輸成本與燃料價格共同指向企業成本壓力仍在；韓國 CPI 3.1% 高於 2.9% 預估、核心 2.5%，也證明亞洲價格壓力並非只停留在能源消息面。這會讓 Fed、BOK、ECB 都更難在六月用單一軟數據轉鴿。

美元走強是這輪定價的第二條傳導，而且它影響的不只是匯率。DXY 收 99.20、日升 0.18%，歐元約 1.1615，日圓接近 159.70；美元同時受避險需求、AI 成長敘事與高利率維持更久定價支撐。對美債來說，真正重要的不是美元能不能壓低輸入通膨，而是它正在同步擠壓非美央行與 EM 資產負債表，讓全球前端政策溢價不容易退。這也是為什麼台幣投資人不能把表面 NAV 改善直接視為可留存報酬。

長端的訊號則更混合。30Y 持平於 4.99%，10s30s 收斂至 52bp，表面上看長端沒有被油價嚇到；但 20Y 與 30Y 都停在 5% 附近，且全球央行與財政供給未轉友善。這意味組合不應把 Curve 收斂當作長端反彈訊號，而應把它視為前端重新定價、長端仍等標售需求與實錢資金承接力確認；在 30Y 明確脫離 5% 壓力區以前，中段 Duration 可以保留，但 20Y+ 不適合提前加碼。

跨市驗證

三、其他市場動向

信用技術面仍穩，但宏觀背景已轉向發行人篩選。

跨市驗證先看歐洲前端，因為這一層先決定全球 Duration 能不能真正鬆下來。Schnabel 已把伊朗戰事帶來的通膨衝擊定義成可能讓通膨預期失錨的風險，而 ECB 消費者調查也顯示三年期通膨期待只從 3.0% 小降至 2.9%，一年期仍在 4%，五年期在 2.4%。只要歐元區 CPI 往 3.2% 靠攏，歐洲前端就不會讓政策溢價輕易退場；對美債來說，這代表全球前端壓力還在，美債也很難單靠一筆軟數據重回多頭。

歐洲第二層壓力在財政與房貸傳導，而且它對長端不是乾淨利多。英國房價出現近一年最大跌幅，代表房貸利率壓力已經開始傷到需求；同時稅制複雜度與整體稅負上升，則讓成長拖累與財政溢價同時存在。這種組合會壓弱需求，卻不一定同步壓低長端供給與期限溢價，所以對英國公債與 UST 長端來說，更像是成長放緩與財政壓力並存，而不是單向受益的 Duration 多頭。

亞洲這一層更接近直接的政策約束，而且它不只影響本地利率。韓國 CPI 3.1% 高於預估，交通、娛樂文化、餐飲與家用品服務項目都維持偏高增幅，而 BOK 上次會議已經有兩位委員主張升息；韓國政府同時加強債市溝通並下調六月發債計畫，說明當局是在用供給管理防止殖利率失序。這代表亞洲並不是單純承接美債回落，而是本地前端也在面對自己的通膨與政策壓力；只要這一層沒有鬆，後面看到的美元資產 NAV 改善就更難自然轉成可留存報酬。

日本與印度提供的是第三層外溢壓力。日本企業獲利仍穩，但資本支出意外轉弱，意味 BOJ 在 6/16 決策前，必須同時平衡通膨、投資與日圓 159.70 附近的匯率壓力；印度則要面對油價、盧比、補貼與財政赤字的同步約束。這種組合代表即使市場預期 RBI 短期按兵不動，印度主權 Curve 也很難完全跟著美債降溫；對配置來說，EM 與亞洲本地利率部位不能因單日 UST 收斂就回頭追收益，必須先確認油價與 FX 壓力一起退。

AI 融資熱度本身也是跨市驗證的一部分。現在要看的不是單一新券能不能過，而是 AI 資本支出是否繼續把信用需求與通膨壓力綁在一起。CoreWeave 相關芝加哥資料中心擬發行 8.5 億美元高收益債、15 年租約收入約 22 億美元；今年 AI 相關風險債已接近 270 億美元，而過去兩個月 AI 相關高收益債供給也超過 200 億美元。這代表信用資金仍願意追逐成長主題，但同時也把能源、半導體與勞動成本壓力重新帶回宏觀定價。

歐洲與美國貸款市場則提供更直接的資金價格訊號。這一段真正要看的不是需求有沒有消失，而是資金價格已經壓到多薄。Citadel Securities 擬把 39.5 億美元 2031 年貸款改展為 42.5 億美元、2033 年到期，初始定價約基準 +200bp、99.5-99.75；歐洲借款人也同步嘗試把歐元定期貸款利差下調 25-50bp，背後則是新供給偏少、年初以來歐洲貸款發行約 1,248 億美元且年減 8%。這代表市場流動性仍願意承接更薄的利差補償，但也意味 Credit / ETF 配置不能只把利差壓縮當成利多；接下來更重要的是哪些發行人還能低讓利再融資，哪些資本結構已經需要靠更高票息或更強條款保護來支撐估值。

壓力面集中在私募信貸、重整與主權條款。這一段真正證明的是，品質分化已經不只發生在貸款定價，而是開始進入高收益、重整與主權條款本身。FS KKR 擬發至少 4 億美元高收益債，殖利率討論在 7% 中段到偏高區間，而 Moody's 與 Fitch 已把其降至非投資等級，KKR 又以 3 億美元特別股資本支援，代表市場已經要求更高風險補償與更明確的贊助方承諾。Atalian 約 9.19 億歐元 2028 債券由持有約 60% 部位的債券人接管，並把約三分之二負債轉成股權；Zambia 2053 債券的 13.6 億美元回購則因清理性贖回條款與 0.5% 可能升至 7.5% 的票息跳升引發債權人抵制。這代表接下來比的不只是資金有沒有進場，而是條款、資產覆蓋與重整順位能不能撐住估值。

配置動作

四、配置含義

先降低方向性 Duration，保留中段逢高買入，信用只買高品質收益。

配置上先守 10Y 4.45% / 4.55% 區間，再決定要不要把 Duration 往中段加回去。10Y 官方收盤 4.47% 仍在區間中央，若 10Y 收破 4.55% 或 2Y 站上 4.15%，代表油價衝擊已從消息面風險轉成 Fed 定價；在那之前，5Y-10Y 可以保留逢高買入彈性，但 20Y+ 還不適合當主要避險工具。

Curve 配置要承認偏空收斂。2s10s 已收斂至 42bp，若跌破 40bp，前端政策溢價會比長端期限溢價更快傷害組合；若重新回到 45bp 以上且 10Y 未破 4.55%，才代表偏空收斂壓力緩和。短線更適合用中段收益與下滑收益，而不是大幅加長端 Duration 風險。

Credit / ETF 配置的重點不是全面加風險，而是把資金留給能穿過品質分化的高品質收益。AI 資料中心、Citadel 貸款與歐洲貸款重定價都說明，高品質資產仍能在低讓利環境下完成融資；但 FS KKR、Atalian 與 Zambia 已經把風險重新拉回資產負債表、條款保護與重整順位。因此對高收益或私募信貸連動曝險，不能只看票息，還要先檢查贊助方支持、資產覆蓋、到期延展條款、新增資金優先順位，以及再融資能否在不大幅讓利下完成。

台幣計價海外債券 ETF 要拆開利率、匯率與利差。美元走強短線有利台幣 NAV，但如果美元強勢來自油價衝擊與前端重定價，利率損失可能抵銷匯率助益；因此高評等短中天期產品優於長天期與低品質 HY beta。若 WTI 回到 90 美元下方且 10Y 重新低於 4.45%，才適合把 NAV 改善視為利率主導。

EM 與亞洲 rates 不宜追逐擁擠收益。韓國 CPI 意外、印度財政 / 油價壓力、日圓 160 風險與 Zambia 收購爭議都指向篩選風險上升；對 EM 硬通貨，應偏向有 IMF 能見度、條款透明與外匯緩衝的主權債；對本地市場，先等央行反應與能源價格穩定，再提高收益部位。

交易觸發

五、後續觀察重點

核心觸發先盯 WTI 95、10Y 4.55、2Y 4.15，再看非農就業。

第一個核心觸發在能源。這一段先看 WTI 95 能不能持續把前端往上推：若 WTI 連續站上 95 美元、Brent / Hormuz 風險沒有緩和，通膨溢價就會從商品面推進到 Fed 定價；到那一步，2Y 4.15% 會比 10Y 4.55% 更重要，因為前端會先反映升息再定價空間。

第二個核心觸發在 10Y 4.45% / 4.55% 區間。這一段先看 10Y 區間能不能守住：若 10Y 回到 4.45% 下方且 2Y 同步回落，代表市場重新相信油價衝擊可以被吸收；但若 10Y 收破 4.55%，即使 30Y 沒有同步大幅上行，也該把 Duration 立場先降回基準或以下。這個區間決定的是中段能不能重新加碼，而不是單純看長端有沒有撐住。

第三個核心觸發在勞動市場與 ISM 後續分項。這一段先看就業與價格分項能不能一起維持韌性：ISM 54.0 已經證明製造業不是衰退交易，價格分項 82.1 也仍偏高；若非農就業、初領失業金或薪資數據繼續維持韌性，Fed swaps 目前反映的 2027 升息定價就更像起點，而不是尾端。那時候市場要重估的，不只是前端利率，還包括中段能不能繼續當最安全的表達。

第四個核心觸發在信用一級市場品質。這一段真正要盯的是新券還能不能低讓利完成：若 AI 相關 HY 或貸款重定價仍能以有限讓利完成，高品質收益就有機會延續，台幣投資人看到的 NAV 改善也比較可能代表收益真的留得住，而不只是匯率順風；但若 FS KKR 類型發行人需要更高票息、歐洲重定價被投資人拒絕，代表技術面開始失效，ETF 與 HY 風險都該下修。

第五個核心觸發在亞洲央行。這一段不是只看 BOJ 會不會動，而是看匯率順風能不能真的留下來：韓國 CPI 3.1%、核心 2.5%、JPY 159.70 與印度財政 / 油價壓力，會一起決定亞洲前端能不能鬆。若 BOJ 6/16 低於市場期待且日圓再測 160，亞洲 FX 壓力就可能抵銷美元資產的利率收益；到那一步，前一段看到的 NAV 改善也不能直接當成可留存報酬。

一、UST 收盤與 Curve 變化6/1 不是 Duration 鬆動，而是油價衝擊帶動的前端主導偏空收斂。

官方 UST 收盤顯示 6/1 美債以前端上行為主：2Y 收 4.05%、日升 7bp，5Y 收 4.18%、日升 5bp，10Y 收 4.47%、日升 2bp，20Y 收 4.99%、日升 1bp，30Y 收 4.99% 持平。這組收盤讓 2s10s 收斂 5bp 至 42bp、5s30s 收斂 5bp 至 81bp，型態屬偏空收斂，而不是乾淨的偏多收斂。

週線仍保留回落，但月線壓力已經回來。2Y、5Y、10Y、30Y 相較 5/22 仍分別低 8bp、9bp、9bp、8bp，可是一拉長到 5/1，2Y、5Y、10Y 已分別高出 17bp、16bp、8bp。這代表市場不是只在交易單日油價，而是在把五月以來的成長韌性、AI 資本支出與能源風險重新放進高利率維持更久框架。

盤中的真正訊號是前端先被快速重定價，但尾盤還沒有演變成失序拋售。油價跳升後，市場材料顯示 10Y 一度逼近 4.5%，2Y 也被推向 4.08% 附近；但官方收盤仍落在 10Y 4.47%、2Y 4.05%，代表資金是在重估政策風險，而不是全面撤出 Duration。對組合來說，這比較像前端風險被重新喚醒，長端則繼續被 5% 附近的期限溢價上限壓住。

主驅動是能源與地緣政治，而且它直接作用在前端政策溢價。WTI 日內一度跳升 6.5% 至 93 美元，收盤約在 92.35 美元；伊朗停止透過中介交流、Hormuz 風險重新升溫，讓市場把油價上行同步翻譯成整體通膨溢價、Fed 再定價空間與美元避險需求。這不是單純商品風險，而是會沿著汽油、運輸、供應商交貨與通膨期待一路傳進利率定價；也就是說，2Y 被重新抬高不是意外，而是油價衝擊自然先落在前端。

成長數據沒有提供反彈保護。ISM 製造業 PMI 升至 54.0、為四年高位，4月是 52.7；價格分項雖從 84.6 降至 82.1、也低於 85.0 預估，但仍是非常高的成本壓力讀數。對 Curve 來說，PMI 偏強代表成長疑慮帶動的買盤不成立，價格分項偏高則代表前端仍要尊重通膨風險，兩者合在一起就是 2Y 上行幅度大於 10Y。

跨資產確認同樣偏向偏空收斂。S&P 500 收 7,599.96、日升 0.26%，Nasdaq 收 27,086.81、日升 0.42%，AI 領漲讓股指維持高位；WTI 約升 4.96%、黃金約跌 1.41%，顯示市場交易的是油價與實質利率，而不是全面避險。當股票風險沒有崩、信用技術面仍強、美元維持偏強，美債長端很難得到完整衰退避險買盤。

二、美債走勢解讀前端重新定價 Fed 政策選擇權，長端守在 5% 門檻附近但沒有真正轉多。

本輪走勢應解讀為油價衝擊帶動的前端重定價。2Y 日升 7bp、10Y 日升 2bp、30Y 持平，說明市場先處理 Fed 反應函數，而不是直接放大期限溢價。若投資人把 30Y 沒有同步上行視為長端安全訊號，會忽略 30Y 仍收在 4.99%、距離 5.00%-5.10% 壓力區很近。

Fed swaps 的定價已經很明確。Fed swaps 已完全反映 2027 年 3 月前升息，10 月仍給約一半機率，12 月 FOMC 日期附近約有 20bp 的升息幅度。把這組定價和 WTI 92.35、ISM 54.0、價格分項 82.1 放在一起看，代表市場已重新承認能源衝擊與成長韌性還能把前端政策溢價往上推；只要這三個訊號不退，2Y 就仍比 10Y 更容易先被抬高。

通膨持續性不只來自油價本身。ISM 價格分項 82.1 雖低於預估，但供應商交貨、提前拉貨訂單、運輸成本與燃料價格共同指向企業成本壓力仍在；韓國 CPI 3.1% 高於 2.9% 預估、核心 2.5%，也證明亞洲價格壓力並非只停留在能源消息面。這會讓 Fed、BOK、ECB 都更難在六月用單一軟數據轉鴿。

美元走強是這輪定價的第二條傳導，而且它影響的不只是匯率。DXY 收 99.20、日升 0.18%，歐元約 1.1615，日圓接近 159.70；美元同時受避險需求、AI 成長敘事與高利率維持更久定價支撐。對美債來說，真正重要的不是美元能不能壓低輸入通膨，而是它正在同步擠壓非美央行與 EM 資產負債表，讓全球前端政策溢價不容易退。這也是為什麼台幣投資人不能把表面 NAV 改善直接視為可留存報酬。

長端的訊號則更混合。30Y 持平於 4.99%，10s30s 收斂至 52bp，表面上看長端沒有被油價嚇到；但 20Y 與 30Y 都停在 5% 附近，且全球央行與財政供給未轉友善。這意味組合不應把 Curve 收斂當作長端反彈訊號，而應把它視為前端重新定價、長端仍等標售需求與實錢資金承接力確認；在 30Y 明確脫離 5% 壓力區以前，中段 Duration 可以保留，但 20Y+ 不適合提前加碼。

三、其他市場動向信用技術面仍穩，但宏觀背景已轉向發行人篩選。

跨市驗證先看歐洲前端，因為這一層先決定全球 Duration 能不能真正鬆下來。Schnabel 已把伊朗戰事帶來的通膨衝擊定義成可能讓通膨預期失錨的風險，而 ECB 消費者調查也顯示三年期通膨期待只從 3.0% 小降至 2.9%，一年期仍在 4%，五年期在 2.4%。只要歐元區 CPI 往 3.2% 靠攏，歐洲前端就不會讓政策溢價輕易退場；對美債來說，這代表全球前端壓力還在，美債也很難單靠一筆軟數據重回多頭。

歐洲第二層壓力在財政與房貸傳導，而且它對長端不是乾淨利多。英國房價出現近一年最大跌幅，代表房貸利率壓力已經開始傷到需求；同時稅制複雜度與整體稅負上升，則讓成長拖累與財政溢價同時存在。這種組合會壓弱需求，卻不一定同步壓低長端供給與期限溢價，所以對英國公債與 UST 長端來說，更像是成長放緩與財政壓力並存，而不是單向受益的 Duration 多頭。

亞洲這一層更接近直接的政策約束，而且它不只影響本地利率。韓國 CPI 3.1% 高於預估，交通、娛樂文化、餐飲與家用品服務項目都維持偏高增幅，而 BOK 上次會議已經有兩位委員主張升息；韓國政府同時加強債市溝通並下調六月發債計畫，說明當局是在用供給管理防止殖利率失序。這代表亞洲並不是單純承接美債回落，而是本地前端也在面對自己的通膨與政策壓力；只要這一層沒有鬆，後面看到的美元資產 NAV 改善就更難自然轉成可留存報酬。

歐洲與美國貸款市場則提供更直接的資金價格訊號。這一段真正要看的不是需求有沒有消失，而是資金價格已經壓到多薄。Citadel Securities 擬把 39.5 億美元 2031 年貸款改展為 42.5 億美元、2033 年到期，初始定價約基準 +200bp、99.5-99.75；歐洲借款人也同步嘗試把歐元定期貸款利差下調 25-50bp，背後則是新供給偏少、年初以來歐洲貸款發行約 1,248 億美元且年減 8%。這代表市場流動性仍願意承接更薄的利差補償，但也意味 Credit / ETF 配置不能只把利差壓縮當成利多；接下來更重要的是哪些發行人還能低讓利再融資，哪些資本結構已經需要靠更高票息或更強條款保護來支撐估值。

壓力面集中在私募信貸、重整與主權條款。這一段真正證明的是，品質分化已經不只發生在貸款定價，而是開始進入高收益、重整與主權條款本身。FS KKR 擬發至少 4 億美元高收益債，殖利率討論在 7% 中段到偏高區間，而 Moody's 與 Fitch 已把其降至非投資等級，KKR 又以 3 億美元特別股資本支援，代表市場已經要求更高風險補償與更明確的贊助方承諾。Atalian 約 9.19 億歐元 2028 債券由持有約 60% 部位的債券人接管，並把約三分之二負債轉成股權；Zambia 2053 債券的 13.6 億美元回購則因清理性贖回條款與 0.5% 可能升至 7.5% 的票息跳升引發債權人抵制。這代表接下來比的不只是資金有沒有進場，而是條款、資產覆蓋與重整順位能不能撐住估值。

四、配置含義先降低方向性 Duration，保留中段逢高買入，信用只買高品質收益。

配置上先守 10Y 4.45% / 4.55% 區間，再決定要不要把 Duration 往中段加回去。10Y 官方收盤 4.47% 仍在區間中央，若 10Y 收破 4.55% 或 2Y 站上 4.15%，代表油價衝擊已從消息面風險轉成 Fed 定價；在那之前，5Y-10Y 可以保留逢高買入彈性，但 20Y+ 還不適合當主要避險工具。

Curve 配置要承認偏空收斂。2s10s 已收斂至 42bp，若跌破 40bp，前端政策溢價會比長端期限溢價更快傷害組合；若重新回到 45bp 以上且 10Y 未破 4.55%，才代表偏空收斂壓力緩和。短線更適合用中段收益與下滑收益，而不是大幅加長端 Duration 風險。

Credit / ETF 配置的重點不是全面加風險，而是把資金留給能穿過品質分化的高品質收益。AI 資料中心、Citadel 貸款與歐洲貸款重定價都說明，高品質資產仍能在低讓利環境下完成融資；但 FS KKR、Atalian 與 Zambia 已經把風險重新拉回資產負債表、條款保護與重整順位。因此對高收益或私募信貸連動曝險，不能只看票息，還要先檢查贊助方支持、資產覆蓋、到期延展條款、新增資金優先順位，以及再融資能否在不大幅讓利下完成。

台幣計價海外債券 ETF 要拆開利率、匯率與利差。美元走強短線有利台幣 NAV，但如果美元強勢來自油價衝擊與前端重定價，利率損失可能抵銷匯率助益；因此高評等短中天期產品優於長天期與低品質 HY beta。若 WTI 回到 90 美元下方且 10Y 重新低於 4.45%，才適合把 NAV 改善視為利率主導。

EM 與亞洲 rates 不宜追逐擁擠收益。韓國 CPI 意外、印度財政 / 油價壓力、日圓 160 風險與 Zambia 收購爭議都指向篩選風險上升；對 EM 硬通貨，應偏向有 IMF 能見度、條款透明與外匯緩衝的主權債；對本地市場，先等央行反應與能源價格穩定，再提高收益部位。

五、後續觀察重點核心觸發先盯 WTI 95、10Y 4.55、2Y 4.15，再看非農就業。

第一個核心觸發在能源。這一段先看 WTI 95 能不能持續把前端往上推：若 WTI 連續站上 95 美元、Brent / Hormuz 風險沒有緩和，通膨溢價就會從商品面推進到 Fed 定價；到那一步，2Y 4.15% 會比 10Y 4.55% 更重要，因為前端會先反映升息再定價空間。

第二個核心觸發在 10Y 4.45% / 4.55% 區間。這一段先看 10Y 區間能不能守住：若 10Y 回到 4.45% 下方且 2Y 同步回落，代表市場重新相信油價衝擊可以被吸收；但若 10Y 收破 4.55%，即使 30Y 沒有同步大幅上行，也該把 Duration 立場先降回基準或以下。這個區間決定的是中段能不能重新加碼，而不是單純看長端有沒有撐住。

第三個核心觸發在勞動市場與 ISM 後續分項。這一段先看就業與價格分項能不能一起維持韌性：ISM 54.0 已經證明製造業不是衰退交易，價格分項 82.1 也仍偏高；若非農就業、初領失業金或薪資數據繼續維持韌性，Fed swaps 目前反映的 2027 升息定價就更像起點，而不是尾端。那時候市場要重估的，不只是前端利率，還包括中段能不能繼續當最安全的表達。

第四個核心觸發在信用一級市場品質。這一段真正要盯的是新券還能不能低讓利完成：若 AI 相關 HY 或貸款重定價仍能以有限讓利完成，高品質收益就有機會延續，台幣投資人看到的 NAV 改善也比較可能代表收益真的留得住，而不只是匯率順風；但若 FS KKR 類型發行人需要更高票息、歐洲重定價被投資人拒絕，代表技術面開始失效，ETF 與 HY 風險都該下修。

第五個核心觸發在亞洲央行。這一段不是只看 BOJ 會不會動，而是看匯率順風能不能真的留下來：韓國 CPI 3.1%、核心 2.5%、JPY 159.70 與印度財政 / 油價壓力，會一起決定亞洲前端能不能鬆。若 BOJ 6/16 低於市場期待且日圓再測 160，亞洲 FX 壓力就可能抵銷美元資產的利率收益；到那一步，前一段看到的 NAV 改善也不能直接當成可留存報酬。

Investment Read

Rates Close

1. UST Close and Curve Moves

6/1 was not duration relief, but a front-end-led bear flattening driven by the oil shock.

Official Treasury CMT data show that the 6/1 UST curve was led by a rise in the front end: 2Y closed at 4.05%, up 7bp on the day; 5Y closed at 4.18%, up 5bp; 10Y closed at 4.47%, up 2bp; 20Y closed at 4.99%, up 1bp; and 30Y closed unchanged at 4.99%. These closing levels tightened 2s10s by 5bp to 42bp and 5s30s by 5bp to 81bp. The curve shape was bear flattening, not a clean bull flattening.

The weekly tape still retains some relief: 2Y is 8bp below 5/22, 5Y is 9bp lower, 10Y is 9bp lower, and 30Y is 8bp lower. But the monthly tape has shifted back into pressure: 2Y is 17bp above 5/1, 5Y is 16bp higher, and 10Y is 8bp higher. This shows the market is not only trading one day of oil prices, but is also putting the growth resilience, AI capex, and energy risk seen since May back into a higher-for-longer framework.

The intraday transmission was sharper than the official close. After oil jumped, market materials show 10Y at one point approaching above 4.5%, while 2Y was pushed toward the 4.08% area. But the CMT close settled at 10Y 4.47% and 2Y 4.05%, indicating no disorderly late-day selling. The portfolio implication of this price action is that front-end policy risk has been reawakened, while the long end is holding near a 5% term-premium cap.

The main driver was energy and geopolitics. WTI at one point jumped 6.5% intraday to USD 93 and closed around USD 92.35; Iran stopped communicating through intermediaries and Hormuz risk reheated. The market therefore translated the move higher in oil directly into a broader inflation premium, Fed hike optionality, and USD haven demand. This is not merely a commodity risk, but a shock that enters rates pricing through gasoline, transportation, supplier deliveries, and inflation expectations.

Growth data did not provide rally protection. The ISM manufacturing PMI rose to 54.0, a four-year high, from 52.7 in April. Prices paid fell from 84.6 to 82.1 and came in below the 85.0 estimate, but it remains a very high reading of price pressure. For the curve, a strong PMI means the growth-scare bid is not in place, while elevated prices paid mean the front end still has to respect inflation risk. Together, those dynamics explain why 2Y rose more than 10Y.

Cross-asset confirmation also pointed toward bear flattening. The S&P 500 closed at 7,599.96, up 0.26% on the day, and the Nasdaq closed at 27,086.81, up 0.42%, with AI leadership keeping equity indices elevated. WTI rose about 4.96% and gold fell about 1.41%, showing that the market was trading oil prices and real rates rather than a broad risk-off move. When equity beta has not broken, credit technicals remain strong, and the USD stays firm, it is hard for the long end of USTs to receive a full recession-hedge bid.

Rates Interpretation

2. Interpreting the UST Move

The front end repriced Fed policy optionality, while the long end stayed near the 5% threshold without turning genuinely bullish.

This move should be read as a front-end repricing driven by the oil shock. 2Y rose 7bp on the day, 10Y rose 2bp, and 30Y was unchanged, showing that the market first processed the Fed reaction function rather than directly amplifying term premium. If investors treat the lack of a parallel rise in 30Y as a safety signal for the long end, they would miss that 30Y still closed at 4.99%, very close to the 5.00%-5.10% pressure zone.

The shift in Fed pricing is quite clear. The rates swaps market has fully priced a hike by March 2027, assigns roughly a half probability to an October hike, and implies about 20bp of hike magnitude around the December FOMC date. Viewed alongside WTI 92.35, ISM 54.0, and the ISM prices sub-index at 82.1, this pricing shows that the market has again acknowledged that the Fed still has policy optionality under an energy shock and resilient growth.

Inflation persistence is not only coming from oil prices themselves. Although the ISM prices sub-index at 82.1 was below expectations, supplier deliveries, front-loaded orders, transportation costs, and fuel prices all point to ongoing corporate cost pressure. Korea CPI at 3.1%, above the 2.9% estimate, with core at 2.5%, also shows that Asia price pressure is not just an energy headline story. This will make it harder for the Fed, BOK, and ECB to turn dovish in June on a single soft data point.

USD strength is the second transmission channel. DXY closed at 99.20, up 0.18% on the day; the euro was around 1.1615; and the yen was close to 159.70. The USD is being supported simultaneously by haven demand, the AI growth narrative, and higher-for-longer pricing. For USTs, a stronger USD usually lowers imported inflation, but in this round the more important point is that it squeezes non-US central banks and EM balance sheets, adding to global front-end policy premium.

The long-end signal is more mixed. 30Y was unchanged at 4.99%, and 10s30s tightened to 52bp, making it appear on the surface that the long end was not scared by oil. But both 20Y and 30Y are sitting near 5%, while global central banks and fiscal supply have not turned supportive. This means portfolios should not treat curve tightening as a long-end rally signal, but rather as a state in which the front end is repricing while the long end is still waiting for auction demand and real-money absorption.

Cross-Market Read

3. Other Market Moves

Credit-market technicals are very strong, but the macro backdrop has shifted from rates-friendly to issuer-selection-friendly.

ECB signals leaned hawkish. Schnabel described the inflation shock from the Iran conflict as a risk that could de-anchor inflation expectations, while the ECB consumer survey showed three-year inflation expectations easing only slightly from 3.0% to 2.9%, one-year expectations still at 4%, and five-year expectations at 2.4%. These figures remain above the 2% target. If euro-area CPI moves toward 3.2%, the European front end will limit any global duration rebound.

The UK and European fiscal backdrop is also not easy. UK house prices posted their largest decline in nearly a year, showing that mortgage-rate transmission is weighing on housing. At the same time, greater UK tax-system complexity and a higher tax burden are unfavorable for the medium-term growth and fiscal mix. For gilts and the long end of USTs, this is a combination of growth drag and fiscal premium, not a one-way positive.

Asia rates pressure is more direct. Korea CPI at 3.1% exceeded estimates, with transportation costs up 11.6% YoY, recreation and culture up 5%, food and lodging up 2.7%, and household goods and services up 2.6%. At the previous BOK meeting, two board members had already argued for a hike. The Korean government has stepped up communication with the bond market and cut its June issuance plan, showing that officials are using supply management to contain disorderly yield moves.

Japan and India offer another combination of pressure. Japanese corporate profits remain stable, but capex unexpectedly weakened, leaving the BOJ to balance inflation, investment, and FX pressure near yen 159.70 at its 6/16 decision. India is simultaneously facing risks from oil, the rupee, subsidies, and the fiscal deficit. Even if most of the market still expects the RBI to stay on hold in the near term, the sovereign curve cannot fully follow USTs lower.

AI infrastructure is the hottest financing theme in credit markets. A CoreWeave-related Chicago data center is planning to issue USD 850mn of high-yield bonds, backed by roughly USD 2.2bn of revenue from a 15-year lease. AI-related risk debt has already approached USD 27bn this year, and a Goldman conference also discussed more than USD 20bn of AI-related HY supply over the past two months. This supports credit demand, but it also brings energy, semiconductor, and labor-cost inflation back into macro pricing.

Leveraged-credit technicals remain strong. Citadel Securities plans to amend and extend a USD 3.95bn 2031 loan into USD 4.25bn maturing in 2033, with initial pricing around benchmark +200bp at 99.5-99.75. European borrowers are trying to cut spreads on euro term loans by 25-50bp, because new supply is limited and European loan issuance has been about USD 124.8bn year to date, down 8% YoY. This means lenders are being forced to accept thinner spread compensation, making quality screening more important than beta exposure.

Stress is concentrated in private credit, restructurings, and sovereign terms. FS KKR plans to issue at least USD 400mn of high-yield bonds, with yield talk in the mid-to-high 7% area, against a backdrop in which Moody's and Fitch have downgraded it to high-yield status and KKR has also provided USD 300mn of preferred-equity capital support. In Atalian's roughly EUR 919mn of 2028 bonds, holders of about 60% of the bonds will take control and convert about two-thirds of liabilities into equity. Zambia's USD 1.36bn buyback of its 2053 bonds has triggered creditor resistance because of the clean-up call provision and a coupon step-up that could rise from 0.5% to 7.5%.

Allocation Read

4. Allocation Implications

Reduce directional duration first, keep room to buy the belly on yield backups, and buy only high-quality income in credit.

Maintain a neutral stance on duration and do not chase the long end for now. The official 10Y close at 4.47% is still between 4.45% and 4.55%. If 10Y closes through 4.55% or 2Y holds above 4.15%, the oil shock will have shifted from headline risk into Fed pricing. Until then, 5Y-10Y can retain flexibility to buy on yield backups, but 20Y+ should not be treated as the primary hedge.

Curve positioning needs to acknowledge bearish flattening. 2s10s has tightened to 42bp. If it breaks below 40bp, front-end policy premium will hurt portfolios faster than long-end term premium. If it returns above 45bp and 10Y has not broken 4.55%, only then would that indicate an easing of bear-flattening pressure. In the near term, it is more suitable to use belly yield and roll-down carry than to add material long-end duration risk.

Credit / ETF exposure should focus on high-quality income. AI data centers, Citadel loans, and European loan repricing all show that market capital is still willing to buy spread, but FS KKR, Atalian, and Zambia show that weak balance sheets and documentation risk are being repriced. For high-yield or private-credit-linked exposure, prioritize sponsor support, asset coverage, maturity-extension terms, and the seniority of new money.

1. UST Close and Curve Moves6/1 was not duration relief, but a front-end-led bear flattening driven by the oil shock.

Official Treasury CMT data show that the 6/1 UST curve was led by a rise in the front end: 2Y closed at 4.05%, up 7bp on the day; 5Y closed at 4.18%, up 5bp; 10Y closed at 4.47%, up 2bp; 20Y closed at 4.99%, up 1bp; and 30Y closed unchanged at 4.99%. These closing levels tightened 2s10s by 5bp to 42bp and 5s30s by 5bp to 81bp. The curve shape was bear flattening, not a clean bull flattening.

The weekly tape still retains some relief: 2Y is 8bp below 5/22, 5Y is 9bp lower, 10Y is 9bp lower, and 30Y is 8bp lower. But the monthly tape has shifted back into pressure: 2Y is 17bp above 5/1, 5Y is 16bp higher, and 10Y is 8bp higher. This shows the market is not only trading one day of oil prices, but is also putting the growth resilience, AI capex, and energy risk seen since May back into a higher-for-longer framework.

The intraday transmission was sharper than the official close. After oil jumped, market materials show 10Y at one point approaching above 4.5%, while 2Y was pushed toward the 4.08% area. But the CMT close settled at 10Y 4.47% and 2Y 4.05%, indicating no disorderly late-day selling. The portfolio implication of this price action is that front-end policy risk has been reawakened, while the long end is holding near a 5% term-premium cap.

The main driver was energy and geopolitics. WTI at one point jumped 6.5% intraday to USD 93 and closed around USD 92.35; Iran stopped communicating through intermediaries and Hormuz risk reheated. The market therefore translated the move higher in oil directly into a broader inflation premium, Fed hike optionality, and USD haven demand. This is not merely a commodity risk, but a shock that enters rates pricing through gasoline, transportation, supplier deliveries, and inflation expectations.

Growth data did not provide rally protection. The ISM manufacturing PMI rose to 54.0, a four-year high, from 52.7 in April. Prices paid fell from 84.6 to 82.1 and came in below the 85.0 estimate, but it remains a very high reading of price pressure. For the curve, a strong PMI means the growth-scare bid is not in place, while elevated prices paid mean the front end still has to respect inflation risk. Together, those dynamics explain why 2Y rose more than 10Y.

Cross-asset confirmation also pointed toward bear flattening. The S&P 500 closed at 7,599.96, up 0.26% on the day, and the Nasdaq closed at 27,086.81, up 0.42%, with AI leadership keeping equity indices elevated. WTI rose about 4.96% and gold fell about 1.41%, showing that the market was trading oil prices and real rates rather than a broad risk-off move. When equity beta has not broken, credit technicals remain strong, and the USD stays firm, it is hard for the long end of USTs to receive a full recession-hedge bid.

2. Interpreting the UST MoveThe front end repriced Fed policy optionality, while the long end stayed near the 5% threshold without turning genuinely bullish.

This move should be read as a front-end repricing driven by the oil shock. 2Y rose 7bp on the day, 10Y rose 2bp, and 30Y was unchanged, showing that the market first processed the Fed reaction function rather than directly amplifying term premium. If investors treat the lack of a parallel rise in 30Y as a safety signal for the long end, they would miss that 30Y still closed at 4.99%, very close to the 5.00%-5.10% pressure zone.

The shift in Fed pricing is quite clear. The rates swaps market has fully priced a hike by March 2027, assigns roughly a half probability to an October hike, and implies about 20bp of hike magnitude around the December FOMC date. Viewed alongside WTI 92.35, ISM 54.0, and the ISM prices sub-index at 82.1, this pricing shows that the market has again acknowledged that the Fed still has policy optionality under an energy shock and resilient growth.

Inflation persistence is not only coming from oil prices themselves. Although the ISM prices sub-index at 82.1 was below expectations, supplier deliveries, front-loaded orders, transportation costs, and fuel prices all point to ongoing corporate cost pressure. Korea CPI at 3.1%, above the 2.9% estimate, with core at 2.5%, also shows that Asia price pressure is not just an energy headline story. This will make it harder for the Fed, BOK, and ECB to turn dovish in June on a single soft data point.

USD strength is the second transmission channel. DXY closed at 99.20, up 0.18% on the day; the euro was around 1.1615; and the yen was close to 159.70. The USD is being supported simultaneously by haven demand, the AI growth narrative, and higher-for-longer pricing. For USTs, a stronger USD usually lowers imported inflation, but in this round the more important point is that it squeezes non-US central banks and EM balance sheets, adding to global front-end policy premium.

The long-end signal is more mixed. 30Y was unchanged at 4.99%, and 10s30s tightened to 52bp, making it appear on the surface that the long end was not scared by oil. But both 20Y and 30Y are sitting near 5%, while global central banks and fiscal supply have not turned supportive. This means portfolios should not treat curve tightening as a long-end rally signal, but rather as a state in which the front end is repricing while the long end is still waiting for auction demand and real-money absorption.

3. Other Market MovesCredit-market technicals are very strong, but the macro backdrop has shifted from rates-friendly to issuer-selection-friendly.

ECB signals leaned hawkish. Schnabel described the inflation shock from the Iran conflict as a risk that could de-anchor inflation expectations, while the ECB consumer survey showed three-year inflation expectations easing only slightly from 3.0% to 2.9%, one-year expectations still at 4%, and five-year expectations at 2.4%. These figures remain above the 2% target. If euro-area CPI moves toward 3.2%, the European front end will limit any global duration rebound.

The UK and European fiscal backdrop is also not easy. UK house prices posted their largest decline in nearly a year, showing that mortgage-rate transmission is weighing on housing. At the same time, greater UK tax-system complexity and a higher tax burden are unfavorable for the medium-term growth and fiscal mix. For gilts and the long end of USTs, this is a combination of growth drag and fiscal premium, not a one-way positive.

Asia rates pressure is more direct. Korea CPI at 3.1% exceeded estimates, with transportation costs up 11.6% YoY, recreation and culture up 5%, food and lodging up 2.7%, and household goods and services up 2.6%. At the previous BOK meeting, two board members had already argued for a hike. The Korean government has stepped up communication with the bond market and cut its June issuance plan, showing that officials are using supply management to contain disorderly yield moves.

Japan and India offer another combination of pressure. Japanese corporate profits remain stable, but capex unexpectedly weakened, leaving the BOJ to balance inflation, investment, and FX pressure near yen 159.70 at its 6/16 decision. India is simultaneously facing risks from oil, the rupee, subsidies, and the fiscal deficit. Even if most of the market still expects the RBI to stay on hold in the near term, the sovereign curve cannot fully follow USTs lower.

AI infrastructure is the hottest financing theme in credit markets. A CoreWeave-related Chicago data center is planning to issue USD 850mn of high-yield bonds, backed by roughly USD 2.2bn of revenue from a 15-year lease. AI-related risk debt has already approached USD 27bn this year, and a Goldman conference also discussed more than USD 20bn of AI-related HY supply over the past two months. This supports credit demand, but it also brings energy, semiconductor, and labor-cost inflation back into macro pricing.

Leveraged-credit technicals remain strong. Citadel Securities plans to amend and extend a USD 3.95bn 2031 loan into USD 4.25bn maturing in 2033, with initial pricing around benchmark +200bp at 99.5-99.75. European borrowers are trying to cut spreads on euro term loans by 25-50bp, because new supply is limited and European loan issuance has been about USD 124.8bn year to date, down 8% YoY. This means lenders are being forced to accept thinner spread compensation, making quality screening more important than beta exposure.

Stress is concentrated in private credit, restructurings, and sovereign terms. FS KKR plans to issue at least USD 400mn of high-yield bonds, with yield talk in the mid-to-high 7% area, against a backdrop in which Moody's and Fitch have downgraded it to high-yield status and KKR has also provided USD 300mn of preferred-equity capital support. In Atalian's roughly EUR 919mn of 2028 bonds, holders of about 60% of the bonds will take control and convert about two-thirds of liabilities into equity. Zambia's USD 1.36bn buyback of its 2053 bonds has triggered creditor resistance because of the clean-up call provision and a coupon step-up that could rise from 0.5% to 7.5%.
