投資解讀

配置重點是把 UST 中段修復、高品質信用收益、低品質壓力分開處理。

盤面定義

一、UST 收盤與 Curve 變化

官方收盤顯示長端小幅修復，前端因勞動數據仍缺乏明確下行空間。

6/2 的盤面定義是溫和多頭壓平，而不是完整 Duration 反彈。官方 UST 收盤顯示 2Y 收 4.05% 持平，5Y 收 4.17% 下行 1bp，10Y 收 4.46% 下行 1bp，20Y 與 30Y 同步下行 2bp 至 4.97%。Curve Spreads也小幅收斂，2s10s 降至 41bp，5s30s 降至 80bp，10s30s 降至 51bp，反映油價回落給長端喘息，但前端沒有跟著釋放降息空間。

週線與月線訊號仍偏緊。2Y 比 5/26 高 4bp、比 5/1 高 17bp，5Y 比月初高 15bp，10Y 比月初高 7bp；30Y 雖然回到 4.97%、月線持平，但沒有離開 5% 附近太遠。這組數字說明五月以來的高利率維持更久壓力還在，只是 6/2 因油價略降與風險資產吸收能力較好，沒有延伸成新的長端賣壓。

JOLTS 是前端的核心障礙。4 月開缺升至 7.6mn，高於 6.8mn 預估，也高於 3 月約 6.9mn，並且是近兩年高位；裁員下降但離職也下降，顯示企業保留勞動力、員工流動意願不高。對 Fed 來說，這不是能快速支持降息的勞動市場，反而讓 2Y 即使沒有升高，也很難大幅回落。

油價是長端修復的必要條件，但不是充分條件。WTI 收在 93.51 美元、日升 1.46%，盤中曾因外交訊號改善而從高位回落，Brent 也從接近 94 美元上方波動。這讓 10Y 和 30Y 能小幅下行，但只要 WTI 還在 90 / 95 區間，市場仍會保留能源對 headline inflation 與企業成本的風險溢價。

跨資產盤面不支持衰退避險。S&P 500 收 7,609.78、再創高，Dow 收 51,307.79、日升 0.4%，Nasdaq 收 27,093.90、微升 0.03%；Marvell、HPE 與大型 AI 資本支出故事繼續支撐股市。當 equity 與 IG primary 都能吸收壓力，美債長端下行更多是油價與定位修復，而不是成長衰退買盤。

供給與一級市場仍是長端約束。美國 IG bond sales 年內突破 1 兆美元，速度是 2020 以來最快，並且與 AI 資本支出與藍籌融資需求直接相關。這種供給可以被市場吸收，是風險資產健康的訊號；但對長端 UST 來說，也代表 private sector Duration demand 與 corporate supply 仍會在 4.90%-5.00% 附近牽制 30Y。

亞洲時段也沒有提供單向利多。Japan 10Y JGB auction bid-to-cover 3.53，高於 12 個月平均 3.35，帶動 10Y JGB 殖利率降 11bp 至 2.57%，這對全球長端有短線穩定效果；但 OIS 仍顯示 BOJ 本月升息機率約 76%，加上 JPY 接近 160，這不是可以外推成全球央行轉鴿的訊號。

定價邏輯

二、美債走勢解讀

市場在勞動強、油價稍緩、AI 風險偏好多三條線之間重新校準 Fed 路徑。

定價邏輯的第一層是 Fed 反應函數。JOLTS 高於預估、裁員下降，與 Cleveland Fed President Beth Hammack 的表態相互呼應；她認為在不確定性下暫時按兵不動合理，但若通膨趨勢延續，政策可能需要很快採取行動。這讓 2Y 即使收盤持平，也保留向 4.15% 測試的風險。

第二層是通膨成分仍偏黏。Eurozone headline CPI 升至 3.2%，符合預估但高於 3.0% 前值；核心 CPI 升至 2.5%，高於 2.4% 預估和 2.2% 前值。加上 Poland CPI 雖低於預期但仍在 3.1%，以及 Asia central banks 面對 AI demand 與 oil imports 的雙重壓力，全球前端很難把油價單日回落解讀成通膨風險消失。

第三層是市場區分周期性賣壓與結構性供給。Pimco 的觀點把近期 UST selloff 主要歸因於 Fed bets，而不是 AI borrowing 本身；這個區分很重要，因為它說明當前利率壓力仍可由油價、勞動與央行語言逆轉，而非已進入長年期 corporate Duration supply 無法吸收的階段。對配置而言，這保留了逢殖利率上行買 5Y-10Y 的空間。

第四層是期限溢價沒有全面擴大。30Y 下行 2bp 至 4.97%，週線下行 6bp，月線持平，代表長端投資人願意在 5% 附近承接，但沒有足夠理由把 30Y 快速壓回 4.85% 以下。若未來非農就業強、IG supply 繼續放量，30Y 在 4.95%-5.05% 之間可能維持區間交易，而不是展開方向性反彈。

第五層是定位尚未完全轉向多頭。市場材料顯示債券空頭雖然修剪了極端部位，但仍在等待 Friday 非農就業測試；10Y 從兩週前接近 4.69% 的高點回落至 4.5% 下方，但仍高於年內較低區域。這代表 NFP 若偏弱可以觸發 short-covering，但若偏強，空方會更有理由重新壓迫 Duration。

第六層是貨幣與黃金沒有確認避險。DXY 收 99.17、日升 0.02%，黃金約 4,539.50 美元、日跌 1.2%，說明美元和實質利率因素壓過避險需求。若市場真正進入成長恐慌，黃金與長端通常會同步受益；6/2 的組合更像高利率韌性環境下的風險資產擴張。

這些訊號合在一起，對 UST 的判讀是：短線可以承認長端修復，但不應把它定義為降息交易重啟。Curve 收斂來自 10Y / 30Y 小幅下行，而不是 2Y 大幅下行；也就是說，市場暫時接受油價壓力緩和，卻還沒有接受 Fed 即將放鬆。

跨市驗證

三、其他市場動向

跨市驗證顯示資金仍充足，但政策與信用壓力正在把曝險切成不同層級。

AI 仍是風險資產的主導敘事。S&P 500 站上 7,600 並錄得九日連漲，是 2025 年 5 月以來最長漲勢；chipmakers 指標接近上升 6%，Marvell 因 Nvidia CEO 提及兆美元潛力而大漲 33%，Hewlett Packard Enterprise 也因 AI 銷售預期上修而受追捧。這種集中領漲延續了 equity wealth effect，也讓 Fed 不容易把金融條件視為已經收緊。

Alphabet 的 800 億美元 equity offering 與 Anthropic 潛在 IPO 讓 AI 資本支出與資本市場供給進一步連結。風險資產接受這些融資，代表市場仍願意把 AI 投資視為盈利與 productivity 故事；但對 FI 來說，電力、晶片、資料中心與勞動需求都可能轉成成本壓力，這正是央行不願快速轉鴿的原因。

信用一級市場的強勢非常明顯。QXO Building Products 為收購 TopBuild 發行 30 億美元高收益債，訂單超過 100 億美元，整體 60 億美元 debt package 的貸款 tranche 也已滿額；CoreWeave 相關資料中心五年期 9 億美元 HY 債券以 par、7.5% 殖利率成交，規模較原計畫增加 5,000 萬美元。這些交易說明有明確資產、AI 主題或收購邏輯的 credit 仍可取得深度需求。

同一時間，藍籌與私募信貸平台也在打開市場。US IG sales 年內破 1 兆美元，是 2020 以來最快；Blue Owl Technology Finance 把原定 3 億美元 investment-grade bond sale 擴大至 5 億美元，定價從初始約 UST +300bp 收斂至 +265bp。這代表殖利率 demand 仍足，但買方要求可理解的資產、平台與用途。

低品質端則是另一個世界。Merlin Entertainments 2027 年 11 月有超過 7.2 億歐元 bond maturities，S&P 去年已降至 CCC+，旅遊需求受 Iran conflict 與消費謹慎影響，市場開始把它從波動信用看成 refinancing access problem。這類個案提醒投資人，風險資產創高不代表所有 HY 借款人都能順利展期。

特殊情況信用更分化。Braskem 正尋求債權人支持以啟動巴西法外重整，需要三分之一債務持有人支持才能取得 90 天 standstill；Atalian、Merlin、FS KKR 這類案例雖屬不同區域與行業，但共同點是高融資成本正在迫使弱資產負債表提前處理到期壓力。FI 配置不能只看 market 曝險，還要看債權人協調能力與新資金順位。

EM 主權債風險也更事件化。Senegal 2031 bond 約在 54 cents，低於 2 月中的 68 cents，主權利差超過 UST 1,500bp；Venezuela 聘 Hogan Lovells 處理約 1,700 億美元 defaulted debt 的重整準備；South Africa 則相反，primary budget surplus 達 GDP 1.1%，優於 0.9% 預估，且 main deficit 4.3% 低於原估 4.6%。這說明 EM 不再是單一收益 trade，而是政治、法律與財政可信度的分層。

央行操作面亦有分化。PBOC 只做 2 億人民幣 7-day reverse repo，扣除到期後淨回收 2,488 億人民幣，三個月累計回收中長期流動性約 1.75 兆人民幣；這不是經典緊縮，而是防止中國債券殖利率因信用需求弱而下得太快。相對地，Poland 在 CPI 低於預估後按兵不動，Japan 借順利拍賣穩住 JGB，說明各國央行都在用局部工具管理市場功能。

UK gilt 市場則把政治、財政與高殖利率價值放在一起交易。Insight Investment 因 longer-dated bond forwards 在 6% 或以上而買入 gilts，認為英國比歐洲更有相對價值；但 BOE 內部如 Megan Greene 的語言偏向若伊朗戰事延續可能需升息，Burnham 選舉期間與 hedge fund 對 fiscal stance 的互動也顯示政治供給風險仍被市場放大。

Muni market 提供另一條殖利率 demand 線索。California Community Choice Financing Authority 批准最高 12.5 億美元 prepaid-energy bonds，受益方是 Pioneer Community Energy，Goldman Sachs 擬承銷；該類債今年已發行約 146 億美元，較 2025 同期增加 94%。在稅免市場，投資人也在用結構與能源 cash flow 換取較高收益，這與 IG / HY 一級市場的需求是一致的。

配置動作

四、配置含義

配置重點是把 UST 中段修復、高品質信用收益、低品質壓力分開處理。

Duration 的操作應偏中性偏多，但加碼需要等待觸發。10Y 收 4.46%，位於 4.45% 支撐與 4.55% 壓力之間；2Y 收 4.05%，JOLTS 後沒有下行。若非農就業前 10Y 再上到 4.50%-4.55%，但油價不破 95、2Y 不破 4.15%，可以分批加 5Y-10Y；若 2Y 先破 4.15%，表示市場在交易 Fed 再加息風險，應先降低 Duration。

Curve 部位偏好中段，不追超長端。2s10s 41bp、5s30s 80bp、10s30s 51bp 都小幅收斂，但 30Y 只是從 4.99% 到 4.97%，仍貼近 5% 門檻。台幣海外債 ETF 若持有長天期 UST，短線可受益於長端修復；但若 30Y 無法連續收在 4.95% 下方，超長端的 convexity payoff 不夠清楚。

Credit / ETF 以高品質收益為主，而不是全面追曝險。IG supply 破 1 兆美元、Blue Owl IG pricing 收斂、QXO 和 CoreWeave 相關 HY 成交強，代表市場願意付錢買清楚題材與可解釋現金流。對 ETF 來說，IG、金融次順位中品質較高的品種、以及中短 Duration credit 更適合保留；低評級旅遊、弱資產負債表私募信貸、與缺乏清晰債權人協調的 EM 主權債應降權。

EM 配置應從收益轉向事件篩選。South Africa 的 primary surplus beat 支持財政可信度，Argentina、Venezuela 這類政治關係改善的 sovereign 可能有獨立驅動；但 Senegal 54 cents 與 1,500bp 利差顯示政治裂痕和 IMF 談判可以把債券快速推進 recovery-pricing。若要持有 EM 硬通貨，應優先選財政路徑可驗證、法律事件透明、且美元流動性充足的 sovereign。

Asia rates 不宜單純跟隨 UST 反彈。Japan auction 回落、PBOC draining、Poland hold、Korea 與 Asia central banks 偏鷹的背景，說明本地央行都在各自處理能源輸入、匯率與市場功能。台幣投資人看海外債 ETF 時，不能只看 UST table；也要看 JPY 160 風險、人民幣債券反彈是否被壓制，以及油價是否讓亞洲央行延後寬鬆。

FX / NAV 方面，DXY 99.17 幾乎持平，USD/TWD 的方向不應成為主要加碼理由。若美元走強是因 AI growth 與 Fed 高利率維持更久，台幣投資人的匯率助益可能同時伴隨利率損失；更乾淨的 NAV 改善需要美元支撐、UST 反彈與利差穩定同時成立。若美元走弱，則需要更強的 UST 價格修復來抵銷 FX cushion 減弱。現階段應把 FX contribution 當避險緩衝，而不是 alpha 核心。

加碼條件要更嚴格。第一，WTI 需要跌破 90 美元或至少連續無法站上 95；第二，10Y 需重新低於 4.45% 且 2Y 不升破 4.15%；第三，非農就業或薪資數據至少不能延續 JOLTS 的強勢。三個條件若缺兩個以上，Duration 與信用曝險都應保持較小部位。

6/2 的配置重點，是把 JOLTS 後的前端壓力和長端小幅修復分開。若只看 10Y / 30Y 下行，容易以為 Duration 已重新打開；但 2Y 幾乎不動，代表 Fed 利率路徑尚未鬆動。台灣債券 ETF 因此適合保留中段與高品質收益，而不是把長端、HY 和 EM 同時加回。這種分段處理能避免在勞動數據仍偏強時過早放大 total-return 波動。

交易觸發

五、後續觀察重點

交易觸發集中在非農就業、WTI 90 / 95、10Y 4.45 / 4.55、以及一級市場品質。

第一個觸發是 Friday 非農就業。JOLTS 已經把市場推向勞動韌性的解讀，若非農就業與薪資同步偏強，2Y 會先測 4.15%，Fed swaps 也可能重新把 2026-2027 升息概率往上推；若就業低於預估且薪資放緩，10Y 低於 4.45% 的機率升高，中段 UST short-covering 會更乾淨。

第二個觸發是 WTI 的 90 / 95 美元區間。低於 90 表示外交進展與供給風險下降足以壓低整體通膨溢價，這會讓 10Y 和 30Y 有理由延續反彈；高於 95 則會把 oil passthrough、運輸成本與央行語言重新連在一起，2Y 與 5Y 會比 30Y 更快承壓。

第三個觸發是 10Y 4.45% / 4.55%。收低於 4.45% 且 S&P 500 不跌，代表市場相信 oil 回落與高品質風險偏好可以共存；收高於 4.55%，則代表 JOLTS、非農就業或 Fed officials 讓前端壓力外溢至長端，台幣海外債 ETF 應降低長天期曝險。

第四個觸發是一級市場品質。若 IG supply 繼續被吸收、QXO / CoreWeave 類交易沒有放大利差讓步，credit 收益可以維持；若 Merlin、Braskem、Senegal 類壓力事件開始影響 broader HY 利差s，表示市場不再只篩選個案，而是在重新定價低品質融資窗口。

第五個觸發是全球央行語言。Hammack、Greene、ECB inflation print、BOJ 6 月會議與 Asia central banks 偏鷹訊號若集中出現，將提高前端殖利率黏性；若政策官員開始強調油價回落與成長放緩，才有利 UST Curve 從溫和修復轉為更明確的 Duration 反彈。

第六個觸發是 EM 政治與法律流程。Venezuela 若正式推進 1,700 億美元債務重整、Senegal 若 IMF talks 仍受政治裂痕拖累、Chile 額外 62 億美元發債若遭國會阻力，EM 利差會更分化。相反，South Africa 的財政 surplus story 若獲得延續，會成為高品質 EM 收益的相對支撐。

USD/TWD 的官方 onshore close 補上後，6/2 的 FX 訊號也更清楚：台幣較前一交易日走弱，對未避險 TWD 計價美元債 ETF 有換算助益；但這不等於可以忽略 JOLTS、油價與信用供給。若非農就業或 wages 讓 2Y 回到 4.15% 上方，FX 順風可能只能抵銷一部分利率損失；若 10Y 同時跌破 4.45%，NAV 改善才比較可能是利率與 FX 同向。

Rates Close

I. UST Close and Curve Moves

UST long-end repair was modest, while the front end still lacks downside room because of labor data.

The 6/2 market tone was mild bull flattening, not a full Duration rally. Treasury CMT showed 2Y closing unchanged at 4.05%, 5Y down 1bp to 4.17%, 10Y down 1bp to 4.46%, and both 20Y and 30Y down 2bp to 4.97%. Curve spreads also tightened modestly, with 2s10s down to 41bp, 5s30s down to 80bp, and 10s30s down to 51bp, reflecting that lower oil gave the long end breathing room, while the front end did not release rate-cut room alongside it.

Weekly and monthly signals still lean tight. 2Y is 4bp above 5/26 and 17bp above 5/1, 5Y is 15bp above the start of the month, and 10Y is 7bp above the start of the month; although 30Y has returned to 4.97% and is flat on the month, it has not moved far away from the 5% area. These numbers show that higher-for-longer pressure since May is still present, but on 6/2, slightly lower oil and better risk-asset absorption kept it from extending into fresh long-end selling pressure.

JOLTS is the core obstacle for the front end. April openings rose to 7.6mn, above the 6.8mn estimate and also above March's roughly 6.9mn, reaching a near two-year high; layoffs declined, but quits also fell, showing firms are retaining labor and employees have limited willingness to move. For the Fed, this is not a labor market that quickly supports rate cuts; instead, even if 2Y did not rise, it is hard for it to fall materially.

Oil is a necessary condition for long-end repair, but not a sufficient one. WTI closed at $93.51, up 1.46% on the day, after retreating from intraday highs on improved diplomatic signals, while Brent also fluctuated around levels near and above $94. That allowed 10Y and 30Y to move modestly lower, but as long as WTI remains in the 90 / 95 range, the market will retain a risk premium for energy's impact on headline inflation and corporate costs.

Cross-asset price action does not support a recession hedge. S&P 500 closed at 7,609.78, a fresh high; Dow closed at 51,307.79, up 0.4% on the day; and Nasdaq closed at 27,093.90, up a marginal 0.03%. Marvell, HPE, and large AI capex stories continued to support equities. When equities and the IG primary market can both absorb pressure, the move lower in the long end of Treasuries is more about oil and positioning repair than growth-slowdown buying.

Supply and primary markets remain constraints on the long end. US IG bond sales broke $1tn year-to-date, the fastest pace since 2020, and are directly tied to AI capex and blue-chip funding needs. The market's ability to absorb this supply is a healthy signal for risk assets; for long-end USTs, however, it also means private-sector duration demand and corporate supply will continue to pin 30Y around the 4.90%-5.00% area.

The Asia session also did not provide a one-way bullish signal. Japan's 10Y JGB auction bid-to-cover was 3.53, above the 12-month average of 3.35, helping push the 10Y JGB yield down 11bp to 2.57%, which had a short-term stabilizing effect on global long ends; however, OIS still shows BOJ hike probability of roughly 76% this month, and with JPY near 160, this is not a signal that can be extrapolated into a global central-bank dovish turn.

Rates Interpretation

II. Reading the UST Move

The market is recalibrating the Fed path across three lines: strong labor, slightly easier oil, and AI-led risk appetite.

The first layer of pricing logic is the Fed reaction function. Above-consensus JOLTS and declining layoffs echoed Cleveland Fed President Beth Hammack's comments; she said standing pat for now is reasonable under uncertainty, but if inflation trends persist, policy may need to act soon. That leaves 2Y with risk of testing 4.15%, even though it closed unchanged.

The second layer is that inflation components remain sticky. Eurozone headline CPI rose to 3.2%, in line with estimates but above the 3.0% prior; core CPI rose to 2.5%, above the 2.4% estimate and the 2.2% prior. Alongside Poland CPI, which was below expectations but still at 3.1%, and Asian central banks facing dual pressure from AI demand and oil imports, the global front end will struggle to interpret a one-day oil pullback as the disappearance of inflation risk.

The third layer is the market's distinction between cyclical selling pressure and structural supply. Pimco's view attributes the recent UST selloff mainly to Fed bets rather than AI borrowing itself; that distinction matters because it implies current rate pressure can still be reversed by oil, labor, and central-bank language, rather than having already moved into a phase where long-maturity corporate duration supply cannot be absorbed. For allocation, this preserves room to buy 5Y-10Y on yield back-ups.

The fourth layer is that term premium has not broadened across the board. 30Y fell 2bp to 4.97%, is down 6bp on the week, and is flat on the month, indicating that long-end investors are willing to add near 5%, but do not have enough reason to quickly push 30Y back below 4.85%. If future payrolls are strong and IG supply continues to rise, 30Y may remain range-bound between 4.95%-5.05% rather than begin a directional rally.

The fifth layer is that positioning has not fully flipped long. Market color suggests bond shorts have trimmed extreme positions but are still waiting for Friday payrolls as the test; 10Y has fallen back below 4.5% from highs near 4.69% two weeks ago, but remains above the lower area seen year-to-date. That means a soft NFP can trigger short covering, but if it is firm, shorts will have more reason to press Duration again.

The sixth layer is that currencies and gold did not confirm a haven bid. DXY closed at 99.17, up 0.02% on the day, while Gold was around $4,539.50, down 1.2%, showing dollar and real-rate factors outweighed haven demand. If the market were truly entering a growth scare, gold and the long end would usually benefit together; the 6/2 combination looks more like risk-asset expansion in a resilient high-rate environment.

Taken together, the read for USTs is this: the near-term long-end repair can be acknowledged, but it should not be defined as a restart of the rate-cut trade. The curve tightening came from modest downside in 10Y / 30Y, not a sharp fall in 2Y; in other words, the market temporarily accepted oil-pressure relief, but has not yet accepted that the Fed is about to ease.

Cross-Market Read

III. Other Market Moves

Cross-market validation shows liquidity remains ample, but policy and credit stress are splitting beta into different tiers.

AI remains the dominant risk-asset narrative. S&P 500 moved above 7,600 and posted a nine-day winning streak, its longest since May 2025; the chipmakers gauge rose nearly 6%, Marvell surged 33% after Nvidia's CEO mentioned trillion-dollar potential, and Hewlett Packard Enterprise was also sought after on an upgraded AI sales outlook. This concentrated leadership extends the equity wealth effect and makes it difficult for the Fed to view financial conditions as already tight.

Alphabet's $80bn equity offering and Anthropic's potential IPO further link AI capex with capital-market supply. Risk assets' acceptance of this financing indicates the market remains willing to treat AI investment as an earnings and productivity story; for FI, however, power, chips, data centers, and labor demand can all translate into cost pressure, which is exactly why central banks are reluctant to turn dovish quickly.

The strength of the credit primary market is very clear. QXO Building Products issued $3bn of junk bonds to fund its TopBuild acquisition, with orders exceeding $10bn, and the loan tranche of the broader $6bn debt package was also fully subscribed; CoreWeave-linked five-year, $900mn HY data-center notes priced at par with a 7.5% yield, $50mn larger than initially planned. These deals show that credit with clear assets, an AI theme, or acquisition logic can still tap deep demand.

At the same time, blue-chip issuers and private-credit platforms are also opening the market. US IG sales broke $1tn year-to-date, the fastest pace since 2020; Blue Owl Technology Finance upsized its planned $300mn investment-grade bond sale to $500mn, with pricing tightening from initial talk around UST +300bp to +265bp. This shows yield demand remains ample, but buyers require understandable assets, platforms, and uses of proceeds.

The low-quality end is a different world. Merlin Entertainments has more than EUR720mn of bond maturities in November 2027, S&P downgraded it to CCC+ last year, travel demand has been hit by the Iran conflict and consumer caution, and the market has begun to view it less as a volatile credit and more as a refinancing-access problem. Cases like this remind investors that risk-asset highs do not mean all HY borrowers can extend smoothly.

Special-situation credit is even more dispersed. Braskem is seeking creditor support to launch an out-of-court restructuring in Brazil and needs backing from one-third of debtholders to obtain a 90-day standstill; cases such as Atalian, Merlin, and FS KKR are in different regions and sectors, but their common point is that high financing costs are forcing weak balance sheets to deal with maturity pressure early. FI allocation cannot look only at market beta; it must also assess creditor coordination and new-money priority.

EM sovereign risk has also become more event-driven. Senegal's 2031 bond is around 54 cents, down from 68 cents in mid-February, with sovereign spreads more than 1,500bp over UST; Venezuela has hired Hogan Lovells to prepare restructuring work on roughly $170bn of defaulted debt; South Africa is the opposite case, with a primary budget surplus of 1.1% of GDP, above the 0.9% estimate, and a main deficit of 4.3%, below the prior 4.6% estimate. This shows EM is no longer a single carry trade, but a tiering of political, legal, and fiscal credibility.

Central-bank operations are also diverging. The PBOC conducted only CNY200mn of 7-day reverse repo operations and, after maturities, drained CNY248.8bn net; over three months, it has withdrawn roughly CNY1.75tn of medium- to long-term liquidity. This is not classic tightening, but an effort to prevent Chinese bond yields from falling too quickly because of weak credit demand. By contrast, Poland held rates after below-consensus CPI, while Japan stabilized JGBs through a smooth auction, showing central banks are using local tools to manage market functioning.

The UK gilt market is trading politics, fiscal risk, and high-yield value together. Insight Investment has been buying gilts because longer-dated bond forwards are at 6% or above, seeing better relative value in the UK than in Europe; however, language from BOE officials such as Megan Greene leans toward possible hikes if the Iran war continues, and the interaction between Burnham's election period and hedge-fund views on the fiscal stance also shows that political supply risk is still being amplified by markets.

The muni market offers another clue on yield demand. California Community Choice Financing Authority approved up to $1.25bn of prepaid-energy bonds, benefiting Pioneer Community Energy, with Goldman Sachs set to underwrite; this type of debt has seen roughly $14.6bn of issuance this year, up 94% from the same period in 2025. In the tax-exempt market, investors are also using structure and energy cash flow to obtain higher yield, consistent with demand in the IG / HY primary markets.

Allocation Read

IV. Allocation Implications

Separate UST belly duration, high-quality credit income, and low-quality stress into distinct allocation buckets.

Duration should be managed with a neutral-to-modestly-long bias, but additions need triggers. 10Y closed at 4.46%, between 4.45% support and 4.55% resistance; 2Y closed at 4.05% and did not fall after JOLTS. If 10Y backs up again to 4.50%-4.55% before payrolls, but oil does not break 95 and 2Y does not break 4.15%, 5Y-10Y can be added in tranches; if 2Y breaks 4.15% first, it means the market is trading renewed Fed hike risk, and Duration should be reduced first.

For curve positioning, prefer the belly and do not chase the ultra-long end. 2s10s at 41bp, 5s30s at 80bp, and 10s30s at 51bp all tightened modestly, but 30Y only moved from 4.99% to 4.97% and remains close to the 5% threshold. TWD offshore bond ETFs with long-dated UST holdings can benefit in the near term from long-end repair; however, if 30Y cannot close below 4.95% for consecutive sessions, the ultra-long convexity payoff is not sufficiently clear.

Credit / ETF allocation should prioritize high-quality income, not a broad beta chase. IG supply above $1tn, tighter Blue Owl IG pricing, and strong QXO and CoreWeave-linked HY deals show the market is willing to pay for clear themes and explainable cash flows. For ETFs, IG, higher-quality financial subordinated names, and short-to-intermediate-duration credit are more suitable to retain; low-rated travel credits, weak-balance-sheet private credit, and EM sovereigns without clear creditor coordination should be underweighted.

EM allocation should move from carry toward event selection. South Africa's primary-surplus beat supports fiscal credibility, while sovereigns such as Argentina and Venezuela with improving political relationships may have independent drivers; however, Senegal at 54 cents and a 1,500bp spread shows political fractures and IMF negotiations can quickly push bonds into recovery-pricing territory. For EM hard currency exposure, prioritize sovereigns with verifiable fiscal paths, transparent legal events, and sufficient dollar liquidity.

Asia rates should not simply follow the UST rally. Japan auction relief, PBOC draining, Poland's hold, and the hawkish backdrop among Korea and Asian central banks show local central banks are each dealing with energy imports, FX, and market functioning. For TWD investors assessing offshore bond ETFs, the UST table is not enough; they also need to monitor JPY 160 risk, whether the RMB bond rally is being restrained, and whether oil causes Asian central banks to delay easing.

On FX / NAV, DXY at 99.17 was almost unchanged, and USD/TWD direction should not be the main reason to add risk. If dollar strength is driven by AI growth and Fed higher-for-longer, FX gains for TWD investors may come alongside rates losses. A cleaner NAV improvement requires dollar support, a UST rally, and stable spreads together; if the dollar weakens, a stronger UST price repair is needed to offset the reduced FX cushion. At this stage, FX contribution should be treated as a hedge cushion rather than the core alpha source.

Addition criteria should be stricter. First, WTI needs to break below $90 or at least fail repeatedly to hold above 95; second, 10Y needs to move back below 4.45% while 2Y does not break above 4.15%; third, payrolls or wage data need at minimum not to extend the strength in JOLTS. If two or more of these three conditions are missing, both Duration and credit beta should remain in smaller positions.

The 6/2 allocation point is to separate post-JOLTS front-end pressure from modest long-end repair. Looking only at lower 10Y and 30Y yields can make Duration look reopened, but 2Y barely moved, so the Fed path had not eased. Taiwan bond ETFs should therefore keep belly duration and quality income rather than adding long-end, HY, and EM exposure at the same time. This segmented approach avoids expanding total-return volatility while labor data are still firm.

Watchlist

V. Follow-Up Watchlist

Trading triggers are concentrated in payrolls, WTI 90 / 95, 10Y 4.45 / 4.55, and primary-market quality.

The first trigger is Friday payrolls. JOLTS has already pushed the market toward a labor-resilience interpretation; if nonfarm payrolls and wages are both firm, 2Y will test 4.15% first, and Fed swaps may also reprice 2026-2027 hike probabilities higher. If employment is below expectations and wages slow, the probability of 10Y moving below 4.45% rises, and belly UST short covering would be cleaner.

The second trigger is WTI's $90 / $95 range. Below 90 would indicate that diplomatic progress and lower supply risks are enough to compress the headline inflation premium, giving 10Y and 30Y reason to extend the rally; above 95 would reconnect oil passthrough, transportation costs, and central-bank language, with 2Y and 5Y likely to come under pressure faster than 30Y.

The third trigger is 10Y 4.45% / 4.55%. A close below 4.45% while S&P 500 does not fall would mean the market believes oil relief and high-quality risk appetite can coexist; a close above 4.55% would mean JOLTS, payrolls, or Fed officials have allowed front-end pressure to spill into the long end, and TWD offshore bond ETFs should reduce long-duration exposure.

The fourth trigger is primary-market quality. If IG supply continues to be absorbed and QXO / CoreWeave-type trades do not require wider spread concessions, credit carry can be maintained; if stress events such as Merlin, Braskem, and Senegal begin to affect broader HY spreads, it would mean the market is no longer only screening single names, but repricing the low-quality financing window.

The fifth trigger is global central-bank language. If signals from Hammack, Greene, the ECB inflation print, the BOJ June meeting, and hawkish Asian central banks cluster together, front-end yield stickiness will rise; only if policymakers begin emphasizing lower oil and slowing growth would the UST curve be able to shift from mild repair to a more explicit Duration rally.

The sixth trigger is EM political and legal process. If Venezuela formally advances a $170bn debt restructuring, Senegal remains dragged down by political fractures in IMF talks, or Chile's additional $6.2bn of issuance faces congressional resistance, EM spreads will become more dispersed. Conversely, if South Africa's fiscal-surplus story continues, it will provide relative support for high-quality EM carry.

With the official USD/TWD onshore close added, the 6/2 FX signal is clearer: TWD weakened versus the prior trading day, helping unhedged TWD-denominated USD bond ETFs through translation. But that does not override JOLTS, oil, or credit supply. If payrolls or wages push 2Y back above 4.15%, the FX tailwind may only offset part of the rates loss. If 10Y also breaks below 4.45%, NAV improvement is more likely to come from rates and FX moving in the same direction.
