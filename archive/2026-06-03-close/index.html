投資解讀

一、UST 收盤與曲線變化

UST 是前端與中段共同上移、長端相對黏著的空頭轉平。

2s10s 維持 41bp、5s30s 收斂 2bp 至 78bp，代表市場把油價、服務業與就業韌性先灌進政策路徑，長端雖仍貼近 5%，但相對沒有再明顯失控。第1個檢查點要回到同一條投組鏈：6/3 的空頭轉平訊號對投組很直接：2Y/5Y 的壓力比 30Y 更敏感，說明市場正在收回降息保費；若台灣 ETF 部位偏長久期，短期價格風險會先從中段開始，再透過曲線壓平影響長天期總報酬。這樣可避免把同一個收盤變化誤讀成單一方向押注。

官方 CMT 的周變化也確認這不是單日雜訊：2Y 一周升 9bp、5Y 升 6bp、10Y 升 4bp，30Y 只升 1bp；月變化則是 2Y 與 5Y 各升 13bp，10Y 只升 4bp，30Y 反而低 3bp。這種結構代表市場正在把降息尾端拿掉，前端與中段久期的收益保護下降，曲線交易不宜只押長端單向走低。第2個檢查點要回到同一條投組鏈：6/3 的空頭轉平訊號對投組很直接：2Y/5Y 的壓力比 30Y 更敏感，說明市場正在收回降息保費；若台灣 ETF 部位偏長久期，短期價格風險會先從中段開始，再透過曲線壓平影響長天期總報酬。這樣可避免把同一個收盤變化誤讀成單一方向押注。

二、美債走勢解讀

定價邏輯從降息時程後移，轉成油價與就業共同維持 Fed 升息選擇權。

這輪上行的核心不是成長恐慌，而是政策反應函數被迫變寬。Fed swaps 把年底 25bp 升息機率推到約 85%，一周前約 60%；這種變化會直接壓縮 2Y 與 5Y 的降息保費，使曲線前端不再把風險資產小跌視為買債理由。只要市場相信 Fed 需要保留升息語言，2s10s 即使沒有再倒掛擴大，也很難快速 steepen。第1個檢查點要回到同一條投組鏈：Fed 官員的條件式語言讓市場重新重視升息選擇權，這不是單一官員發言，而是油價、服務業、就業與關稅成本共同提高反應函數的不確定性；因此 10Y 未破 4.55% 前仍不能視為利率壓力結束。這樣可避免把同一個收盤變化誤讀成單一方向押注。

Williams 表示政策位置目前合適、沒有明顯升降息方向，Logan 則提醒若通膨與需求不降溫，今年仍可能需要升息。這兩種訊號合在一起，等於把 Fed 從單一路徑改成條件式反應：油價、關稅與 AI 投資若使金融條件維持寬鬆，前端要付更高保險；若就業資料轉弱，長端才有足夠理由把 10Y 拉回 4.45% 下方。第2個檢查點要回到同一條投組鏈：Fed 官員的條件式語言讓市場重新重視升息選擇權，這不是單一官員發言，而是油價、服務業、就業與關稅成本共同提高反應函數的不確定性；因此 10Y 未破 4.55% 前仍不能視為利率壓力結束。這樣可避免把同一個收盤變化誤讀成單一方向押注。

Beige Book 的訊息支持這個條件式定價。12 個地區中有 10 個顯示活動小幅至溫和擴張，能源、運輸、包材、食品與肥料成本仍被企業提到，製造業招聘也受到國防與資料中心需求支撐。這讓服務業 54.5 的成長訊號更難被解讀成短暫反彈，因為成本傳導與投資需求仍在，使 Fed 不容易以一次弱就業分項就轉鴿。第3個檢查點要回到同一條投組鏈：Fed 官員的條件式語言讓市場重新重視升息選擇權，這不是單一官員發言，而是油價、服務業、就業與關稅成本共同提高反應函數的不確定性；因此 10Y 未破 4.55% 前仍不能視為利率壓力結束。這樣可避免把同一個收盤變化誤讀成單一方向押注。

三、其他市場動向

跨市驗證集中在央行、信用一級市場、私募信用與 EM 資金條件的分化。

英國與歐洲的訊號偏向限制全球久期多頭。OECD 預估 BOE 今年按兵不動、2027 年才降至 3.5%，同時把英國 CPI 看在 2026 年 3.7%、2027 年 2.4%，失業率 2026 年 5.5%、2027 年 5.3%，債務占 GDP 可能自 2023 年 98.8% 升至 2027 年 105.4%。這組數字意味 gilts 難以提供明確的全球領漲，UST 長端也少了一個外部多頭轉平錨。

歐中與美國關稅議題則透過商品、供應鏈與投資成本影響利率。歐盟準備對家庭與企業提示更強硬的中國貿易措施，美國也討論對多個貿易夥伴至少 10% 的進口關稅；這些不是單日 CPI 數字，卻會提高企業投入成本與庫存策略的不確定性。對債市而言，供應鏈再定價越像成本推動，前端越難因成長疑慮而快速轉鴿。

澳洲與印度顯示亞洲本地資金條件並不一致。澳洲 7Y A$1bn 標售 bid-to-cover 超過 4 倍，反映 RBA 暫停訊號與 Q1 GDP 只增 0.3% 支撐中段債需求；但印度銀行貸款年增 16.2%，地方債銷售年減 11% 至 10.9 兆盧比，主權殖利率升至 7.04%。這代表亞洲久期不是整體買盤，資金會在成長放緩市場與高油價壓力市場之間重新分配。

信用一級市場仍有窗口，但品質區分非常明顯。Alphabet 透過 California Community Choice Financing Authority 進行 10 億美元預付能源免稅債，Bahrain 10Y 美元債在逾 32 億美元訂單下定價 7.125%、較初始指引收窄 37.5bp；同時海灣主權收入受油運與安全風險牽動。這說明高品質、稅務效率或主權收益仍能吸引資金，但信用買盤要求更清楚的收益補償。

AI 與資料中心相關融資持續支撐投資需求，卻也加劇信用分化。美國企業股權與債務供給被市場吸收，資料中心投資在澳洲 GDP 中也有明顯貢獻；但如果 AI 資本支出讓金融條件保持寬鬆，Fed 的通膨與需求風險會更難解除。對 credit 與 rates 的交集來說，AI 相關高品質發行可承接，不代表 HY 或軟體再融資可以用同樣利差通過。

私募信用與軟體貸款是風險端最需要區分的區域。部分軟體借款人面臨到期牆、AI 替代壓力與直接貸款人更挑剔的承作條件，老經濟中型企業反而被部分 distressed credit 投資人視為更可分析的收益來源。這個分化對債券 ETF 的含義是，投資級現金流與抵押品質仍可收收益，但 HY 軟體與槓桿貸款 beta 不宜只因指數利差尚穩就放大部位。

EM 主權風險呈現油價、IMF 條款與本幣政策的三重差異。Zambia 債券約 81c，高於 78c 回購價，且超過 25% 的持有人群組足以影響回購條款；若 IMF 指標達成，票息可能自 0.5% 階梯升至 7.5%。EM 硬通貨配置要看條款與商品收入，不宜只買高名目收益。

波蘭、南非、印尼、斯里蘭卡與其他 EM 央行的升息或偏緊訊號，說明地緣與油價已讓新興市場優先保護通膨可信度。波蘭央行在政策利率 3.75% 附近暫停，市場卻已反映不少收緊風險

四、配置含義

配置重點是降低追價、保留 5Y-10Y 相對價值，信用以高品質收益為主。

久期建議維持偏短至中性，而不是在 10Y 4.49% 立即大幅加碼。理由是 ADP +122k、ISM Services 54.5 與 WTI 95 美元共同支撐 Fed 升息選擇權，且 2Y 與 5Y 一周已升 9bp、6bp，政策敏感段尚未確認釋放壓力。

曲線上偏好 5Y-10Y 的相對價值，避免單純買 30Y。30Y 接近 4.99%、20Y 5.00%，看似收益吸引，但 5s30s 已收斂至 78bp、月內收斂 16bp，長端期限溢價未被完全壓下。若 30Y 站上 5.05% 且 10Y 未同步破 4.55%，才考慮分批增加長端；否則以 5Y-10Y 做主要久期表達，降低 fiscal 與供給風險。

信用配置維持 IG 與高品質金融、基建、能源效率相關收益，HY beta 暫不追。Alphabet 免稅能源融資與 Bahrain 7.125% 主權債顯示一級市場仍能承接清楚故事，但軟體私募信用、Zambia 條款與 EM 油價壓力提醒市場正在重估弱資產負債表。若 HY ETF 利差未給足油價與再融資風險，應把收益留在 IG、短中天期與有現金流抵押的結構。

EM 與亞洲利率配置要把油價與美元放在同一個觸發點。印度銀行貸款 +16.2% 與主權殖利率 7.04% 說明本地資金成本正在上行；Ghana CPI 3.7% 低於預期則提供特定主權利多。

加碼條件應設在 10Y 低於 4.45%、WTI 低於 92 美元、且利差穩定，三者至少兩項成立。

若需要維持收益曝險，較好的方式是用 barbell 降低單點政策風險：短端保留現金與短票的再投資彈性，中段用 5Y-10Y UST 或高評級美元債承接收益，長端只用小比例做凸性。這樣可以在 Fed swaps 85% 升息機率回落時參與多頭轉平，也能在非農強於 +85k、薪資高於 +0.3% 時避免前端與中段部位過度受損。

風險預算上，今天不宜把美股回落解讀成買債保證。S&P 500 -0.7%、Nasdaq -0.9% 仍不足以抵消油價與就業數據；配置上應把 equity drawdown、油價衝擊、Fed 重新定價分開管理，不用單一股債負相關假設。

對帳戶層級的風險控管，建議把觸發條件寫成可執行的三段式。第一段是 10Y 4.45% 下方才把久期從中性調成偏多；第二段是 WTI 92 美元下方才放寬對前端政策溢價的警戒；但必須確認利率與利差未抵銷。若三段只成立一段，配置維持防守；若成立兩段以上，才把現金轉入中段債與 IG ETF。

Investment Read

1. UST Close and Curve Moves

USTs saw bear flattening driven by a joint upward move in the front end and belly, with the long end relatively sticky.

The UST close defined the session as bear flattening rather than haven-style bull flattening: 2Y closed at 4.08%, up 3bp on the day; 5Y closed at 4.21%, up 4bp; 10Y closed at 4.49%, up 3bp; and 30Y closed at 4.99%, up 2bp. 2s10s held at 41bp and 5s30s compressed 2bp to 78bp, indicating that the market first priced oil, services, and employment resilience into the policy path. The long end remains close to 5%, but it did not lose control further in relative terms.

The weekly change in the official CMT series also confirms this was not one-day noise: 2Y rose 9bp over the week, 5Y rose 6bp, 10Y rose 4bp, and 30Y rose only 1bp. On a monthly basis, 2Y and 5Y are each up 13bp, 10Y is up only 4bp, and 30Y is lower by 3bp. This structure shows the market is removing the rate-cut tail. Carry protection in front-end and belly Duration has declined, and curve trades should not simply bet on a one-way decline in the long end.

Employment data was the first catalyst for front-end repricing. ADP showed May private payrolls at +122k and wage growth at 4.4% YoY, above market expectations and the strongest signal of corporate hiring since January 2025. Friday’s nonfarm payrolls consensus remains only +85k, with the unemployment rate at 4.3% and average hourly earnings at +0.3% m/m. If the official jobs report does not weaken materially, the discount applied to the Fed hike option in 2Y and 5Y will continue to shrink, and support below 4.45% in 10Y will harden.

Services data put growth resilience and inflation pressure on the same curve. ISM Services for May came in at 54.5, above market consensus near 53.7 and the prior 53.6. New orders rose to 57.3 and business activity to 57.7, while the employment component remained at 47.9. The rates implication of this mix is not simply pro-growth or pro-recession. Demand is still not weak enough for the Fed to ease, while the employment component reminds investors that the long end cannot absorb higher real rates indefinitely. That makes the relative pressure in 5Y versus 30Y clearer.

Oil was the second catalyst, with WTI closing near $95.23, up around 1.8% on the day, while Brent was still above $98 intraday. An energy shock first lifts CPI expectations and corporate costs, then transmits through Fed swaps into the short end. Gold fell 1.2% the same day to $4,434, showing the tape was not simply a geopolitical haven move, but a return of the dollar and real rates. For USTs, elevated oil prices mean risk compensation in 2Y-5Y adjusts faster than in 20Y-30Y.

The cross-asset close also confirmed that rates pressure is starting to affect risk absorption. The S&P 500 closed at 7,553.68, down 0.7%; the Nasdaq closed at 26,853.98, down 0.9%; and the Dow closed at 50,687.07, down 1.2%, breaking a nine-day advance. The equity decline did not generate material UST buying, meaning the risk-asset pullback was still offset by oil and the policy premium, reducing Duration’s defensive function in the near term.

Supply was not the main theme, but the long end near 5% remains a price signal that allocations must respect. 20Y closed at 5.00% and 30Y at 4.99%, while 10s30s compressed to 50bp, showing that long-end term premium was not fully washed out by the decline in risk assets. When 30Y near 5% can still only follow slightly lower or slightly higher, the absorption capacity of insurers, pension funds, and overseas buyers will decide whether the curve can move from bear flattening into a more tradable belly-led bull flattening.

2. Interpreting the UST Move

The pricing logic has shifted from a delayed easing timetable to oil and employment jointly preserving the Fed hike option.

The core of this upward move is not growth panic, but a forced widening of the policy reaction function. Fed swaps pushed the probability of a 25bp hike by year-end to around 85%, versus roughly 60% one week ago. This shift directly compresses the rate-cut premium in 2Y and 5Y, so the front end no longer treats a modest risk-asset decline as a reason to buy bonds. As long as the market believes the Fed needs to retain hike language, 2s10s will struggle to steepen quickly even if inversion does not extend further.

Williams said the current policy stance is appropriate and offered no clear direction on hikes or cuts, while Logan warned that a hike could still be needed this year if inflation and demand do not cool. Taken together, these signals move the Fed from a single path to a conditional reaction function: if oil, tariffs, and AI investment keep financial conditions loose, the front end must pay more insurance; if labor data weakens, only then does the long end have enough basis to pull 10Y back below 4.45%.

The Beige Book supports this conditional pricing. Ten of 12 districts reported slight to modest activity expansion, with companies still citing energy, transportation, packaging, food, and fertilizer costs. Manufacturing hiring was also supported by defense and data-center demand. This makes the 54.5 services-growth signal harder to interpret as a temporary rebound, because cost pass-through and investment demand remain in place, making it difficult for the Fed to turn dovish on one weak employment sub-index.

The transmission from oil and geopolitical conflict into the curve is mainly through inflation risk, not immediate liquidity stress. The U.S. and Iran took mutual military actions, Gulf sovereign risk rose, and WTI remained above $95 even after falling from its intraday highs. If companies pass energy costs into services prices, the Fed’s tolerance for second-round effects will decline. This makes 5Y the most uncomfortable bucket, because it reflects both the next several FOMC meetings and medium-term inflation risk.

Long-end stickiness reflects the joint constraints of fiscal risk and global Duration. 30Y closed at 4.99% and 20Y at 5.00%, with no material rally despite the equity pullback. For Treasuries, this means that when 10Y is near 4.50%, investors should look beyond risk assets and assess whether overseas long-end markets can provide real-money absorption.

Curve trades should view this episode as near-term compression after a rise in front-end policy premium, not a full long-end repricing. 2s10s has compressed 9bp over the month and 5s30s has compressed 16bp, indicating the curve has already reflected substantial policy repricing. If Friday’s nonfarm payrolls merely match consensus at +85k, wages at +0.3%, and unemployment at 4.3%, the curve may first consolidate in a 10Y 4.45%-4.55% range rather than immediately opening a new large selloff.

Conversely, if nonfarm payrolls and initial jobless claims confirm that the labor market has not cooled, the 85% hike probability in Fed swaps could move from tail risk to base case, with 2Y repricing faster than 30Y. That would expose carry books to negative convexity: coupons remain, but price volatility is concentrated in policy-sensitive tenors, reducing the win rate of simply extending Duration and waiting for haven demand.

Another factor that needs to be separated is the divergence between the dollar and gold. Instead, it embedded that risk into the dollar, real rates, and front-end policy premium.

3. Other Market Developments

Cross-market confirmation is concentrated in central banks, credit primary markets, private credit, and diverging EM funding conditions.

Signals from the UK and Europe skew toward limiting global Duration bulls. The OECD expects the BOE to stay on hold this year and cut only to 3.5% in 2027, while forecasting UK CPI at 3.7% in 2026 and 2.4% in 2027, unemployment at 5.5% in 2026 and 5.3% in 2027, and debt-to-GDP rising from 98.8% in 2023 to 105.4% in 2027. This set of numbers means gilts are unlikely to provide a clear global lead rally, leaving the UST long end without an external bull-flattening anchor.

EU-China and U.S. tariff issues are affecting rates through goods, supply chains, and investment costs. The EU is preparing to advise households and companies on tougher trade measures toward China, while the U.S. is also discussing import tariffs of at least 10% on several trade partners. These are not one-day CPI prints, but they increase uncertainty around corporate input costs and inventory strategies. For bond markets, the more supply-chain repricing looks like cost-push inflation, the harder it is for the front end to turn dovish quickly on growth concerns.

If BOJ communication is more conservative than markets expect, yen weakness will lift import-inflation pressure. If the BOJ shifts toward a clearer hike, long-end JGBs and overseas hedging costs will compress the room for Japanese capital to allocate to USD bonds.

Australia and India show that local Asian funding conditions are not uniform. Australia’s A$1bn 7Y auction had a bid-to-cover ratio above 4x, reflecting demand for belly bonds supported by the RBA pause signal and Q1 GDP growth of only 0.3%. But India’s bank loans rose 16.2% YoY, state bond sales fell 11% YoY to INR10.9tn, and sovereign yields rose to 7.04%. This means Asian Duration is not a broad-based bid. Capital will be reallocated between markets with slowing growth and markets under pressure from high oil prices.

The credit primary window remains open, but quality differentiation is very clear. Alphabet executed $1bn of prepaid energy tax-exempt bonds through the California Community Choice Financing Authority, while Bahrain priced 10Y USD bonds at 7.125% on more than $3.2bn of orders, 37.5bp tighter than initial guidance. At the same time, Gulf sovereign revenues are tied to shipping and security risks. This shows that high quality, tax efficiency, or sovereign yield can still attract capital, but credit buyers are demanding clearer yield compensation.

AI and data-center-related financing continues to support investment demand, but it is also intensifying credit dispersion. U.S. corporate equity and debt supply has been absorbed by the market, and data-center investment also made a visible contribution to Australian GDP. But if AI capex keeps financial conditions loose, the Fed’s inflation and demand risks will be harder to remove. At the intersection of credit and rates, demand for high-quality AI-related issuance does not mean HY or software refinancing can clear at the same spreads.

Private credit and software loans are the areas of the risk complex that require the most differentiation. Some software borrowers face maturity walls, AI substitution pressure, and more selective underwriting standards from direct lenders, while parts of the old-economy middle market are being viewed by some distressed credit investors as more analyzable sources of yield. For bond ETFs, the implication is that investment-grade cash flows and collateral quality can still harvest yield, but HY software and leveraged-loan beta should not be scaled up simply because index spreads remain stable.

EM sovereign risk shows a three-way divergence across oil prices, IMF terms, and local policy. Zambia bonds are around 81c, above the 78c buyback price, and a holder group with more than 25% is enough to influence buyback terms. If IMF metrics are met, coupons could step up from 0.5% to 7.5%. EM hard-currency allocation should focus on terms and commodity revenues, rather than simply buying high nominal yield.

Hike or hawkish signals from Poland, South Africa, Indonesia, Sri Lanka, and other EM central banks show that geopolitics and oil prices have pushed emerging markets to prioritize protection of inflation credibility. Poland’s central bank paused with the policy rate near 3.75%, but the market has already reflected considerable tightening risk. This type of local-market policy premium affects UST demand through the dollar, oil, and risk capital.

4. Allocation Implications

The allocation focus is to reduce chasing, preserve relative value in 5Y-10Y, and keep credit exposure centered on high-quality yield.

Duration should remain short-to-neutral, rather than adding heavily as soon as 10Y reaches 4.49%. The reason is that ADP +122k, ISM Services 54.5, and WTI at $95 jointly support the Fed hike option, while 2Y and 5Y have already risen 9bp and 6bp over the week, with policy-sensitive tenors not yet confirmed to have released pressure.

On the curve, favor relative value in 5Y-10Y and avoid simply buying 30Y. 30Y near 4.99% and 20Y at 5.00% look yield-attractive, but 5s30s has already compressed to 78bp and by 16bp over the month, while long-end term premium has not been fully suppressed. If 30Y rises above 5.05% and 10Y does not simultaneously break 4.55%, then consider gradually increasing long-end exposure. Otherwise, use 5Y-10Y as the main Duration expression to reduce fiscal and supply risk.

Credit allocation should remain in IG and high-quality financials, infrastructure, and energy-efficiency-related yield, while not chasing HY beta for now. Alphabet’s tax-exempt energy financing and Bahrain’s 7.125% sovereign bond show the primary market can still absorb clear stories, but software private credit, Zambia terms, and EM oil-price pressure show that the market is repricing weaker balance sheets. If HY ETF spreads do not adequately compensate for oil and refinancing risk, keep yield in IG, short-to-intermediate maturities, and structures backed by cash-flow collateral.

EM and Asian rates allocation should put oil and the dollar into the same trigger framework. India bank loans at +16.2% and sovereign yields at 7.04% show local funding costs are rising, while Ghana CPI at 3.7% below expectations provides specific sovereign support.

If yield exposure must be maintained, the better approach is to use a barbell to reduce single-point policy risk: keep cash and short bills at the front end for reinvestment flexibility, use 5Y-10Y USTs or high-grade USD bonds in the belly to harvest yield, and use only a small allocation to the long end for convexity. This allows participation in bull flattening if the 85% hike probability in Fed swaps falls back, while avoiding excessive damage in front-end and belly positions if nonfarm payrolls beat +85k and wages exceed +0.3%.

In risk budgeting, today’s equity decline should not be read as a guaranteed bond-buying signal. S&P 500 -0.7% and Nasdaq -0.9% were still not enough to offset oil and employment data. Allocation should manage equity drawdown, oil shock, and Fed repricing separately, without relying on a single negative stock-bond correlation assumption.

At the account level, risk controls should be written as an executable three-stage framework. The first stage is to move Duration from neutral to overweight only below 4.45% in 10Y; the second is to relax caution on front-end policy premium only below $92 in WTI; If only one of the three stages is met, keep allocations defensive. If two or more are met, then rotate cash into belly bonds and IG ETFs.

5. Watchlist

Trading triggers are concentrated in nonfarm payrolls, oil, Fed language, key 10Y/30Y levels, and credit issuance quality.

The first trigger is Friday’s nonfarm payrolls and Thursday’s initial jobless claims. If payrolls exceed +125k, the unemployment rate is below 4.3%, or average hourly earnings exceed +0.3% m/m, the hike tail risk in Fed swaps will look more like the base case, with 2Y potentially retesting the 4.15% area and 5Y moving back above 4.25%. If payrolls are below +50k and initial claims rise meaningfully, only then would 10Y have the conditions to break below 4.45%.

The second trigger is oil. If WTI moves back above $98, the market will bring energy cost pass-through and second-round inflation effects back into the June FOMC, and 2s10s could compress again. If WTI falls back below $92 and Brent returns below $95, Fed officials’ defensive language on oil will weaken, giving 5Y-10Y Duration a cleaner rebound window.

The third trigger is 10Y 4.45% / 4.55% and 30Y 5.05%. If 10Y holds above 4.55%, services and employment data are overpowering the risk-asset pullback, and investors should reduce chasing in the belly. Only if 10Y breaks below 4.45% and 30Y does not stay above 5% would it indicate real-money demand has regained control of the curve. If 30Y breaks above 5.05%, long-end supply and fiscal premium will force curve positions to reduce leverage.

The fourth trigger is the 6/16-17 FOMC statement language and official commentary. If the statement removes rate-cut-leaning hints, or the dot plot or press conference retains hike conditions, the front end will reprice premium. If neutral language similar to Williams dominates and Logan’s hike risk is downplayed, the market can treat the 85% hike probability as overpricing. At that point, 2Y below 4.00% would be needed to confirm that rate-cut premium has returned.

The fifth trigger is credit primary-market quality. If IG, financials, and utilities new issues can still price tighter while HY software and private-credit-related borrowers require larger concessions, allocation should maintain a high-quality barbell. If Bahrain-like sovereigns and AI-related financing both require meaningful concession, that would mean high rates are starting to penetrate the primary market, and credit beta should be de-risked earlier than Duration.

The sixth trigger is the dollar and Asian policy. Before that condition holds, additions to overseas bond ETFs should be phased and concentrated in intermediate maturities.

The seventh trigger is whether the market can convert risk-asset weakness back into bond haven demand. If the S&P 500 falls further but 10Y still holds near 4.50%, oil and Fed pricing are still overpowering haven demand, and investors should not chase Duration.

The eighth trigger is supply and auction tails. If upcoming 10Y or 30Y auctions show a clear tail and bid-to-cover falls below recent averages, the market will read 20Y at 5.00% and 30Y at 4.99% as still insufficient to attract long-term capital, and the curve needs to guard against a long-end reset. If auction demand is stable and oil falls, the long end can become a portfolio hedge again.
