盤面定義

一、UST 收盤與曲線變化

週五是前中段帶動的戰術性利率修復，長端仍貼近 5% 壓力區。

週五美債收盤呈現短端帶動的多頭轉平回落，但不是長端全面重估。官方 UST 收盤顯示 2Y 收 3.98%、日降 1bp，5Y 收 4.13%、日降 2bp，10Y 收 4.45% 持平，30Y 反而小升 1bp 至 4.99%；2s10s 擴至 47bp、5s30s 擴至 86bp，說明反彈主要集中在前中段，超長端仍被期限溢價與供給風險牽制。第1個檢查點要回到同一條投組鏈：這一天的關鍵不是短端下行本身，而是 30Y 仍貼近 5% 使長端修復不完整；因此 5Y-10Y 的風險報酬優於 20Y+，但若後續標售需求或期限溢價沒有改善，收盤的利率修復不能外推成長端趨勢。這樣可避免把同一個收盤變化誤讀成單一方向押注。

從週線看，2Y 回落 15bp、5Y 回落 14bp、10Y 回落 11bp，30Y 僅回落 8bp，這種結構通常代表市場先降低短期政策風險，而不是買進完整久期曲線。月線則更清楚：2Y 仍高於月前 6bp、5Y 高 8bp、10Y 高 3bp、30Y 高 1bp，代表五月以來的高利率維持更久與長端重估並未被週五走勢完全抹去。第2個檢查點要回到同一條投組鏈：這一天的關鍵不是短端下行本身，而是 30Y 仍貼近 5% 使長端修復不完整；因此 5Y-10Y 的風險報酬優於 20Y+，但若後續標售需求或期限溢價沒有改善，收盤的利率修復不能外推成長端趨勢。這樣可避免把同一個收盤變化誤讀成單一方向押注。

宏觀驅動來自兩個相反力量。第一，油價在五月跌幅超過 16%，Brent 週五約 92 美元，降低整體通膨溢價與前端通膨平衡率壓力；第二，週一亞洲時段 Brent 回到約 93 美元且美元跌幅收斂，反映 geopolitics 尚未解除。因此週五的利率下行更像油價回落帶來的戰術性 trade，而非通膨風險的結構性下降。第3個檢查點要回到同一條投組鏈：這一天的關鍵不是短端下行本身，而是 30Y 仍貼近 5% 使長端修復不完整；因此 5Y-10Y 的風險報酬優於 20Y+，但若後續標售需求或期限溢價沒有改善，收盤的利率修復不能外推成長端趨勢。這樣可避免把同一個收盤變化誤讀成單一方向押注。

定價邏輯

二、美債走勢解讀

油價回落只提供短線喘息，Fed 與全球央行仍需要更明確的通膨降溫。

本輪反彈的性質應定義為戰術性多頭轉平，而不是 structural 長端重估。2Y 與 5Y 的週線下行幅度大於 10Y 與 30Y，反映市場先降低 immediate 政策溢價；但 10s30s 仍在 54bp、30Y 接近 5%，表示期限溢價、財政供給與全球長端外溢還沒有被重新定價到偏低區間。第1個檢查點要回到同一條投組鏈：換句話說，市場先降低近期政策風險，卻沒有解除長端資本吸收壓力；這會讓中段 ETF 受益於利率回落，但 20Y+ ETF 仍需面對供給、海外久期與凸性放大的價格風險。這樣可避免把同一個收盤變化誤讀成單一方向押注。

跨市驗證

三、其他市場動向

歐洲、英國、亞洲與信用市場都指向品質分化，而不是無條件加風險。

歐洲政策線索偏向 inflation credibility，而非立即轉鴿。ECB 會議前，市場關注官員是否傾向較早調整的政策調整；若通膨預期或薪資數據不配合，Bund 曲線對美債的外溢會集中在 5Y-10Y relative value，壓縮 UST 中段反彈的空間。

英國訊號同樣指向期限溢價黏性。BOE 官員指出低通膨好運時期已結束，這不是單一國家的表述，而是全球央行面對能源、供應鏈與 wage inertia 時的共同語言。對固定收益而言，這代表長端反彈需要更高門檻，單靠風險偏多或油價回落不足以讓 30Y 快速遠離 5%。

亞洲方面，印度在央行會議前強調 inflation vigilance，核心傳導來自能源、rupee 與 wholesale price pressure。

Credit 市場維持分化但尚未轉弱。大型媒體併購相關融資規模約 500 億美元，橫跨 IG、HY 與 leveraged loans，投資人仍願意承接具 rating support 或資產覆蓋的 supply。這種環境支持高品質收益，但不代表應該追逐所有 low-quality beta，因為融資方案的定價會把發行人 quality 差異放大。

EM debt 的強勢開始碰到容量約束。部分策略因小型、distressed 或流動性較差市場容量有限而控制資金，這通常出現在收益 trade 已變擁擠、而非剛起步的階段。對配置而言，創紀錄反彈可維持票息收益，但 sovereign selection、IMF program risk 與二級流動性要比單純買 beta 更重要。

信用市場的風險不是需求消失，而是需求開始挑剔。大型媒體融資案可以測試 IG、HY 與貸款市場的承接深度；若具 rating support 的 borrower 仍能低讓利完成，代表高品質收益還可持有；若弱資本結構 borrower 也被迫加大 concession，則代表技術面開始鬆動。

EM debt 的容量限制是另一個需要放進配置的訊號。當小型或 distressed 市場因資金容量受限而需要控管流入，表示反彈已經不只是起步階段。

中國製造業訊號偏弱也提供反向檢驗。若中國需求疲弱壓低 commodity impulse，理論上有利全球通膨；因此中國資料不能只當作油價利多，也要看它是否轉化為亞洲風險資產壓力。

跨資產的真正訊號，是風險資產仍願意買成長題材，但固定收益市場沒有把這件事視為全面放鬆金融條件。美股創高、AI beta 續強與信用融資需求仍在，說明資金不是在撤出風險；但長端接近 5% 也表示投資人仍要求期限溢價補償。對 ETF 配置而言，這比單純 risk-on / risk-off 更重要：高品質收益可留，長天期凸性不能因股市強就盲目加碼。

亞洲與 EM 的讀法也必須分層。若 EM 債吸金但容量受限，代表收益仍有需求但已開始挑市場。

配置動作

四、配置含義

配置上只承接中段Duration與高品質收益，不追長端與低品質 beta。

久期 stance 維持中性偏多，但集中在 5Y-10Y，不追 20Y+。10Y 4.45% 是戰術性 pivot，若能跌破 4.45% 並守住，代表非農就業或通膨數據支持中段修復；若回到 4.55% 上方，則說明油價回落被政策與供給風險抵消，應降低長久期 beta。

曲線上偏好戰術性多頭轉平，而非押注長端 structural 反彈。2s10s 在 47bp、5s30s 在 86bp，前中段仍有受益空間；但 30Y 4.99% 與 20Y 4.98% 仍貼近 5%，若 30Y 重新站回 5.10% 或標售尾差擴大，應把曲線 view 從中段偏多轉為少量 steepener protection。

Credit / ETF 配置以高品質收益優先。IG、金融與具 rating support 的 primary supply 可維持選擇性曝險；HY 與 loans 若只靠股票風險支撐，風險報酬已不如五月中旬。

若油價守在低位、美元漲勢放緩，則 5Y-10Y 收益 plus 下滑收益仍是較乾淨的表達。

配置執行上，第一層是用 5Y-10Y 高評等美元債承接 10Y 4.45% 附近的中段修復。這一層的優勢是能吃到收益與下滑收益，也避免 30Y 供給與期限溢價突然回升時承受最大價格波動。若 10Y 跌破 4.45% 且 2Y 同步下行，再逐步提高久期 beta。

第二層是信用品質。IG、金融債與有資產覆蓋的大型融資案仍可配置，但 HY 與貸款要看再融資能否低讓利完成。若市場只因股市創高就推升低品質 beta，風險報酬會變差；真正能留在組合裡的是能穿過利率波動、流動性退潮與再融資壓力的發行人。

這也是為什麼長天期 ETF 不宜因單日收盤改善就大幅追價。

部位控管上，這份 close note 應當被視為「可持有但不追價」的訊號。若組合已經有中段久期，可以讓 5Y-10Y 持倉繼續吃收益與下滑收益；若部位不足，應等 10Y 接近 4.55% 或非農就業給出更弱數據再加，而不是在 4.45% 下方追入超長天期。

因此 5/29 的部位語言應是『持有中段、保留現金、等待更好加碼點』。若 10Y 在 4.45% 下方但 30Y 仍貼近 5%，追 20Y+ ETF 的風險報酬不如持有 5Y-10Y；若信用新券仍能被吸收但弱質 borrower concessions 擴大，IG / 金融 / 公用事業類收益會優於 HY beta。這種環境不是全面減風險，而是把收益來源從方向 beta 換成品質與期限選擇。

對收入型投資人而言，這一天也要避免把配息需求和總報酬判斷混在一起。

這也是為什麼 5/29 的最佳表達不是追高長債，而是把既有部位拆成三層：中段利率部位續抱、高品質信用續收收益、低品質與長端凸性等更便宜的進場。若三層訊號沒有同步改善，部位就應維持分段而非一次加滿。

交易觸發

五、後續觀察重點

後續先看 10Y 4.45/4.55、30Y 5.10、油價 95 與非農就業。

第一個觀察點是 10Y 4.45% / 4.55% 區間。若 10Y 明確跌破 4.45% 且 2Y 同步下行，代表市場把就業降溫轉成政策預期；若 10Y 站回 4.55%，則週五反彈會被定義為月底與油價回落，而非可追的久期趨勢。

第二個觀察點是 30Y 5.00%-5.10% 壓力區。若 30Y 重新站回 5.10%，表示期限溢價、標售需求或全球長端外溢重新主導，20Y-30Y 曝險應降到基準附近；若 30Y 能連續守在 5% 下方，quality 長久期 ETF 才有更好的凸性 payoff。

第三個觀察點是能源與美元的組合。

第四個觀察點是五月非農就業的構成，而不只是 headline +89k 與失業率 4.3%。healthcare、construction、leisure-hospitality 若仍吸收就業，Fed 對服務業通膨會保持耐心；若製造、construction 或 data-center investment 同步轉弱，中段反彈的基本面支撐會更強。

若 10Y 跌破 4.45% 但 2Y 沒有同步下行，代表市場可能只是在買長端供給後的技術性修復，而不是真正重估 Fed 路徑。這種情況下中段可以持有，但不應把超長端加到主動超配。

若 30Y 站回 5.10%，要把它視為長端期限溢價重新主導，而不是單純一天的殖利率 backup。這個門檻一旦被突破，20Y+ 會同時承受供給、財政和實錢帳戶 demand 的檢驗，ETF 久期曝險應先降回基準。

若非農就業、初領失業金或薪資資料顯示就業降溫，5Y-10Y 才有機會延續；

最後要監控的是信用與利率是否開始分道揚鑣。若股市與新券市場繼續強、但 2Y 與 10Y 同時回升，表示市場在接受成長韌性而非降息交易；這種環境對高品質收益友善，卻會壓縮長天期 ETF 的估值空間。只有當信用需求穩、油價低、利率也同步下行，才是較完整的加碼訊號。

Rates Close

1. UST Close and Curve Changes

Friday was a tactical rates repair led by the front end and belly, while the long end remained close to the 5% pressure zone.

USTs closed Friday with a front-end-led bull-flattening relief move, but not a broad long-end revaluation. Treasury CMT showed 2Y closing at 3.98%, down 1bp on the day; 5Y at 4.13%, down 2bp; 10Y unchanged at 4.45%; and 30Y slightly higher by 1bp to 4.99%. 2s10s widened to 47bp and 5s30s to 86bp, indicating the rally was concentrated mainly in the front end and belly, while the ultra-long end remained constrained by term premium and supply risk.

On a weekly basis, 2Y fell 15bp, 5Y fell 14bp, 10Y fell 11bp, and 30Y fell only 8bp. This structure usually means the market first reduced near-term policy risk rather than buying the full duration curve. The monthly picture is even clearer: 2Y is still 6bp above a month ago, 5Y 8bp higher, 10Y 3bp higher, and 30Y 1bp higher, meaning the higher-for-longer and long-end reset since May have not been fully erased by Friday's move.

The macro impulse came from two opposing forces. First, oil fell more than 16% in May, with Brent around $92 on Friday, reducing headline inflation premium and front-end inflation breakeven pressure. Second, Brent returned to around $93 in Monday Asia trading and the dollar's decline narrowed, reflecting that geopolitics has not cleared. Friday's rate decline therefore looked more like a tactical trade driven by oil relief than a structural decline in inflation risk.

Cross-asset confirmation does not support a growth-recession narrative. US equities hit new highs Friday, the S&P 500 rose for a ninth straight week, Nasdaq 100 futures were still around +0.2% in Monday Asia trading, and junk bonds and Treasuries benefited at the same time. When equity beta, credit beta, and EM debt are all firm, 10Y staying near 4.45% looks more like a risk environment that allows duration relief than a market pricing a hard landing.

Supply remains the long-end ceiling constraint. 5Y-30Y yields have already fallen 8-14bp on the week, but 20Y is still at 4.98% and 30Y at 4.99%, showing investors need clearer auction demand or inflation data before they are willing to push the long end more deeply below 5%. After the month-end coupon cycle is complete, the next round of refunding, tails, indirect demand, and dealer balance-sheet capacity will determine whether 30Y can leave the 5.00%-5.10% pressure zone.

From a trade-structure perspective, the larger weekly decline in 2Y and 5Y versus 30Y shows investors first bought back the front end and belly rather than extended duration across the curve. if the ultra-long end is merely holding below 5%, it does not yet prove that term premium has been compressed again.

The monthly oil decline and Monday rebound coexist, so the 5/29 close should be judged in two layers: the official close and pre-market risk.

Supply also argues against over-extrapolating the rally. The month-end coupon cycle is complete, which does reduce near-term auction pressure; but 20Y at 4.98% and 30Y at 4.99% are still near 5%, meaning real-money demand has not yet pulled the long end into a comfortable zone. If later reopenings show tails or weaker indirect demand, Friday's belly repair will be tested again.

Rates Interpretation

2. Interpreting the UST Move

The oil pullback only provides near-term breathing room; the Fed and global central banks still need clearer evidence of inflation cooling.

This rally should be defined as tactical bull flattening, not a structural long-end reset. The larger weekly declines in 2Y and 5Y than in 10Y and 30Y show the market first reduced immediate policy premium; but 10s30s is still at 54bp and 30Y is close to 5%, meaning term premium, fiscal supply, and global long-end spillover have not been repriced into a lower range.

The key to the Fed reaction function is whether the labor market validates oil relief. May nonfarm payrolls are expected at around +89k, with unemployment at 4.3%. If the result undershoots expectations and unemployment rises, 5Y-10Y can extend the rally because the market will convert growth softening into a rate-cut timetable; if employment, wages, or services vacancies remain resilient, the 2Y relief at 3.98% will be seen as premature.

The inflation path is still controlled jointly by energy and second-round effects. The sharp monthly oil decline helps the near-month path for headline CPI / PCE, but central-bank officials still view wage-setting, services prices, and import costs as slow-moving variables. This means the front end cannot restart an aggressive easing trade solely on one day or one week of Brent weakness. For the curve, oil below the 90-95 dollar range is helpful for the belly; a return to that range would bring front-end breakevens and hike optionality back together.

Global duration spillover also limits the UST long end. If Bunds, gilts, or the JGB ultra-long end come under pressure again, UST 30Y would struggle to launch a deep rally on its own even if it holds near 5% in the short term.

The next step in the Fed reaction function is not whether oil falls for one week, but whether labor and services inflation jointly provide confirmation. Only if payrolls come in below the +89k forecast and unemployment rises toward above 4.3% will the market have reason to upgrade the 5Y-10Y rally from tactical repair to a more durable policy-expectations move. Conversely, if wages or services employment remain resilient, 2Y downside will be clawed back first.

Global central-bank signals also set a threshold for any long-end rally. Inflation expectations remain high ahead of the ECB meeting, the BOE's warning about the end of the low-inflation era continues, and the RBI is also cautious across energy, the rupee, and wholesale costs. These signals will make it difficult for Bunds, gilts, and Asia front ends to ease together, so UST 30Y needs stronger domestic US data or auction demand before it can leave the 5% pressure zone.

Putting the 5/29 close into the 5/28 to 6/1 sequence, the most important change is that the market is moving from relief on cooling oil prices into range trading while waiting for labor-data confirmation. On 5/28, the intermediate-to-long end first moved lower; on 5/29, 10Y held at 4.45% and 30Y rose slightly to 4.99%. This shows the market is willing to retain a belly long, but is not willing to fully buy the long end ahead of payrolls, the ECB, and the next round of supply.

Cross-Market Read

3. Other Market Moves

Europe, the UK, Asia, and credit markets all point to quality differentiation rather than unconditional risk adding.

European policy signals lean toward inflation credibility rather than an immediate dovish turn. Ahead of the ECB meeting, markets are watching whether officials favor sooner-rather-than-later policy adjustment. If inflation expectations or wage data do not cooperate, Bund-curve spillover to USTs will be concentrated in 5Y-10Y relative value, compressing room for a UST belly rally.

The UK signal also points to sticky term premium. A BOE official said the period of low-inflation luck has ended. This is not just a country-specific statement, but the shared language of global central banks facing energy, supply-chain, and wage inertia. For fixed income, it means a higher threshold for a long-end rally; risk-on conditions or lower oil alone are not enough to move 30Y quickly away from 5%.

In Asia, India emphasized inflation vigilance ahead of the central-bank meeting, with the key transmission channels coming from energy, the rupee, and wholesale price pressure.

The credit market remains differentiated but has not turned weak. Large media M&A-related financing totals roughly $50bn and spans IG, HY, and leveraged loans. Investors are still willing to absorb supply with rating support or asset coverage. This backdrop supports high-quality income, but does not mean investors should chase all low-quality beta, because financing-package pricing will amplify differences in issuer quality.

The strength in EM debt is starting to run into capacity constraints. Some strategies are managing capital because of limited capacity in smaller, distressed, or less liquid markets. This usually appears when the carry trade has become crowded, not when it is just beginning. For allocation, a record rally can sustain coupon carry, but sovereign selection, IMF program risk, and secondary-market liquidity matter more than simply buying beta.

The risk in credit is not that demand has disappeared, but that demand is becoming more selective. Large media financing can test the depth of demand across IG, HY, and loan markets. If borrowers with rating support can still complete deals with limited concessions, high-quality income remains holdable; if borrowers with weaker capital structures are also forced to increase concessions, technicals are starting to loosen.

Capacity limits in EM debt are another signal that needs to be built into allocation. When smaller or distressed markets need to manage inflows because of capacity constraints, it indicates the rally is no longer merely in its early stage.

Softer China manufacturing signals also provide a counter-check. If weak China demand suppresses commodity impulse, it is theoretically helpful for global inflation; China data therefore cannot be treated only as positive for oil; investors also need to watch whether it turns into pressure on Asia risk assets.

The real cross-asset message is that risk assets were still willing to buy growth themes, while fixed income did not treat that as a full easing of financial conditions. Record equities, strong AI beta, and ongoing financing demand show risk capital was not leaving the market. But the long end near 5% also shows investors still required term-premium compensation. For ETF allocation, that distinction matters more than a simple risk-on / risk-off label: quality carry can stay, but long-end convexity should not be added just because equities are firm.

Asia and EM also need a layered read. EM debt inflows show carry demand, but capacity constraints mean investors are already choosing markets more carefully.

Allocation Read

4. Allocation Implications

Allocation should receive belly duration and high-quality income only, without chasing the long end or low-quality beta.

The duration stance remains neutral to slightly long, but concentrated in 5Y-10Y and not chasing 20Y+. 10Y at 4.45% is the tactical pivot. If it can break and hold below 4.45%, it would signal payrolls or inflation data supporting belly relief; if it moves back above 4.55%, that would show oil relief has been offset by policy and supply risk, and long-duration beta should be reduced.

On the curve, prefer tactical bull flattening rather than betting on a structural long-end rally. 2s10s at 47bp and 5s30s at 86bp still leave room for front-to-belly benefits; but 30Y at 4.99% and 20Y at 4.98% remain close to 5%. If 30Y moves back to 5.10% or auction tails widen, the curve view should shift from belly long to modest steepener protection.

For Credit / ETF allocation, prioritize high-quality income. IG, financials, and primary supply with rating support can maintain selective exposure; HY and loans supported only by equity beta have less attractive risk-reward than in mid-May.

If oil stays low and the dollar advance slows, 5Y-10Y carry plus roll-down remains the cleaner expression.

For implementation, start with 5Y-10Y high-grade USD bonds to receive belly repair around 10Y 4.45%. This expression can capture income and roll-down while avoiding the largest price volatility if 30Y supply and term premium suddenly rise again. If 10Y breaks below 4.45% and 2Y declines at the same time, duration beta can then be raised gradually.

The second layer is credit quality. IG, financials, and large financing deals with asset coverage remain allocatable, but HY and loans need confirmation that refinancing can be completed with limited concessions. If the market pushes up low-quality beta only because equities are at highs, risk-reward will deteriorate. The issuers that can truly remain in portfolios are those that can withstand rates volatility, fading liquidity, and refinancing pressure.

If only the dollar strengthens or only rates fall while credit spreads begin to widen, near-term performance may simply be compensation from a single factor. This is also why long-duration ETFs should not be chased aggressively just because one daily close improves.

For position control, this close note should be treated as a signal to hold but not chase. If portfolios already have belly duration, 5Y-10Y holdings can continue to earn income and roll-down. If positioning is light, add only when 10Y approaches 4.55% or payrolls provide weaker data, rather than chasing the ultra-long end below 4.45%.

The 5/29 position language should therefore be: hold the belly, keep cash, and wait for a better add point. If 10Y is below 4.45% but 30Y is still close to 5%, chasing 20Y+ ETFs has a weaker risk/reward than holding 5Y-10Y. If new credit supply is absorbed while weak borrowers pay wider concessions, IG, financials, and utilities carry should outperform HY beta. This is not a broad de-risking tape; it is a shift from directional beta toward quality and tenor selection.

Income-oriented investors should also avoid mixing distribution needs with total-return judgment.

That is why the best 5/29 expression is not chasing long bonds. Split the existing book into three layers: keep belly rates exposure, keep high-quality credit carry, and wait for cheaper entry in low-quality beta and long-end convexity. If those layers do not improve together, keep adds staged rather than fully deployed.

Watchlist

5. Watchlist

Next, watch 10Y 4.45/4.55, 30Y 5.10, oil at 95, and payrolls.

The first watch point is the 10Y 4.45% / 4.55% range. If 10Y clearly breaks below 4.45% and 2Y declines at the same time, the market is translating employment cooling into policy expectations. If 10Y moves back above 4.55%, Friday's rally will be defined as month-end and oil relief rather than a chaseable duration trend.

The second watch point is the 30Y 5.00%-5.10% pressure zone. If 30Y moves back above 5.10%, term premium, auction demand, or global long-end spillover is again dominating, and 20Y-30Y exposure should be cut back toward benchmark. If 30Y can stay below 5% for consecutive sessions, quality long-duration ETFs would have a better convexity payoff.

The third watch point is the combination of energy and the dollar.

The fourth watch point is the composition of May payrolls, not just the headline +89k and unemployment at 4.3%. If healthcare, construction, and leisure-hospitality are still absorbing labor, the Fed will remain patient on services inflation. If manufacturing, construction, or data-center investment weaken at the same time, the fundamental support for a belly rally will be stronger.

If 10Y breaks below 4.45% but 2Y does not decline at the same time, the market may only be buying a technical repair after long-end supply rather than truly revaluing the Fed path. In that case, the belly can be held, but the ultra-long end should not be raised to an active overweight.

If 30Y moves back above 5.10%, it should be treated as long-end term premium regaining control, not simply a one-day yield backup. Once this threshold is broken, 20Y+ will simultaneously face tests from supply, fiscal risk, and real-money demand, and ETF duration risk should first be cut back to benchmark.

If payrolls, claims, or wage data show employment cooling, 5Y-10Y has room to extend;

The final watch point is whether credit and rates begin to diverge. If equities and the new-issue market remain strong while 2Y and 10Y both rise, the market is accepting growth resilience rather than a rate-cut trade. That environment is friendly for high-quality income, but it compresses valuation room for long-duration ETFs. A more complete add-risk signal requires stable credit demand, low oil, and rates falling at the same time.
