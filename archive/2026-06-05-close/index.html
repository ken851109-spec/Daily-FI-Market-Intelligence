盤面定義

一、UST 收盤與 Curve 變化

美債Curve是就業與通膨尾端風險重新定價，不是單純股市下跌帶來避險買債。

6 月 5 日美債收盤的核心不是「風險資產大跌所以買債」，而是就業數據把 Fed 政策路徑重新往右推。官方 CMT 顯示 2Y 收 4.17%、日升 12bp，5Y 收 4.29%、日升 11bp，10Y 收 4.55%、日升 8bp，20Y 與 30Y 分別在 5.03%、5.01%。這種前端與中段升幅大於長端的形狀，表示市場先重新定價升息尾端風險，再要求長端維持更高期限溢價；對台灣債券型 ETF 而言，這比單看 10Y 絕對殖利率更重要。因此，前端政策溢價回到盤面本身就是配置訊號；若只看價格變化，容易把較高殖利率誤判成已經具備足夠補償。

Curve 型態確認這是熊平而非單純長端拋售。2s10s 收 38bp、日內壓平 4bp，5s30s 收 72bp、日內壓平 7bp，10s30s 收 46bp、日內壓平 4bp。若只是風險資產下跌帶來避險需求，長端應該更容易下行；但實際上長端仍守在 5% 附近，說明長端買盤沒有取得主導權。這會讓 20Y+ ETF 的價格彈性受限，也讓中段 Duration 的風險報酬比相對更乾淨。因此熱圖裡的Curve列要被解讀為風險來源，而不是單純的數字摘要；壓平來自政策路徑，不是長端便宜後自動反彈。

非農 +172k 明顯高於約 +80k 共識，失業率維持 4.3%，4 月就業還上修到 +179k。這組數字讓市場很難繼續用「勞動市場快速轉弱」來定價降息，尤其 2Y 與 5Y 對 Fed 路徑最敏感。平均時薪年增 3.4% 本身不是失控，但它不足以抵銷就業驚喜與油價風險，因此前端重新定價不是單一資料點反應，而是把降息假設重新交給 CPI 與 FOMC 語句驗證。這也是市場驅動需要把就業與 Fed 定價放在同一群組、而不是和其他央行混在一起的原因，因為這一組才是當天 UST 的主因。

從一周與一個月變化看，這不是一天噪音。2Y 周升 19bp、月升 24bp，5Y 周升 16bp、月升 21bp，10Y 周升 10bp、月升 12bp；Curve 2s10s 月內壓平 12bp，5s30s 月內壓平 18bp。這些累積變化代表市場已從「何時降息」轉向「是否需要保留升息選項」。如果後續 CPI 接近 swap 隱含的 4.3%，這條重新定價鏈會延伸；若 CPI 低於 4.1%，才比較有條件把這次前端 selloff 視為過度反應。對 ETF 決策而言，這代表今天的關鍵不是找最低點，而是確認下一個資料是否推翻升息尾端風險，讓收益率提高轉成可承擔的風險補償。

長端沒有真正受惠於股市下跌，這是本日盤面的重要訊號。30Y 收 5.01%，20Y 收 5.03%，即使 Nasdaq 100 與 SOX 大跌，長端仍未出現乾淨的避險買盤。對台灣長天期美債 ETF 來說，這意味著「股市跌」不等於「長債 NAV 會修復」；長端需要同時看到供給壓力緩和、油價回落、CPI 不再上衝，以及 30Y 重新跌回 5% 下方，價格彈性才會回到比較有利的位置。換句話說，長端評估不能只看 5% 附近的殖利率，還要同步觀察 30Y 是否跌回 5% 下方，才有辦法判斷避險買盤是否有效。

油價與地緣風險是長端不能快速修復的第二條線。Brent 周五收 93.09、日跌 2.0%，WTI 收 90.54、日跌 2.7%，表面上有助通膨溢價降溫；但周末亞洲盤 Brent 反彈到約 95.60，表示市場還不能把能源風險從 Fed 反應函數裡移除。油價若再回 98 上方，長端損益平衡通膨與實質所得壓力會同時上升，Duration 部位就需要更嚴格的停損與分層。這條能源線也解釋為什麼油價應留在風險監控和市場驅動之間：它不是配置主軸，但會決定 Fed 與長端能否接受較低風險補償。

跨資產收盤也支持「折現率重估」而非單純風險偏好切換。S&P 500 跌 2.6%，Nasdaq Composite 跌 4.2%，Nasdaq 100 跌 4.8%，SOX 跌 10.3%，但 DXY 升 0.66%、黃金跌 3.1%。如果這是一場傳統避險交易，美元與黃金通常會更一致地反映避險需求；本次更像是實質利率和美元利差上升，壓縮長Duration現金流估值，並同步壓住 IG credit 的總報酬。這種組合對台灣投資人特別重要，因為海外債 ETF 的損益會同時承受美元資產價格、信用代理和台幣換算，不能只看單一市場。

因此，06/05 close 對台灣債券型 ETF 的第一層解讀是：中短天期利率風險重新升高，長天期仍受期限溢價約束，信用與股市長Duration資產同步承壓。若只看 10Y 4.55% 的絕對殖利率，會低估 CPI 與 Fed 語句帶來的前端風險；若只看 USD/TWD 31.475 對台幣 NAV 有利，會低估 UST 價格和 LQD 下跌可能抵銷匯兌助益。所以第一層結論應是「收益率更高但確認不足」：殖利率改善提供觀察價值，但 CPI、長端與信用仍未形成同向確認。

定價邏輯

二、美債走勢解讀

Fed 定價已從等降息轉為測試抗通膨可信度，中段比長端更適合作為主要觀察區。

利率定價的核心變化，是 Fed 反應函數從「等待降息條件」轉成「維持抗通膨可信度」。就業公布後，市場已把年底前 25bp 升息納入定價，10 月升息機率也被推到約六成。這種變化不代表 Fed 一定會升息，而是代表前端不能再把降息當作單向路徑；台灣 ETF 投資人若持有 5Y-10Y 或更長天期商品，必須把收益率提高與價格波動重新放在同一個框架評估。因此政策路徑必須拆成前端與中段兩條線：前端反映 Fed 尾端風險，中段承受重新定價，長端則另受供給和期限溢價牽制。

CPI 是下一個真正分水嶺。swap 隱含本周 CPI 約 4.3% y/y，若數字接近這個水準，10Y 4.55% 只是中繼點，市場會重新測試 4.65%，同時要求 Fed 在 6 月 16-17 日聲明中移除偏降息語氣。反過來說，若 CPI 低於 4.1%，非農衝擊缺乏通膨確認，前端與中段才有較高機率修復；這也是為什麼配置結論不應寫成全面買進，而應保留明確的確認條件。這個條件式寫法可以避免報告變得過度主觀；它不是預測單一 CPI 數字，而是說明不同結果如何改變 ETF 的風險報酬。

政策溝通的重要性也上升。Warsh 首場會議面對的是強就業、油價反彈、股市去槓桿與賣方預測分歧：Goldman 已移除今年降息預期，Citi 仍維持降息但變成少數派。當機構預測分散，市場會把每一句官方措辭轉成機率分布；2Y 是否站穩 4.15%、5Y 是否守住 4.30%，會比單日股市跌幅更能反映政策尾端風險。因此，Fed 定價與就業應被視為同一條政策機制：就業韌性抬高升息尾端風險，前端與中段才會比長端更快反映。

Curve 交易上，追空前端與追買長端都不乾淨。2s10s 已壓到 38bp，5s30s 已壓到 72bp，壓平交易已有一段利潤；但 30Y 還在 5.01%，若長端供給與期限溢價繼續黏著，過早買入 20Y+ 等於承擔另一種風險。較合理的做法是把 5Y-10Y 視為主要觀察區，長端只有在 30Y 重新跌破 4.95% 且油價回落時才提高信心。Curve 資訊需要直接連到配置含義：中段承擔的是政策尾端風險，長端承擔的是期限溢價與供給風險。

長端的問題不是單一事件，而是多個風險補償同時存在。財政供給、長天期發行吸收能力、MBS 避險調整、油價通膨溢價，以及 AI 融資需求都會影響 30Y 是否能穩定回到 5% 下方。這些因素不一定每天都主導價格，但當科技股大跌仍無法拉低 30Y 時，就表示長端投資人仍要求很高的讓利；長天期 ETF 的 NAV 修復速度因此會慢於直覺。這些來源不一定每天成為主因，但會決定長天期 ETF 的左尾風險；若被壓縮成一句「長端承壓」，就會低估供給、油價與融資需求的共同壓力。

加拿大就業 +87.8k、失業率降至 6.6% 是輔助證據。它不是 UST 的核心 driver，但說明北美需求韌性不是美國單點故事，全球前端利率都在重新檢查「需求尚未冷卻」的風險。對台灣投資人而言，這會影響美元債 ETF 的兩個層面：一是全球投資級債的折現率，二是央行政策敘事是否能快速回到降息主軸。這條輔助證據應降階處理：它強化全球利率背景，但不應搶走美國就業與 CPI 的主導位置。

美元走強與黃金下跌也支持實質利率重新上行的解讀。DXY 升 0.66%，黃金跌 3.1%，代表市場不是單純買避險，而是在重新評估美元利差與實質收益。這對台幣計價海外債 ETF 有兩面效果：美元偏強有利台幣 NAV 換算，但實質利率上行會壓住 UST 與 IG bond 的美元價格；兩者不能互相抵銷後就直接得出正面結論，必須分別監控。因此 FX 對 NAV 的正向換算只能改善台幣報酬的一部分，不能被寫成抵銷所有利率風險，這是本輪報告必須修正的核心邏輯。

因此，利率策略不應把 06/05 close 解讀成「殖利率高了就全面提高曝險」。更好的框架是：先承認 Fed 尾端風險回來，承認長端期限溢價尚未解除，再用事件條件決定節奏。CPI 低於 4.1%、10Y 回到 4.45%-4.50%、Brent 低於 92，才是中段 Duration 風險報酬改善的組合；CPI 接近 4.3% 或 30Y 站上 5.05% 則要優先保留流動性。這種條件化策略能保持中立：不把單日高殖利率直接轉成強烈方向押注，而是把可驗證門檻列清楚，讓配置節奏可追蹤。

跨市驗證

三、其他市場動向

AI 融資、IG credit、亞洲 FX 與油價都在驗證同一件事：資金成本被重新抬高。

風險資產的訊號不是單純「股市跌」，而是長Duration現金流被折現率重新定價。Nasdaq 100 跌 4.8%、SOX 跌 10.3%，跌幅遠大於 Dow 的 1.3%，顯示市場先賣對利率最敏感的 AI 與半導體現金流。這種跌法對 FI 的含義不是「風險偏好消失所以買債」，而是高成長資產和長天期債券同時被較高折現率壓住，兩者不能被視為天然對沖。因此跨資產訊號應服務於 FI 判讀，而不是成為另一個股市摘要；重點是哪些資產正在驗證折現率上行。

AI 融資與 IPO 供給讓這個股市訊號延伸到信用市場。Apollo/Anthropic 350 億美元晶片融資、SpaceX 750 億美元 IPO 需求，以及大型資料中心與電力投資，都顯示 AI 題材需要持續吸收外部資本。當 5Y-10Y 殖利率重新上行，AI 不只是股市長Duration題材，也會變成私募信用、IG 替代供給與長端資金需求題材；這也是為什麼信用判讀不能只停在 LQD 漲跌；目前更關心 IG 美元債對台灣 ETF 的映射，而不是用 HY 或股市來代表整體信用。

LQD 是這次信用代理的關鍵觀察。LQD 收 108.17、日跌 0.62%，HYG 收 79.43、日跌 0.50%，代表 IG Duration 與 HY 風險曝險都承壓。IG 本身仍有收益吸引力，但當 LQD 與 UST 同跌時，不能把信用市場簡化成「穩定」或「不穩定」；真正要拆的是 Duration 損失、利差補償、一級市場品質與 ETF liquidity，尤其台灣投資人買到的是總報酬。LQD 的單日變化應連到信用量化條件，包括收益緩衝、利差、供給與流動性，而不是被解讀成單一方向訊號。

信用事件也提醒市場不要只看指數。Raizen 債務重組、AI 相關融資、以及日本企業槓桿上升，都是資金成本上升後的微觀壓力。這些事件不必然代表系統性信用風險，但會讓低品質或長天期信用在利率上行時缺乏保護。對美元債 ETF 配置而言，IG 可保留收益來源，但 HY 和弱主權需要更高風險補償，而不是跟著股市反彈就回到高曝險。這些微觀例子提供的是風險分布的尾端資訊，重點在於深化信用分層，而不是把單一事件誤讀成整體市場方向。

亞洲與日本訊號應放在跨市場驗證，而不是混入 Fed driver。Nikkei 期貨低於現貨、科技股承壓、BOJ 升息押注升高，會影響亞洲開盤風險承接與 JGB 對全球長端的外溢；但它不是 06/05 美債熊平的主因。正確排序是：美國就業和 CPI 決定 UST 第一層，油價和長端供給決定期限溢價，亞洲政策與 JGB 波動決定隔日是否形成第二波全球 Duration 壓力。因此這項訊號應被定位為隔日風險濾網，而不是取代美國就業、CPI 與 Fed 定價的核心利率邏輯。

Indonesia 與亞洲 FX 也是風險監控，而不是核心利率驅動。印尼股指距高點約 -39%、印尼盾年內約 -8% 並曾破 18,000，說明 EM 本地債需要更高實質殖利率與更可信的政策組合。這對台灣投資人有兩個用途：一是提醒 EM local 與亞洲信用不能只看收益率，二是當美元走強時，弱 FX 國家的信用曝險會放大，應與 UST 中段配置分開判斷。這也讓亞洲政策訊號的定位更清楚：它是隔日盤前的風險濾網，不是 06/05 美債收盤的主因，避免市場驅動再次混亂。

油價把跨資產與利率重新連在一起。周五 Brent 與 WTI 收低，理論上有利通膨溢價下降；但周末亞洲盤 Brent 反彈到約 95.60，表示地緣風險還沒有解除。對利率而言，油價不是只有商品題材，而會傳導到 CPI、實質所得、Fed 語句和長端通膨平衡。若 Brent 回 98 上方，即使股市偏弱，長端債券也未必能得到避險買盤。如果油價高於 98，同樣的 10Y 殖利率會變得比較危險；如果油價低於 92，則高殖利率比較像補償充分後的觀察機會。

美元與台幣則必須用產品角度拆解。CBC USD/TWD 收 31.475，較前一交易日升 0.015，對台幣計價美元債 ETF 的 NAV 換算是正向，因為底層資產是美元債券但基金用台幣計價；台幣越弱，換算後 NAV 越有支撐。不過同一天 10Y 上升 8bp、LQD 下跌 0.62%，代表匯兌助益可能被債券美元價格損失抵銷，不能把 DXY 或 USD/TWD 單獨當成配置結論。這段必須放在跨資產後半，因為它直接修正匯率與 NAV 的方向：台幣走弱有利換算，但不保證總報酬為正。

配置動作

四、配置含義

配置重點應從單一結論改成三段歸因：UST、credit、USD/TWD，並讓每個觸發連到具體 ETF 風險。

Duration 配置的結論是中性偏多，但主要觀察區應放在 5Y-10Y。10Y 4.55% 已比前一周高，提供較好的進場殖利率；可是非農驚喜、CPI swap 4.3% 與 Fed 語句風險都表示現在不是一次拉滿部位的環境。若投資人用台灣上市美債 ETF 表達，應先確認商品實際存續期間與追蹤指數，避免用看似中天期的收益率承擔接近長天期的價格波動。這比單純說「中性偏多」更可執行，因為它把產品存續期間、事件風險和進場區間連在同一個風險報酬框架。

長天期美債 ETF 仍不適合追價。30Y 5.01% 看似提供高收益，但它沒有在股市大跌時明顯修復，代表長端買盤尚未完全回來。若 30Y 站上 5.05%，20Y+ ETF 的價格風險會再度放大；若 30Y 回到 4.95% 下方並且 Brent 低於 92，才比較能說期限溢價和通膨溢價同時降溫。這是價格風險管理，不是對長債長期收益率的否定。長端的核心不是收益率絕對高低，而是它有沒有在該反彈的時候反彈；若沒有，代表市場仍在要求額外補償。

Curve 配置要避免追逐已經走了一段的壓平。2s10s 38bp、5s30s 72bp 已反映相當多前端重定價，繼續追壓平需要 CPI 或 Fed 語句再給確認。比較好的做法是中段超配、長端小幅低配，保留 income 與下滑收益，同時降低 30Y 受供給與期限溢價重新上行的傷害。若後續 CPI 轉弱，Curve 反而可能先出現中段修復，而不是長端立即大幅走強。配置結論與機制應分層處理：先給出部位方向，再解釋Curve位置為什麼會改變風險來源。

信用配置應偏向 IG 收益與短中天期，不宜用 HY 追風險。LQD -0.62% 顯示 IG 也受 Duration 影響，但 IG 的票息與品質仍優於低品質信用；HYG -0.50% 則提醒 HY 不是 safe asset。對台灣美元債 ETF 投資人而言，重點不是硬解釋單日 LQD 下跌，而是確認 credit quantitative 條件：利差 cushion 是否足夠、primary supply 是否壓價格、ETF discount/liquidity 是否穩定。因此 LQD 應作為 IG 量化觀察指標，不應被硬解釋成單日漲跌故事；真正重要的是它是否和 UST 同跌。

台幣計價海外債 ETF 要把 FX 換算、UST 價格與信用利差拆開。USD/TWD 31.475 表示匯率換算助益增加；因底層持有美元債，台幣走弱時，換算後的台幣 NAV 通常得到支撐。同一天仍要另外扣除 UST 價格與 LQD 表現，因為債券美元價格下跌可能吃掉一部分匯兌利益。這也是先前邏輯必須修正的地方：台幣越弱通常有利台幣計價海外債 NAV，但它不是總報酬的全部，不能用匯率掩蓋利率與信用損失。這個三段歸因是台灣債券型 ETF 報告的基本框架，之後生成也應固定檢查，避免未來生成時再次寫錯匯率方向。

若投資人持有高配息或長天期 ETF，這次盤面要求降低「配息等於總報酬」的想像。殖利率提高確實改善未來收益，但短期價格損失會先反映在 NAV；若利率上行速度快於收益累積，高配息也無法立即保護本金。比較務實的評估方式是把配息率、存續期間、指數殖利率、匯率換算與信用曝險同時列出，不把單一高配息當作防守訊號。這個拆解比單純列配息率更有用，因為它說明哪一些收益看得到、哪一些價格損失會先發生，避免被表面殖利率誤導。

EM 與亞洲美元債要以政策可信度篩選。Indonesia 的 FX 防守、日本企業槓桿與亞洲科技股壓力，都不是 UST 主軸，但會影響隔日亞洲信用與主權利差。若 USD/IDR 仍在 18,000 上方且政策回應低於市場期待，EM local 與弱信用需要更高收益補償；若政策能穩定 FX，才有助於亞洲信用曝險。這部分應留在風險監控，不應和 Fed/CPI 放在同一張驅動卡。這樣安排也能降低報告噪音：EM 政策只在影響亞洲盤風險承接時才進入決策，不再和美國核心利率驅動混在一起。

整體配置語氣應是準備與條件確認，而不是全面提高風險。資料支持中段殖利率較有吸引力，也支持 FX 對台幣 NAV 的短線助益；但 CPI、油價、30Y 與 LQD 都還沒有給出同一方向的確認。若四者同時改善，可以把 5Y-10Y 從觀察轉成較積極配置；若任一項惡化，優先保留流動性與高品質收益，避免用單一便宜殖利率解釋所有 ETF。最終，配置重點應該像風險預案而不是結論堆疊；每一個判斷都要能連到可驗證條件與具體資產部位。

交易觸發

五、後續觀察重點

後續觀察不只是事件清單，而是每個觸發都對應一個部位決策。

第一個觸發是 CPI。若 6 月 10 日 CPI 接近 swap 隱含的 4.3%，前端會重新測試升息尾端風險，10Y 可能往 4.65% 靠近，5Y-10Y 部位應維持觀望；若 CPI 低於 4.1%，非農衝擊缺乏通膨確認，中段 Duration 才有更好的修復條件。這個觸發對所有美債 ETF 都重要，但對存續期間集中在 5Y-10Y 的商品最直接。因此，CPI 不是孤立事件，而是直接決定 5Y-10Y 與長天期 ETF 風險分層的核心觸發。

第二個觸發是 FOMC 語句。6 月 16-17 日會議若移除降息傾向或強調抗通膨可信度，2Y 與 5Y 仍會比 30Y 更敏感，Curve 可能繼續維持熊平；若聲明保留雙向風險並淡化升息選項，市場才會重新給中段 Duration 修復空間。這裡要看的是措辭如何改變市場對下一步政策的分布，而不是單純看會議是否按兵不動。單列會議日期不足以形成交易判斷；真正需要追蹤的是語句變化如何傳導到 2Y、5Y 與 Curve。

第三個觸發是 10Y 與 30Y 價位。10Y 下破 4.45% 才代表買盤真正接手，4.55%-4.65% 則是風險管理區；30Y 若站上 5.05%，代表期限溢價和供給壓力重新主導，20Y+ ETF 需降低價格彈性假設。若 10Y 回落但 30Y 仍高於 5%，表示市場只修復中段，長端仍不適合被視為乾淨的避險資產。這個價位框架能避免只看 10Y 一個數字；長端是否同步確認，才是判斷 20Y+ ETF 能不能修復的關鍵。

第四個觸發是油價。Brent 92 下方有助通膨溢價降溫，也能讓 Fed 語句比較容易保持彈性；Brent 98 上方則會重新推升能源通膨、實質所得壓力與長端損益平衡通膨。油價不是獨立商品訊號，而是決定 CPI 與長端修復勝率的前置變數。對 ETF 配置而言，油價走低才會讓高殖利率更像機會，油價上衝則讓高殖利率更像風險補償。因此油價訊號不應只被當作商品價格，而要說清楚它如何影響 CPI、Fed 溝通與長端通膨平衡。

第五個觸發是 LQD 與 HYG。LQD 守住 108 且 HYG 守住 79，表示信用代理雖受利率壓力但沒有失控，可以保留 IG 收益；若兩者同步跌破，代表利率與利差同時傷害美元債 ETF，應降低信用曝險。LQD 的用途不是預測每一天信用利差，而是提供 IG 總報酬是否能承受高利率環境的量化檢查。若未來有更完整利差或 CDX 資料，可優先取代 ETF 代理指標；在目前資料條件下，LQD 是較貼近 IG 需求的可交易檢查點。

第六個觸發是 USD/TWD。31.40 下方代表台幣走強、FX 換算助益減弱，台幣計價海外債 ETF 會更依賴 UST 與信用表現；31.55 上方則表示匯率換算助益增加。若台幣走弱同時伴隨油價與 Fed 壓力，底層債券美元價格仍可能下跌，所以需要分開看 FX、UST 與信用三條損益。這裡必須使用台灣央行銀行間收盤或台北外匯經紀公司境內收盤匯率，離岸報價只能當盤中參考。這一點也要寫進未來生成規則：USD/TWD 優先使用台灣央行銀行間收盤或台北外匯經紀境內收盤匯率，避免被離岸報價帶偏。

第七個觸發是亞洲政策防守。Indonesia 若能讓 USD/IDR 回到 18,000 下方並提供可信政策組合，EM 風險可穩定；若政策低於市場期待，EM 本地債與亞洲信用利差會需要更高補償。這一項不應取代美國 CPI 與 Fed 的主軸，但可以幫助判斷隔日亞洲盤是否把 UST 壓力擴散成 EM、FX 與 credit 的連鎖反應。這樣亞洲訊號能保留資訊價值，同時避免把所有央行事件混成同一條利率驅動。

最後要看 AI 融資與科技股是否止血。若 SOX 回到 12,800 上方、NDX 反彈且 10Y 同時低於 4.50%，代表折現率壓力緩和，可以降低對信用與成長股去槓桿的擔憂；若 SOX 失守 12,000 且 LQD 續跌，AI 就不只是 growth story，而是資金需求、信用供給與長端資金吸收的共同壓力點。這能把股市訊號重新映射到 FI 配置，而不是只當跨資產摘要。這條傳導能區分兩種情境：科技股壓力若只是股市波動，對 FI 的影響有限；若同時伴隨 LQD 下跌與 10Y 高位，則會變成信用與資金成本問題。

一、UST 收盤與 Curve 變化美債Curve是就業與通膨尾端風險重新定價，不是單純股市下跌帶來避險買債。

6 月 5 日美債收盤的核心不是「風險資產大跌所以買債」，而是就業數據把 Fed 政策路徑重新往右推。官方 CMT 顯示 2Y 收 4.17%、日升 12bp，5Y 收 4.29%、日升 11bp，10Y 收 4.55%、日升 8bp，20Y 與 30Y 分別在 5.03%、5.01%。這種前端與中段升幅大於長端的形狀，表示市場先重新定價升息尾端風險，再要求長端維持更高期限溢價；對台灣債券型 ETF 而言，這比單看 10Y 絕對殖利率更重要。因此，前端政策溢價回到盤面本身就是配置訊號；若只看價格變化，容易把較高殖利率誤判成已經具備足夠補償。

Curve 型態確認這是熊平而非單純長端拋售。2s10s 收 38bp、日內壓平 4bp，5s30s 收 72bp、日內壓平 7bp，10s30s 收 46bp、日內壓平 4bp。若只是風險資產下跌帶來避險需求，長端應該更容易下行；但實際上長端仍守在 5% 附近，說明長端買盤沒有取得主導權。這會讓 20Y+ ETF 的價格彈性受限，也讓中段 Duration 的風險報酬比相對更乾淨。因此熱圖裡的Curve列要被解讀為風險來源，而不是單純的數字摘要；壓平來自政策路徑，不是長端便宜後自動反彈。

非農 +172k 明顯高於約 +80k 共識，失業率維持 4.3%，4 月就業還上修到 +179k。這組數字讓市場很難繼續用「勞動市場快速轉弱」來定價降息，尤其 2Y 與 5Y 對 Fed 路徑最敏感。平均時薪年增 3.4% 本身不是失控，但它不足以抵銷就業驚喜與油價風險，因此前端重新定價不是單一資料點反應，而是把降息假設重新交給 CPI 與 FOMC 語句驗證。這也是市場驅動需要把就業與 Fed 定價放在同一群組、而不是和其他央行混在一起的原因，因為這一組才是當天 UST 的主因。

從一周與一個月變化看，這不是一天噪音。2Y 周升 19bp、月升 24bp，5Y 周升 16bp、月升 21bp，10Y 周升 10bp、月升 12bp；Curve 2s10s 月內壓平 12bp，5s30s 月內壓平 18bp。這些累積變化代表市場已從「何時降息」轉向「是否需要保留升息選項」。如果後續 CPI 接近 swap 隱含的 4.3%，這條重新定價鏈會延伸；若 CPI 低於 4.1%，才比較有條件把這次前端 selloff 視為過度反應。對 ETF 決策而言，這代表今天的關鍵不是找最低點，而是確認下一個資料是否推翻升息尾端風險，讓收益率提高轉成可承擔的風險補償。

長端沒有真正受惠於股市下跌，這是本日盤面的重要訊號。30Y 收 5.01%，20Y 收 5.03%，即使 Nasdaq 100 與 SOX 大跌，長端仍未出現乾淨的避險買盤。對台灣長天期美債 ETF 來說，這意味著「股市跌」不等於「長債 NAV 會修復」；長端需要同時看到供給壓力緩和、油價回落、CPI 不再上衝，以及 30Y 重新跌回 5% 下方，價格彈性才會回到比較有利的位置。換句話說，長端評估不能只看 5% 附近的殖利率，還要同步觀察 30Y 是否跌回 5% 下方，才有辦法判斷避險買盤是否有效。

油價與地緣風險是長端不能快速修復的第二條線。Brent 周五收 93.09、日跌 2.0%，WTI 收 90.54、日跌 2.7%，表面上有助通膨溢價降溫；但周末亞洲盤 Brent 反彈到約 95.60，表示市場還不能把能源風險從 Fed 反應函數裡移除。油價若再回 98 上方，長端損益平衡通膨與實質所得壓力會同時上升，Duration 部位就需要更嚴格的停損與分層。這條能源線也解釋為什麼油價應留在風險監控和市場驅動之間：它不是配置主軸，但會決定 Fed 與長端能否接受較低風險補償。

跨資產收盤也支持「折現率重估」而非單純風險偏好切換。S&P 500 跌 2.6%，Nasdaq Composite 跌 4.2%，Nasdaq 100 跌 4.8%，SOX 跌 10.3%，但 DXY 升 0.66%、黃金跌 3.1%。如果這是一場傳統避險交易，美元與黃金通常會更一致地反映避險需求；本次更像是實質利率和美元利差上升，壓縮長Duration現金流估值，並同步壓住 IG credit 的總報酬。這種組合對台灣投資人特別重要，因為海外債 ETF 的損益會同時承受美元資產價格、信用代理和台幣換算，不能只看單一市場。

因此，06/05 close 對台灣債券型 ETF 的第一層解讀是：中短天期利率風險重新升高，長天期仍受期限溢價約束，信用與股市長Duration資產同步承壓。若只看 10Y 4.55% 的絕對殖利率，會低估 CPI 與 Fed 語句帶來的前端風險；若只看 USD/TWD 31.475 對台幣 NAV 有利，會低估 UST 價格和 LQD 下跌可能抵銷匯兌助益。所以第一層結論應是「收益率更高但確認不足」：殖利率改善提供觀察價值，但 CPI、長端與信用仍未形成同向確認。

二、美債走勢解讀Fed 定價已從等降息轉為測試抗通膨可信度，中段比長端更適合作為主要觀察區。

利率定價的核心變化，是 Fed 反應函數從「等待降息條件」轉成「維持抗通膨可信度」。就業公布後，市場已把年底前 25bp 升息納入定價，10 月升息機率也被推到約六成。這種變化不代表 Fed 一定會升息，而是代表前端不能再把降息當作單向路徑；台灣 ETF 投資人若持有 5Y-10Y 或更長天期商品，必須把收益率提高與價格波動重新放在同一個框架評估。因此政策路徑必須拆成前端與中段兩條線：前端反映 Fed 尾端風險，中段承受重新定價，長端則另受供給和期限溢價牽制。

CPI 是下一個真正分水嶺。swap 隱含本周 CPI 約 4.3% y/y，若數字接近這個水準，10Y 4.55% 只是中繼點，市場會重新測試 4.65%，同時要求 Fed 在 6 月 16-17 日聲明中移除偏降息語氣。反過來說，若 CPI 低於 4.1%，非農衝擊缺乏通膨確認，前端與中段才有較高機率修復；這也是為什麼配置結論不應寫成全面買進，而應保留明確的確認條件。這個條件式寫法可以避免報告變得過度主觀；它不是預測單一 CPI 數字，而是說明不同結果如何改變 ETF 的風險報酬。

政策溝通的重要性也上升。Warsh 首場會議面對的是強就業、油價反彈、股市去槓桿與賣方預測分歧：Goldman 已移除今年降息預期，Citi 仍維持降息但變成少數派。當機構預測分散，市場會把每一句官方措辭轉成機率分布；2Y 是否站穩 4.15%、5Y 是否守住 4.30%，會比單日股市跌幅更能反映政策尾端風險。因此，Fed 定價與就業應被視為同一條政策機制：就業韌性抬高升息尾端風險，前端與中段才會比長端更快反映。

Curve 交易上，追空前端與追買長端都不乾淨。2s10s 已壓到 38bp，5s30s 已壓到 72bp，壓平交易已有一段利潤；但 30Y 還在 5.01%，若長端供給與期限溢價繼續黏著，過早買入 20Y+ 等於承擔另一種風險。較合理的做法是把 5Y-10Y 視為主要觀察區，長端只有在 30Y 重新跌破 4.95% 且油價回落時才提高信心。Curve 資訊需要直接連到配置含義：中段承擔的是政策尾端風險，長端承擔的是期限溢價與供給風險。

長端的問題不是單一事件，而是多個風險補償同時存在。財政供給、長天期發行吸收能力、MBS 避險調整、油價通膨溢價，以及 AI 融資需求都會影響 30Y 是否能穩定回到 5% 下方。這些因素不一定每天都主導價格，但當科技股大跌仍無法拉低 30Y 時，就表示長端投資人仍要求很高的讓利；長天期 ETF 的 NAV 修復速度因此會慢於直覺。這些來源不一定每天成為主因，但會決定長天期 ETF 的左尾風險；若被壓縮成一句「長端承壓」，就會低估供給、油價與融資需求的共同壓力。

加拿大就業 +87.8k、失業率降至 6.6% 是輔助證據。它不是 UST 的核心 driver，但說明北美需求韌性不是美國單點故事，全球前端利率都在重新檢查「需求尚未冷卻」的風險。對台灣投資人而言，這會影響美元債 ETF 的兩個層面：一是全球投資級債的折現率，二是央行政策敘事是否能快速回到降息主軸。這條輔助證據應降階處理：它強化全球利率背景，但不應搶走美國就業與 CPI 的主導位置。

美元走強與黃金下跌也支持實質利率重新上行的解讀。DXY 升 0.66%，黃金跌 3.1%，代表市場不是單純買避險，而是在重新評估美元利差與實質收益。這對台幣計價海外債 ETF 有兩面效果：美元偏強有利台幣 NAV 換算，但實質利率上行會壓住 UST 與 IG bond 的美元價格；兩者不能互相抵銷後就直接得出正面結論，必須分別監控。因此 FX 對 NAV 的正向換算只能改善台幣報酬的一部分，不能被寫成抵銷所有利率風險，這是本輪報告必須修正的核心邏輯。

因此，利率策略不應把 06/05 close 解讀成「殖利率高了就全面提高曝險」。更好的框架是：先承認 Fed 尾端風險回來，承認長端期限溢價尚未解除，再用事件條件決定節奏。CPI 低於 4.1%、10Y 回到 4.45%-4.50%、Brent 低於 92，才是中段 Duration 風險報酬改善的組合；CPI 接近 4.3% 或 30Y 站上 5.05% 則要優先保留流動性。這種條件化策略能保持中立：不把單日高殖利率直接轉成強烈方向押注，而是把可驗證門檻列清楚，讓配置節奏可追蹤。

三、其他市場動向AI 融資、IG credit、亞洲 FX 與油價都在驗證同一件事：資金成本被重新抬高。

風險資產的訊號不是單純「股市跌」，而是長Duration現金流被折現率重新定價。Nasdaq 100 跌 4.8%、SOX 跌 10.3%，跌幅遠大於 Dow 的 1.3%，顯示市場先賣對利率最敏感的 AI 與半導體現金流。這種跌法對 FI 的含義不是「風險偏好消失所以買債」，而是高成長資產和長天期債券同時被較高折現率壓住，兩者不能被視為天然對沖。因此跨資產訊號應服務於 FI 判讀，而不是成為另一個股市摘要；重點是哪些資產正在驗證折現率上行。

AI 融資與 IPO 供給讓這個股市訊號延伸到信用市場。Apollo/Anthropic 350 億美元晶片融資、SpaceX 750 億美元 IPO 需求，以及大型資料中心與電力投資，都顯示 AI 題材需要持續吸收外部資本。當 5Y-10Y 殖利率重新上行，AI 不只是股市長Duration題材，也會變成私募信用、IG 替代供給與長端資金需求題材；這也是為什麼信用判讀不能只停在 LQD 漲跌；目前更關心 IG 美元債對台灣 ETF 的映射，而不是用 HY 或股市來代表整體信用。

LQD 是這次信用代理的關鍵觀察。LQD 收 108.17、日跌 0.62%，HYG 收 79.43、日跌 0.50%，代表 IG Duration 與 HY 風險曝險都承壓。IG 本身仍有收益吸引力，但當 LQD 與 UST 同跌時，不能把信用市場簡化成「穩定」或「不穩定」；真正要拆的是 Duration 損失、利差補償、一級市場品質與 ETF liquidity，尤其台灣投資人買到的是總報酬。LQD 的單日變化應連到信用量化條件，包括收益緩衝、利差、供給與流動性，而不是被解讀成單一方向訊號。

信用事件也提醒市場不要只看指數。Raizen 債務重組、AI 相關融資、以及日本企業槓桿上升，都是資金成本上升後的微觀壓力。這些事件不必然代表系統性信用風險，但會讓低品質或長天期信用在利率上行時缺乏保護。對美元債 ETF 配置而言，IG 可保留收益來源，但 HY 和弱主權需要更高風險補償，而不是跟著股市反彈就回到高曝險。這些微觀例子提供的是風險分布的尾端資訊，重點在於深化信用分層，而不是把單一事件誤讀成整體市場方向。

亞洲與日本訊號應放在跨市場驗證，而不是混入 Fed driver。Nikkei 期貨低於現貨、科技股承壓、BOJ 升息押注升高，會影響亞洲開盤風險承接與 JGB 對全球長端的外溢；但它不是 06/05 美債熊平的主因。正確排序是：美國就業和 CPI 決定 UST 第一層，油價和長端供給決定期限溢價，亞洲政策與 JGB 波動決定隔日是否形成第二波全球 Duration 壓力。因此這項訊號應被定位為隔日風險濾網，而不是取代美國就業、CPI 與 Fed 定價的核心利率邏輯。

Indonesia 與亞洲 FX 也是風險監控，而不是核心利率驅動。印尼股指距高點約 -39%、印尼盾年內約 -8% 並曾破 18,000，說明 EM 本地債需要更高實質殖利率與更可信的政策組合。這對台灣投資人有兩個用途：一是提醒 EM local 與亞洲信用不能只看收益率，二是當美元走強時，弱 FX 國家的信用曝險會放大，應與 UST 中段配置分開判斷。這也讓亞洲政策訊號的定位更清楚：它是隔日盤前的風險濾網，不是 06/05 美債收盤的主因，避免市場驅動再次混亂。

油價把跨資產與利率重新連在一起。周五 Brent 與 WTI 收低，理論上有利通膨溢價下降；但周末亞洲盤 Brent 反彈到約 95.60，表示地緣風險還沒有解除。對利率而言，油價不是只有商品題材，而會傳導到 CPI、實質所得、Fed 語句和長端通膨平衡。若 Brent 回 98 上方，即使股市偏弱，長端債券也未必能得到避險買盤。如果油價高於 98，同樣的 10Y 殖利率會變得比較危險；如果油價低於 92，則高殖利率比較像補償充分後的觀察機會。

美元與台幣則必須用產品角度拆解。CBC USD/TWD 收 31.475，較前一交易日升 0.015，對台幣計價美元債 ETF 的 NAV 換算是正向，因為底層資產是美元債券但基金用台幣計價；台幣越弱，換算後 NAV 越有支撐。不過同一天 10Y 上升 8bp、LQD 下跌 0.62%，代表匯兌助益可能被債券美元價格損失抵銷，不能把 DXY 或 USD/TWD 單獨當成配置結論。這段必須放在跨資產後半，因為它直接修正匯率與 NAV 的方向：台幣走弱有利換算，但不保證總報酬為正。

四、配置含義配置重點應從單一結論改成三段歸因：UST、credit、USD/TWD，並讓每個觸發連到具體 ETF 風險。

Duration 配置的結論是中性偏多，但主要觀察區應放在 5Y-10Y。10Y 4.55% 已比前一周高，提供較好的進場殖利率；可是非農驚喜、CPI swap 4.3% 與 Fed 語句風險都表示現在不是一次拉滿部位的環境。若投資人用台灣上市美債 ETF 表達，應先確認商品實際存續期間與追蹤指數，避免用看似中天期的收益率承擔接近長天期的價格波動。這比單純說「中性偏多」更可執行，因為它把產品存續期間、事件風險和進場區間連在同一個風險報酬框架。

長天期美債 ETF 仍不適合追價。30Y 5.01% 看似提供高收益，但它沒有在股市大跌時明顯修復，代表長端買盤尚未完全回來。若 30Y 站上 5.05%，20Y+ ETF 的價格風險會再度放大；若 30Y 回到 4.95% 下方並且 Brent 低於 92，才比較能說期限溢價和通膨溢價同時降溫。這是價格風險管理，不是對長債長期收益率的否定。長端的核心不是收益率絕對高低，而是它有沒有在該反彈的時候反彈；若沒有，代表市場仍在要求額外補償。

Curve 配置要避免追逐已經走了一段的壓平。2s10s 38bp、5s30s 72bp 已反映相當多前端重定價，繼續追壓平需要 CPI 或 Fed 語句再給確認。比較好的做法是中段超配、長端小幅低配，保留 income 與下滑收益，同時降低 30Y 受供給與期限溢價重新上行的傷害。若後續 CPI 轉弱，Curve 反而可能先出現中段修復，而不是長端立即大幅走強。配置結論與機制應分層處理：先給出部位方向，再解釋Curve位置為什麼會改變風險來源。

信用配置應偏向 IG 收益與短中天期，不宜用 HY 追風險。LQD -0.62% 顯示 IG 也受 Duration 影響，但 IG 的票息與品質仍優於低品質信用；HYG -0.50% 則提醒 HY 不是 safe asset。對台灣美元債 ETF 投資人而言，重點不是硬解釋單日 LQD 下跌，而是確認 credit quantitative 條件：利差 cushion 是否足夠、primary supply 是否壓價格、ETF discount/liquidity 是否穩定。因此 LQD 應作為 IG 量化觀察指標，不應被硬解釋成單日漲跌故事；真正重要的是它是否和 UST 同跌。

台幣計價海外債 ETF 要把 FX 換算、UST 價格與信用利差拆開。USD/TWD 31.475 表示匯率換算助益增加；因底層持有美元債，台幣走弱時，換算後的台幣 NAV 通常得到支撐。同一天仍要另外扣除 UST 價格與 LQD 表現，因為債券美元價格下跌可能吃掉一部分匯兌利益。這也是先前邏輯必須修正的地方：台幣越弱通常有利台幣計價海外債 NAV，但它不是總報酬的全部，不能用匯率掩蓋利率與信用損失。這個三段歸因是台灣債券型 ETF 報告的基本框架，之後生成也應固定檢查，避免未來生成時再次寫錯匯率方向。

若投資人持有高配息或長天期 ETF，這次盤面要求降低「配息等於總報酬」的想像。殖利率提高確實改善未來收益，但短期價格損失會先反映在 NAV；若利率上行速度快於收益累積，高配息也無法立即保護本金。比較務實的評估方式是把配息率、存續期間、指數殖利率、匯率換算與信用曝險同時列出，不把單一高配息當作防守訊號。這個拆解比單純列配息率更有用，因為它說明哪一些收益看得到、哪一些價格損失會先發生，避免被表面殖利率誤導。

EM 與亞洲美元債要以政策可信度篩選。Indonesia 的 FX 防守、日本企業槓桿與亞洲科技股壓力，都不是 UST 主軸，但會影響隔日亞洲信用與主權利差。若 USD/IDR 仍在 18,000 上方且政策回應低於市場期待，EM local 與弱信用需要更高收益補償；若政策能穩定 FX，才有助於亞洲信用曝險。這部分應留在風險監控，不應和 Fed/CPI 放在同一張驅動卡。這樣安排也能降低報告噪音：EM 政策只在影響亞洲盤風險承接時才進入決策，不再和美國核心利率驅動混在一起。

整體配置語氣應是準備與條件確認，而不是全面提高風險。資料支持中段殖利率較有吸引力，也支持 FX 對台幣 NAV 的短線助益；但 CPI、油價、30Y 與 LQD 都還沒有給出同一方向的確認。若四者同時改善，可以把 5Y-10Y 從觀察轉成較積極配置；若任一項惡化，優先保留流動性與高品質收益，避免用單一便宜殖利率解釋所有 ETF。最終，配置重點應該像風險預案而不是結論堆疊；每一個判斷都要能連到可驗證條件與具體資產部位。

五、後續觀察重點後續觀察不只是事件清單，而是每個觸發都對應一個部位決策。

第一個觸發是 CPI。若 6 月 10 日 CPI 接近 swap 隱含的 4.3%，前端會重新測試升息尾端風險，10Y 可能往 4.65% 靠近，5Y-10Y 部位應維持觀望；若 CPI 低於 4.1%，非農衝擊缺乏通膨確認，中段 Duration 才有更好的修復條件。這個觸發對所有美債 ETF 都重要，但對存續期間集中在 5Y-10Y 的商品最直接。因此，CPI 不是孤立事件，而是直接決定 5Y-10Y 與長天期 ETF 風險分層的核心觸發。

第二個觸發是 FOMC 語句。6 月 16-17 日會議若移除降息傾向或強調抗通膨可信度，2Y 與 5Y 仍會比 30Y 更敏感，Curve 可能繼續維持熊平；若聲明保留雙向風險並淡化升息選項，市場才會重新給中段 Duration 修復空間。這裡要看的是措辭如何改變市場對下一步政策的分布，而不是單純看會議是否按兵不動。單列會議日期不足以形成交易判斷；真正需要追蹤的是語句變化如何傳導到 2Y、5Y 與 Curve。

第三個觸發是 10Y 與 30Y 價位。10Y 下破 4.45% 才代表買盤真正接手，4.55%-4.65% 則是風險管理區；30Y 若站上 5.05%，代表期限溢價和供給壓力重新主導，20Y+ ETF 需降低價格彈性假設。若 10Y 回落但 30Y 仍高於 5%，表示市場只修復中段，長端仍不適合被視為乾淨的避險資產。這個價位框架能避免只看 10Y 一個數字；長端是否同步確認，才是判斷 20Y+ ETF 能不能修復的關鍵。

第四個觸發是油價。Brent 92 下方有助通膨溢價降溫，也能讓 Fed 語句比較容易保持彈性；Brent 98 上方則會重新推升能源通膨、實質所得壓力與長端損益平衡通膨。油價不是獨立商品訊號，而是決定 CPI 與長端修復勝率的前置變數。對 ETF 配置而言，油價走低才會讓高殖利率更像機會，油價上衝則讓高殖利率更像風險補償。因此油價訊號不應只被當作商品價格，而要說清楚它如何影響 CPI、Fed 溝通與長端通膨平衡。

第五個觸發是 LQD 與 HYG。LQD 守住 108 且 HYG 守住 79，表示信用代理雖受利率壓力但沒有失控，可以保留 IG 收益；若兩者同步跌破，代表利率與利差同時傷害美元債 ETF，應降低信用曝險。LQD 的用途不是預測每一天信用利差，而是提供 IG 總報酬是否能承受高利率環境的量化檢查。若未來有更完整利差或 CDX 資料，可優先取代 ETF 代理指標；在目前資料條件下，LQD 是較貼近 IG 需求的可交易檢查點。

第六個觸發是 USD/TWD。31.40 下方代表台幣走強、FX 換算助益減弱，台幣計價海外債 ETF 會更依賴 UST 與信用表現；31.55 上方則表示匯率換算助益增加。若台幣走弱同時伴隨油價與 Fed 壓力，底層債券美元價格仍可能下跌，所以需要分開看 FX、UST 與信用三條損益。這裡必須使用台灣央行銀行間收盤或台北外匯經紀公司境內收盤匯率，離岸報價只能當盤中參考。這一點也要寫進未來生成規則：USD/TWD 優先使用台灣央行銀行間收盤或台北外匯經紀境內收盤匯率，避免被離岸報價帶偏。

第七個觸發是亞洲政策防守。Indonesia 若能讓 USD/IDR 回到 18,000 下方並提供可信政策組合，EM 風險可穩定；若政策低於市場期待，EM 本地債與亞洲信用利差會需要更高補償。這一項不應取代美國 CPI 與 Fed 的主軸，但可以幫助判斷隔日亞洲盤是否把 UST 壓力擴散成 EM、FX 與 credit 的連鎖反應。這樣亞洲訊號能保留資訊價值，同時避免把所有央行事件混成同一條利率驅動。

最後要看 AI 融資與科技股是否止血。若 SOX 回到 12,800 上方、NDX 反彈且 10Y 同時低於 4.50%，代表折現率壓力緩和，可以降低對信用與成長股去槓桿的擔憂；若 SOX 失守 12,000 且 LQD 續跌，AI 就不只是 growth story，而是資金需求、信用供給與長端資金吸收的共同壓力點。這能把股市訊號重新映射到 FI 配置，而不是只當跨資產摘要。這條傳導能區分兩種情境：科技股壓力若只是股市波動，對 FI 的影響有限；若同時伴隨 LQD 下跌與 10Y 高位，則會變成信用與資金成本問題。

Rates Close

1. Treasury Close and Curve Move

Payrolls pushed hike-tail risk back into the front end; the curve bear-flattened rather than rallying on risk aversion.

The June 5 Treasury close was not a simple equities-down, bonds-up session. Payrolls pushed the Fed path to the right. Official CMT marks put the 2Y at 4.17%, up 12bp, the 5Y at 4.29%, up 11bp, the 10Y at 4.55%, up 8bp, and the 20Y/30Y at 5.03% and 5.01%. The front end and belly rose more than the long end, showing that the market repriced hike-tail risk first and then kept long-end compensation elevated through supply and inflation risk.

The curve confirms bear flattening rather than a clean long-end selloff. The 2s10s closed at 38bp, 4bp flatter on the day; the 5s30s closed at 72bp, 7bp flatter; and the 10s30s closed at 46bp, 4bp flatter. If this had been only a risk-off bond rally, the 10Y and 30Y should have fallen more easily. Instead, the 30Y stayed above 5%, which means inflation, supply and long-end risk premium still matter for long-duration assets.

Payrolls were the first-order shock. Nonfarm payrolls rose 172k versus roughly 80k consensus, unemployment held at 4.3%, and April payrolls were revised up to 179k. That makes it hard for the market to keep pricing rapid labor-market deterioration as the basis for cuts. Wage growth at 3.4% y/y was not disorderly, but with oil and CPI risk still open, it is not enough to let the Fed move back to an explicitly dovish stance.

The move is not one-day noise when viewed over a week and a month. The 2Y is up 19bp on the week and 24bp on the month, while the 5Y is up 16bp and 21bp. The 2s10s has flattened 12bp over the month, and the 5s30s has flattened 18bp. That structure says the market has moved from debating the timing of cuts to debating whether the Fed needs to preserve optionality to hike again.

The long end did not receive much protection from the equity selloff, and that is the key signal. The 30Y at 5.01% and the 20Y at 5.03% failed to rally cleanly even as the Nasdaq 100 and SOX sold off heavily. For Taiwan-listed long Treasury ETFs, that means a weaker equity tape alone is not enough to assume NAV repair. The long end needs a break below 5%, lower oil, and less supply pressure.

Oil is the second constraint on long-end repair. Brent and WTI fell on the U.S. close, but Brent rebounded toward 95.60 in early Asia trading after renewed geopolitical stress. That keeps the inflation-premium channel alive. The 10Y at 4.55% should not be treated as a safe high if CPI comes in near the roughly 4.3% level implied by swaps.

Cross-asset closes also point to discount-rate repricing rather than a plain risk-off rotation. The S&P 500 fell 2.6%, the Nasdaq Composite fell 4.2%, the Nasdaq 100 fell 4.8%, and the SOX fell 10.3%, while DXY rose to 100.07 and gold fell 3.1%. The market was not only hiding in safe assets; it was demanding a higher discount rate for long-duration cash flows.

The first read for Taiwan bond ETF allocation is therefore clear: front-end and belly rates risk is back, the long end is still constrained by term-premium and supply pressure, and credit plus equity-duration assets are under pressure at the same time. Looking only at the absolute 10Y yield would miss the CPI/Fed risk in the 2Y-5Y area; looking only at the tech selloff would overstate the protection for 20Y-plus exposure.

Rates Interpretation

2. Rates Interpretation

Fed pricing has shifted from waiting for cuts to testing anti-inflation credibility; the belly is more actionable than the long end.

The key change in rates pricing is the Fed reaction function. The market has moved from waiting for cut conditions to testing whether the Fed must defend inflation credibility. After payrolls, year-end hike optionality was priced back in, with October hike odds pushed toward roughly 60%. That does not mean a hike is certain, but it means the front end can no longer price a one-way path toward cuts.

CPI is the next dividing line. CPI swaps imply roughly 4.3% y/y for the upcoming release. If the print is close to that level, the 10Y at 4.55% is not a ceiling and the market can test 4.65%, while the June FOMC statement would likely need to remove cut-friendly language. If CPI is below 4.1%, the payroll shock can be treated as an overreaction in the front end and the belly can repair more cleanly.

Policy communication is now more important. Warsh’s first meeting has to face strong payrolls, an oil rebound, equity deleveraging and a split economist community: Goldman has removed cuts for this year while Citi remains a dovish outlier. When forecasts are dispersed, every official phrase becomes an input to the probability distribution. That is why the 2Y break above 4.15% matters more than the 30Y day move.

Curve trades are not clean on either side. The 2s10s at 38bp and 5s30s at 72bp already reflect a lot of flattening. Chasing that flattening requires another CPI or Fed-language confirmation. At the same time, buying a long-end steepener too early is risky because the 30Y remains above 5%. The cleaner expression is staged 5Y-10Y exposure while keeping a small underweight in the long end.

The long-end issue is not one event. Fiscal supply, long-dated issuance absorption, mortgage hedging, oil-linked inflation premium and AI funding demand all affect whether the 30Y can stay below 5%. These channels do not dominate every day, but when a major equity selloff fails to pull the 30Y lower, it shows that long-end buyers still require a high yield to step in.

Canada employment is supporting evidence, not the main driver. Canada added 87.8k jobs and the unemployment rate fell to 6.6%, reinforcing that North American demand resilience is not purely a U.S. data point. For Taiwan investors, this lowers the probability of a quick-cut narrative and makes short-to-intermediate income more attractive than simply extending duration.

The dollar and gold also support the real-yield interpretation. DXY rose 0.66% and gold fell 3.1%, which means the market was repricing real returns and dollar yield advantage, not only buying havens. For TWD-denominated overseas bond ETFs, the stronger dollar helps FX translation, but higher real yields and weaker credit proxies pressure the underlying USD bond prices.

The strategy conclusion is not to add broadly just because yields are higher. The correct framework is to acknowledge that Fed-tail risk is back, that long-end term premium has not disappeared, and that exposure should be conditional. CPI below 4.1%, the 10Y back toward 4.45%-4.50%, and Brent below 92 would support adding belly duration; CPI near 4.3% or the 30Y above 5.05% would argue for pausing.

Cross-Market Read

3. Cross-Market Read

AI, IG credit, Asia FX and oil all confirm the same message: the cost of capital has repriced higher.

The equity signal is not simply that stocks fell. The long-duration equity complex was repriced. The Nasdaq 100 fell 4.8% and the SOX fell 10.3%, far worse than the Dow’s 1.3% decline, showing that AI and semiconductors were the first assets hit by a higher discount rate. That lines up with the move in the Treasury front end.

AI funding turns this from an equity story into a credit story. Apollo/Anthropic chip financing, SpaceX IPO demand and large data-center or power investments all show that the AI narrative needs large pools of external capital. When 5Y-10Y yields move higher, that affects not only equity multiples but also IG substitute supply, private-credit structures and long-dated credit spreads.

LQD is the important credit proxy for this tape. LQD closed at 108.17, down 0.62%, while HYG fell 0.50% to 79.43. IG duration and HY beta were both under pressure. IG still offers income, but when it falls together with Treasuries, credit cannot be summarized as stable risk appetite. It has to be decomposed into duration, spread compensation, primary quality and ETF liquidity.

Credit events also argue against reading only the index. Raizen restructuring, AI-linked financing and rising leverage in corporate Japan are micro examples of what higher funding costs can do. These do not imply systemic credit stress, but they reduce the cushion for weak or long-duration credit. Taiwan bond ETF allocation should preserve IG income while demanding more compensation for HY and EM exposure.

Japan and Asia belong in cross-market validation rather than the core Fed driver. Weak Nikkei futures, pressure on tech shares and higher BOJ-hike expectations affect Asia open risk and potential JGB spillover into global duration. They are not the main reason USTs bear-flattened on June 5. The correct read is that U.S. front-end repricing is the primary driver, while Japan and Asia determine whether a second global-duration wave follows.

Indonesia and Asia FX are risk-monitor items, not core rates drivers. Indonesian equities are far below their highs and the rupiah is down around 8% year to date after breaching 18,000. That matters for EM and Asia credit exposure because local bonds need higher real yields and stronger policy credibility. It should be watched for EM beta, not treated as the same driver as U.S. payrolls and CPI.

Oil reconnects cross-asset and rates. The U.S. close showed lower Brent and WTI, which would normally help inflation premium. But Brent’s rebound toward 95.60 in early Asia means geopolitics is still active. For rates, oil is not a separate commodity theme; it feeds CPI, real incomes, Fed language and long-end breakeven pressure. Brent back above 98 would make duration repair harder.

USD/TWD must be interpreted through product mechanics. The CBC USD/TWD close at 31.475 is positive for the TWD NAV translation of USD bond ETFs. But the same day also saw the 10Y up 8bp and LQD down 0.62%. The cross-asset conclusion is therefore not that a stronger dollar is automatically bullish for ETFs; FX helps, while rates and credit can offset it.

Allocation Read

4. Allocation Read

Allocation should separate belly duration, IG income and FX translation instead of forcing one broad risk call.

The duration stance is neutral-to-slightly long, but only through staged 5Y-10Y exposure. The 10Y at 4.55% is more attractive than a week ago, but payrolls, a 4.3% CPI-swap setup and Fed statement risk argue against buying the full position at once. The first tranche can be observed around 4.55%-4.60%; the second requires CPI below 4.1% or a move back toward 4.45%-4.50%.

Long Treasury ETFs should not be chased. The 30Y at 5.01% offers yield, but it did not rally meaningfully during the equity selloff. If the 30Y breaks above 5.05%, price risk in 20Y-plus ETFs rises again. A move below 4.95% alongside Brent below 92 would be a cleaner sign that term premium and inflation premium are both cooling.

Curve allocation should avoid chasing a move that has already happened. The 2s10s at 38bp and 5s30s at 72bp already reflect a significant amount of front-end repricing. Further flattening needs CPI or Fed confirmation. A better expression is belly overweight, small long-end underweight, preserving income and roll-down while limiting damage if long-end term premium expands again.

Credit should lean toward IG income and shorter-to-intermediate maturity, not HY beta. LQD’s 0.62% decline shows that IG is still exposed to duration, but its income and quality remain better than lower-quality credit. HYG’s decline reminds investors that HY is not a safe asset. If LQD holds 108 and HYG holds 79, IG exposure can be retained; if both break, reduce credit beta.

TWD-denominated overseas bond ETFs require three-way attribution: FX translation, UST price return and credit spread return. USD/TWD at 31.475 helps TWD NAV because the underlying USD bonds translate into more TWD. But Treasury and LQD losses can offset part of that benefit. The decision cannot rely only on DXY or TWD depreciation; it must separate the three return channels.

High-distribution and long-duration ETFs should not be treated as free carry. Higher yields improve future income, but price losses hit NAV first when yields rise quickly. If the speed of rate adjustment exceeds income accrual, distribution yield will not protect principal in the short run. The more practical stance is to keep existing income exposure, avoid chasing, and use levels to decide staged adds.

EM and Asia USD bonds should be filtered by policy credibility. Indonesia FX defense, corporate Japan leverage and Asia tech pressure are not the core UST story, but they affect Asia credit and sovereign spreads. If USD/IDR stays above 18,000 and policy delivery disappoints, EM local and weaker Asia credit require more compensation. Taiwan investors should prefer short maturity and quasi-sovereign quality.

The overall stance is prepare and conditional exposure, not broad risk expansion. The data support better belly yields and a short-term FX translation benefit for TWD NAV, but CPI, oil, the 30Y and LQD still need confirmation. If all four improve, belly duration can be moved from watch to higher confidence. If any worsens, prioritize liquidity and high-quality income.

Watchlist

5. Watchlist

This week is about CPI, FOMC language, Brent, the 30Y and LQD; each trigger maps to a position change.

The first trigger is CPI. If the June 10 print is near the roughly 4.3% implied by swaps, the front end will test hike-tail risk again, the 10Y can move toward 4.65%, and 5Y-10Y adds should pause. If CPI is below 4.1%, the payroll shock lacks inflation confirmation and belly duration can be moved from watch to staged add.

The second trigger is FOMC language. If the June 16-17 statement removes cut-friendly language or stresses inflation credibility, the 2Y and 5Y remain more sensitive than the 30Y and bear flattening can persist. If the statement preserves two-sided risk and downplays hike optionality, the belly can attract buyers again.

The third trigger is the 10Y and 30Y level board. A 10Y break below 4.45% would show that buyers are taking control; 4.55%-4.65% is the risk-management zone. A 30Y break above 5.05% means term premium and supply are back in control, so 20Y-plus ETF risk should be cut. Below 4.95% would be a more credible long-end repair signal.

The fourth trigger is oil. Brent below 92 helps cool inflation premium and gives the Fed more communication flexibility. Brent above 98 would lift energy inflation, real-income pressure and long-end breakeven pressure. Oil is not a standalone commodity input here; it is a lead indicator for CPI risk and long-end repair.

The fifth trigger is LQD and HYG. LQD above 108 and HYG above 79 would show that credit proxies are absorbing the rates shock, allowing IG income exposure to remain. If both break lower, rates and spreads are hurting USD bond ETFs at the same time, and investors should not mistake FX translation support for total-return support.

The sixth trigger is USD/TWD. Below 31.40, TWD strength reduces the FX translation cushion and overseas bond ETFs rely more on UST and credit performance. Above 31.55, FX support improves, but if it comes with oil and Fed stress, underlying bond losses can still offset it.

The seventh trigger is Asia policy defense. Indonesia stabilizing USD/IDR below 18,000 with credible policy delivery would help EM beta. If policy falls short, EM local debt and Asia credit spreads need more compensation. This is an EM/Asia risk monitor, not a replacement for U.S. CPI or the Fed as the main rates driver.

The final trigger is whether AI financing pressure stabilizes. SOX back above 12,800 with the 10Y below 4.50% would reduce concern about equity-duration and credit deleveraging. SOX below 12,000 with LQD falling would turn AI from a growth story into a funding-demand and credit-supply pressure point.

The 2s10s has flattened 12bp over the month, and the 5s30s has flattened 18bp. That structure says the market has moved from debating the timing of cuts to debating whether the Fed needs to preserve optionality to hike again.

The long end did not receive much protection from the equity selloff, and that is the key signal. The 30Y at 5.01% and the 20Y at 5.03% failed to rally cleanly even as the Nasdaq 100 and SOX sold off heavily. For Taiwan-listed long Treasury ETFs, that means a weaker equity tape alone is not enough to assume NAV repair. The long end needs a break below 5%, lower oil, and less supply pressure.

Oil is the second constraint on long-end repair. Brent and WTI fell on the U.S. close, but Brent rebounded toward 95.60 in early Asia trading after renewed geopolitical stress. That keeps the inflation-premium channel alive. The 10Y at 4.55% should not be treated as a safe high if CPI comes in near the roughly 4.3% level implied by swaps.

Cross-asset closes also point to discount-rate repricing rather than a plain risk-off rotation. The S&P 500 fell 2.6%, the Nasdaq Composite fell 4.2%, the Nasdaq 100 fell 4.8%, and the SOX fell 10.3%, while DXY rose to 100.07 and gold fell 3.1%. The market was not only hiding in safe assets; it was demanding a higher discount rate for long-duration cash flows.

The first read for Taiwan bond ETF allocation is therefore clear: front-end and belly rates risk is back, the long end is still constrained by term-premium and supply pressure, and credit plus equity-duration assets are under pressure at the same time. Looking only at the absolute 10Y yield would miss the CPI/Fed risk in the 2Y-5Y area; looking only at the tech selloff would overstate the protection for 20Y-plus exposure.

2. Rates InterpretationFed pricing has shifted from waiting for cuts to testing anti-inflation credibility; the belly is more actionable than the long end.

The key change in rates pricing is the Fed reaction function. The market has moved from waiting for cut conditions to testing whether the Fed must defend inflation credibility. After payrolls, year-end hike optionality was priced back in, with October hike odds pushed toward roughly 60%. That does not mean a hike is certain, but it means the front end can no longer price a one-way path toward cuts.

CPI is the next dividing line. CPI swaps imply roughly 4.3% y/y for the upcoming release. If the print is close to that level, the 10Y at 4.55% is not a ceiling and the market can test 4.65%, while the June FOMC statement would likely need to remove cut-friendly language. If CPI is below 4.1%, the payroll shock can be treated as an overreaction in the front end and the belly can repair more cleanly.

Policy communication is now more important. Warsh’s first meeting has to face strong payrolls, an oil rebound, equity deleveraging and a split economist community: Goldman has removed cuts for this year while Citi remains a dovish outlier. When forecasts are dispersed, every official phrase becomes an input to the probability distribution. That is why the 2Y break above 4.15% matters more than the 30Y day move.

Curve trades are not clean on either side. The 2s10s at 38bp and 5s30s at 72bp already reflect a lot of flattening. Chasing that flattening requires another CPI or Fed-language confirmation. At the same time, buying a long-end steepener too early is risky because the 30Y remains above 5%. The cleaner expression is staged 5Y-10Y exposure while keeping a small underweight in the long end.

The long-end issue is not one event. Fiscal supply, long-dated issuance absorption, mortgage hedging, oil-linked inflation premium and AI funding demand all affect whether the 30Y can stay below 5%. These channels do not dominate every day, but when a major equity selloff fails to pull the 30Y lower, it shows that long-end buyers still require a high yield to step in.

Canada employment is supporting evidence, not the main driver. Canada added 87.8k jobs and the unemployment rate fell to 6.6%, reinforcing that North American demand resilience is not purely a U.S. data point. For Taiwan investors, this lowers the probability of a quick-cut narrative and makes short-to-intermediate income more attractive than simply extending duration.

The dollar and gold also support the real-yield interpretation. DXY rose 0.66% and gold fell 3.1%, which means the market was repricing real returns and dollar yield advantage, not only buying havens. For TWD-denominated overseas bond ETFs, the stronger dollar helps FX translation, but higher real yields and weaker credit proxies pressure the underlying USD bond prices.

The strategy conclusion is not to add broadly just because yields are higher. The correct framework is to acknowledge that Fed-tail risk is back, that long-end term premium has not disappeared, and that exposure should be conditional. CPI below 4.1%, the 10Y back toward 4.45%-4.50%, and Brent below 92 would support adding belly duration; CPI near 4.3% or the 30Y above 5.05% would argue for pausing.

3. Cross-Market ReadAI, IG credit, Asia FX and oil all confirm the same message: the cost of capital has repriced higher.

The equity signal is not simply that stocks fell. The long-duration equity complex was repriced. The Nasdaq 100 fell 4.8% and the SOX fell 10.3%, far worse than the Dow’s 1.3% decline, showing that AI and semiconductors were the first assets hit by a higher discount rate. That lines up with the move in the Treasury front end.

AI funding turns this from an equity story into a credit story. Apollo/Anthropic chip financing, SpaceX IPO demand and large data-center or power investments all show that the AI narrative needs large pools of external capital. When 5Y-10Y yields move higher, that affects not only equity multiples but also IG substitute supply, private-credit structures and long-dated credit spreads.

LQD is the important credit proxy for this tape. LQD closed at 108.17, down 0.62%, while HYG fell 0.50% to 79.43. IG duration and HY beta were both under pressure. IG still offers income, but when it falls together with Treasuries, credit cannot be summarized as stable risk appetite. It has to be decomposed into duration, spread compensation, primary quality and ETF liquidity.

Credit events also argue against reading only the index. Raizen restructuring, AI-linked financing and rising leverage in corporate Japan are micro examples of what higher funding costs can do. These do not imply systemic credit stress, but they reduce the cushion for weak or long-duration credit. Taiwan bond ETF allocation should preserve IG income while demanding more compensation for HY and EM exposure.

Japan and Asia belong in cross-market validation rather than the core Fed driver. Weak Nikkei futures, pressure on tech shares and higher BOJ-hike expectations affect Asia open risk and potential JGB spillover into global duration. They are not the main reason USTs bear-flattened on June 5. The correct read is that U.S. front-end repricing is the primary driver, while Japan and Asia determine whether a second global-duration wave follows.

Indonesia and Asia FX are risk-monitor items, not core rates drivers. Indonesian equities are far below their highs and the rupiah is down around 8% year to date after breaching 18,000. That matters for EM and Asia credit exposure because local bonds need higher real yields and stronger policy credibility. It should be watched for EM beta, not treated as the same driver as U.S. payrolls and CPI.

Oil reconnects cross-asset and rates. The U.S. close showed lower Brent and WTI, which would normally help inflation premium. But Brent’s rebound toward 95.60 in early Asia means geopolitics is still active. For rates, oil is not a separate commodity theme; it feeds CPI, real incomes, Fed language and long-end breakeven pressure. Brent back above 98 would make duration repair harder.

USD/TWD must be interpreted through product mechanics. The CBC USD/TWD close at 31.475 is positive for the TWD NAV translation of USD bond ETFs. But the same day also saw the 10Y up 8bp and LQD down 0.62%. The cross-asset conclusion is therefore not that a stronger dollar is automatically bullish for ETFs; FX helps, while rates and credit can offset it.

4. Allocation ReadAllocation should separate belly duration, IG income and FX translation instead of forcing one broad risk call.

The duration stance is neutral-to-slightly long, but only through staged 5Y-10Y exposure. The 10Y at 4.55% is more attractive than a week ago, but payrolls, a 4.3% CPI-swap setup and Fed statement risk argue against buying the full position at once. The first tranche can be observed around 4.55%-4.60%; the second requires CPI below 4.1% or a move back toward 4.45%-4.50%.

Long Treasury ETFs should not be chased. The 30Y at 5.01% offers yield, but it did not rally meaningfully during the equity selloff. If the 30Y breaks above 5.05%, price risk in 20Y-plus ETFs rises again. A move below 4.95% alongside Brent below 92 would be a cleaner sign that term premium and inflation premium are both cooling.

Curve allocation should avoid chasing a move that has already happened. The 2s10s at 38bp and 5s30s at 72bp already reflect a significant amount of front-end repricing. Further flattening needs CPI or Fed confirmation. A better expression is belly overweight, small long-end underweight, preserving income and roll-down while limiting damage if long-end term premium expands again.

Credit should lean toward IG income and shorter-to-intermediate maturity, not HY beta. LQD’s 0.62% decline shows that IG is still exposed to duration, but its income and quality remain better than lower-quality credit. HYG’s decline reminds investors that HY is not a safe asset. If LQD holds 108 and HYG holds 79, IG exposure can be retained; if both break, reduce credit beta.

TWD-denominated overseas bond ETFs require three-way attribution: FX translation, UST price return and credit spread return. USD/TWD at 31.475 helps TWD NAV because the underlying USD bonds translate into more TWD. But Treasury and LQD losses can offset part of that benefit. The decision cannot rely only on DXY or TWD depreciation; it must separate the three return channels.

High-distribution and long-duration ETFs should not be treated as free carry. Higher yields improve future income, but price losses hit NAV first when yields rise quickly. If the speed of rate adjustment exceeds income accrual, distribution yield will not protect principal in the short run. The more practical stance is to keep existing income exposure, avoid chasing, and use levels to decide staged adds.

EM and Asia USD bonds should be filtered by policy credibility. Indonesia FX defense, corporate Japan leverage and Asia tech pressure are not the core UST story, but they affect Asia credit and sovereign spreads. If USD/IDR stays above 18,000 and policy delivery disappoints, EM local and weaker Asia credit require more compensation. Taiwan investors should prefer short maturity and quasi-sovereign quality.

The overall stance is prepare and conditional exposure, not broad risk expansion. The data support better belly yields and a short-term FX translation benefit for TWD NAV, but CPI, oil, the 30Y and LQD still need confirmation. If all four improve, belly duration can be moved from watch to higher confidence. If any worsens, prioritize liquidity and high-quality income.
