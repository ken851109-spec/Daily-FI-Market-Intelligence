投資解讀

配置維持中段小幅承接、高品質收益與現金緩衝；20Y+、HY beta 與新興市場利率先等確認。

盤面定義

一、UST 收盤與曲線變化

美債小幅反彈只是 CPI 前部位回補；2Y 仍在 4.13%、30Y 仍壓近 5%，升息與長端風險未解除。

曲線訊號比單一殖利率更重要。2s10s 收在約 +40 bp，日內小幅壓平；5s30s 收 +75 bp、10s30s 收 +48 bp，長端相對中段仍未給出乾淨的反彈。對長天期 ETF 而言，30Y 仍在 5% 上方，使 DV01 風險與期限溢價都停留在壓力區，不能把 10Y 回落 3 bp 直接解讀成全面加久期的訊號。換成 DV01 管理，現在更適合先控制槓桿與天期集中度，等事件風險釋放後再把觀察倉位轉成確認部位。

日變動雖然偏多，但周變動與月變動仍偏空。2Y 較 6 月 2 日高 8 bp、較 5 月 8 日高 23 bp；5Y 較一周前高 9 bp、較一個月前高 24 bp；10Y 分別高 7 bp 與 15 bp。這代表 Friday 非農就業後的重新定價只出現局部回吐，市場仍把更強就業、油價與 CPI 風險放進利率曲線。這也意味著下滑收益與票息可以留下，但若政策重新定價擴大，價格損失會快速吃掉當日收益。

中段 5Y-10Y 是本日相對可承接的區域，因為 5Y 與 10Y 日內各回落 3 bp，且 5s30s 未再明顯轉陡。若 CPI 沒有高於預期，5Y-10Y 可透過收益、下滑收益與較低凸性風險取得較穩定的反彈；但若核心 CPI 與能源項同步走高，這段仍會先被 Fed 利率路徑重新定價打到，不能用月內高殖利率當作單一買進理由。若後續數字沒有支持這個通道，部位應回到高品質收益與現金緩衝，避免把短線反彈誤認成趨勢反轉。

定價邏輯

二、美債走勢解讀

政策定價仍由就業韌性、CPI 上行風險與油價尾端共同主導，Fed 不必降息的敘事尚未被推翻。

本日利率解讀要從強就業後的政策路徑開始。公開資料與市場報導均指向 May 非農就業約 +172k，且先前月份上修，使 Fed cuts 更遠、升息保護更貴。即使 6 月 9 日收盤美債殖利率回落，這只是投資人等 CPI 前先鎖定部分獲利，並不是勞動市場放緩或通膨降溫已被確認。換成 DV01 管理，現在更適合先控制槓桿與天期集中度，等事件風險釋放後再把觀察倉位轉成確認部位。

CPI 是下一個政策定價檢查點。市場預期 5 月整體 CPI 約 +0.5% m/m、+4.2% y/y，核心約 +0.3% m/m、+2.9% y/y；若公布值符合或高於這組數字，前端會重新測試升息風險，因為油價、運輸與部分服務價格會讓 Fed 很難把通膨歸類成短暫雜訊。若低於預期，才有條件讓 5Y-10Y 反彈延伸到較乾淨的多頭壓平。這也意味著下滑收益與票息可以留下，但若政策重新定價擴大，價格損失會快速吃掉當日收益。

油價是政策定價的第二條線。WTI 收 $89.12、Brent 收 $92.36，日內雖回落約 2%，但清晨 Markets Wrap 已顯示美軍打擊伊朗、WTI 盤後反彈到 $89 一帶。能源收盤回落降低了當天通膨平衡壓力，卻沒有消除 6 月 CPI 與後續汽油傳導的尾端風險；這會使 Fed language 很難快速轉鴿。若後續數字沒有支持這個通道，部位應回到高品質收益與現金緩衝，避免把短線反彈誤認成趨勢反轉。

全球央行同步提高了美債反彈的門檻。BOJ 調查顯示 49/51 位經濟學家預期 6 月 16 日升息 25 bp 到 1.00%，年末中位數看 1.25%；Bank Indonesia 已突襲升息至 5.50% 以防 rupiah 失序；BoC 雖可能按兵不動，但也無法忽略油價對通膨的 second-round effects。這些不是美債當天的直接主因，卻是全球久期估值不能過快下修的背景。換成 DV01 管理，現在更適合先控制槓桿與天期集中度，等事件風險釋放後再把觀察倉位轉成確認部位。

所以，今日策略語言應該是「利率高位整理中的選擇性承接」，不是全面加風險。若 CPI 低於預期且 10Y 收回 4.45% 下方，5Y-10Y 的價格彈性可以被放大；若 CPI 高於預期或 30Y 再站上 5.08%，前一日反彈會被視為短暫 cover，曲線風險會重新壓回長天期 ETF。這也意味著下滑收益與票息可以留下，但若政策重新定價擴大，價格損失會快速吃掉當日收益。

跨市驗證

三、其他市場動向

股票市場的訊號是分裂的。S&P 500 收 7,386.65、跌 0.26%；Nasdaq Composite 跌 0.97%、Nasdaq 100 跌 1.12%、SOX 跌 1.93%，但 Dow 小漲 0.17%。九大類股上漲與科技回落同時出現，代表市場不是全面 risk-off，而是從擁擠的 AI / mega-上限 trade 轉向較防禦、較低估值的板塊。這也意味著下滑收益與票息可以留下，但若政策重新定價擴大，價格損失會快速吃掉當日收益。

油價收盤回落與地緣升級之間存在時間差。WTI 6 月 9 日收 $89.12、Brent 收 $92.36，較前一日各跌約 2%，但後續美軍打擊伊朗使亞洲盤前油價回升。這使表面通膨壓力在收盤數據上降溫，事件風險卻在台北早盤重新升高；對 CPI、通膨平衡與 Fed 語句，市場仍會要求 oil-risk premium。若後續數字沒有支持這個通道，部位應回到高品質收益與現金緩衝，避免把短線反彈誤認成趨勢反轉。

Credit proxy 的表現較穩，但不是加大 beta 的訊號。LQD 收 108.41、漲 0.32%，HYG 收 79.62、漲 0.10%；IG proxy 受益於利率回落與高品質收益，HY beta 只小幅改善。MSD Investment 3Y IG note 最終 T+240 bp、由 T+270 bp initial talk 收窄，顯示有收益補償與投資級故事仍能成交，但 Shutterfly 12% 殖利率與 broadband distress 說明低品質信用仍需結構保護。這也意味著下滑收益與票息可以留下，但若政策重新定價擴大，價格損失會快速吃掉當日收益。

配置動作

四、配置含義

配置維持中段小幅承接、高品質收益與現金緩衝；20Y+、HY beta 與新興市場利率先等確認。

曲線表達優先於單純拉長年期。2s10s 約 +40 bp、5s30s +75 bp，顯示前端升息風險與長端期限溢價同時存在。若 CPI 低於預期，可用 5Y-10Y 中段反彈或 5s30s 小幅壓平表達；若 CPI 高於預期，應避免把部位集中在 2Y-5Y，因為前端會先反映 Fed 利率路徑上修。若後續數字沒有支持這個通道，部位應回到高品質收益與現金緩衝，避免把短線反彈誤認成趨勢反轉。

現金與短票部位仍有價值，因為 3M-1Y 官方 UST 曲線約 3.79%-3.90%，短端收益不低，且 CPI 與 FOMC 前的不確定性高。若投資組合已有長天期 ETF，新增資金不必急著拉長；用短票或 2Y-5Y ladder 保留再平衡能力，比在 30Y 仍 5% 上方時一次買滿更符合風險報酬。這也意味著下滑收益與票息可以留下，但若政策重新定價擴大，價格損失會快速吃掉當日收益。

交易觸發

五、後續觀察重點

接下來的觸發集中在 CPI、10Y 4.45/4.60、30Y 4.95/5.

第一個觸發是 U.S.CPI。若 headline 低於 +0.5% m/m 且 core 低於 +0.3% m/m，10Y 有機會測試 4.45% 下方，5Y-10Y 久期可由觀察區轉為初步加碼；若 headline 或 core 高於預期，2Y 會重新靠近 4.20%，前端升息保護會壓回整條曲線。若後續數字沒有支持這個通道，部位應回到高品質收益與現金緩衝，避免把短線反彈誤認成趨勢反轉。

第四個觸發是 10Y auction 與 30Y supply。3Y note 已以 4.192% 高殖利率成交，本周更重要的是長端供給能否在 5% 附近被實質買盤吸收。若標售尾端價差擴大或間接投標需求偏弱，10s30s 可能再轉陡，20Y+ ETF 要延後；若長端供給被順利吸收，CPI 後反彈才有延續條件。這也意味著下滑收益與票息可以留下，但若政策重新定價擴大，價格損失會快速吃掉當日收益。

Rates Close

1. UST Close and Curve Moves

The UST rally is pre-CPI covering, not a removal of hike or long-end risk.

The June 9 UST close was a price rebound, not a clean risk reset. Treasury CMT puts 2Y at 4. 13%, 5Y at 4. 26%, 10Y at 4. 53%, 20Y at 5. 02% and 30Y at 5. 01%, down roughly 2-3 bp on the day. The level is still well above both one-week and one-month anchors, so the market has not removed the Fed tightening tail from the curve.

The curve matters more than the single-day rally. 2s10s closed near +40 bp, slightly flatter on the day; 5s30s closed at +75 bp and 10s30s at +48 bp. For long-duration bond ETFs, a 30Y yield still above 5% keeps DV01 and term-premium risk in the pressure zone, so a 3 bp decline in 10Y is not enough to justify broad duration extension.

The day change is constructive, but the weekly and monthly changes are still bearish. 2Y is 8 bp above June 2 and 23 bp above May 8; 5Y is 9 bp and 24 bp higher; 10Y is 7 bp and 15 bp higher. The payroll-driven repricing has only partially retraced, while jobs strength, oil and CPI risk remain embedded in rates.

The front-end point is not the 2 bp decline, but the fact that 2Y is still above the top of the Fed target range. SOFR option and futures flows still point to protection against at least one hike, with some structures targeting action around September. That keeps the 2Y-5Y sector highly sensitive to CPI and makes carry less stable than it looks.

The 5Y-10Y sector is the more investable part of the curve for now. Both 5Y and 10Y declined 3 bp and 5s30s did not steepen materially. If CPI is not above expectations, this sector can benefit from carry, roll-down and lower convexity risk; if core CPI and energy are both hot, Fed-path repricing will hit the belly first.

The long end remains the constraint. 20Y at 5. 02% and 30Y at 5. 01% show some pre-CPI duration demand but no abandonment of fiscal, supply and AI-infrastructure term premium. The $58 billion 3Y auction cleared at a 4. 192% high yield and the market still has 10Y and 30Y supply ahead, so any long-end rally needs both CPI and auction confirmation.

Rates Interpretation

2. UST Interpretation

Jobs resilience, CPI risk and oil still dominate policy pricing.

The rates interpretation starts with the policy path after strong jobs data. Public reports point to May payrolls near 172k with upward revisions, pushing Fed cuts further away and making hike protection more expensive. The June 9 yield decline is therefore pre-CPI profit taking, not confirmation that labor or inflation pressure has cooled.

CPI is the next policy-pricing check. Consensus looks for headline CPI around +0. 5% m/m and +4. 2% y/y, with core around +0. 3% m/m and +2. 9% y/y. If the release meets or beats those estimates, the front end can retest hike risk because energy, transport and services would be hard for the Fed to dismiss as noise.

The market message before Chair Warsh first FOMC is direct: 2Y at 4. 13% is above the top of the policy band, and 10Y at 4. 53% is close to a pressure level for mortgages and corporate funding. The market has tightened some financial conditions for the Fed, but it also demands proof of anti-inflation credibility.

Oil is the second leg of policy pricing. WTI closed at $89. 12 and Brent at $92. 36, both down about 2%, but the morning market packet pointed to renewed gains after U. S. strikes on Iran. The closing data reduce the immediate breakeven impulse, while the event risk keeps an oil-risk premium in CPI and Fed language.

Supply and capital demand are the third leg. The 3Y note cleared at 4. 192%, up from 3. 965% last time; AI infrastructure, the SpaceX IPO, the OpenAI and Anthropic pipeline, and potential CoreWeave debt issuance all point to continuing capital absorption. That makes term premium more persistent than front-end relief.

Global central banks also raise the hurdle for a UST rally. BOJ economists expect a June hike to 1. 00%, Bank Indonesia already surprised with a hike to defend the rupiah, and the Bank of Canada may stay on hold while still acknowledging energy inflation. These are not the direct UST close drivers, but they keep global duration valuation from easing too quickly.

The right strategy language is selective buying in a high-rate range, not broad risk-on. If CPI is below expectations and 10Y closes below 4. 45%, the 5Y-10Y rally can extend. If CPI is hot or 30Y moves back above 5. 08%, the prior rally becomes only short covering and curve risk returns to long-duration ETFs.

Cross-Market Read

3. Cross-Market Moves

The equity signal is split. The S&P 500 closed at 7,386. 65, down 0. 26%; Nasdaq Composite fell 0. 97%, Nasdaq 100 fell 1. 12% and SOX fell 1. 93%, while the Dow rose 0. 17%. The tape shows rotation away from crowded AI and mega-cap positions toward more defensive and lower-valuation sectors.

That rotation has two implications for bonds. It can support a modest pre-CPI Treasury bid by reducing overheated equity risk, but sustained IPO supply and AI funding needs can also lift liquidity demand and pressure valuations. The FI book should not treat technology weakness as a one-way safe-haven bond signal.

There is a timing gap between lower oil closes and higher geopolitical risk. WTI closed at $89. 12 and Brent at $92. 36, down roughly 2%, but U. S. strikes on Iran pushed oil higher into the Asia morning. The closing tape looks less inflationary, while the event tape keeps CPI and breakeven risk alive.

Gold fell 2. 11% to $4,244. 20, showing that haven demand did not overpower real-rate and dollar pressure. If the market were in full risk-off mode, gold and long-end USTs would usually benefit together. Gold down and 30Y above 5% instead point to inflation and policy risk, not simple safety demand.

Credit proxies were stable but not a beta signal. LQD rose 0. 32% to 108. 41 and HYG rose 0. 10% to 79. 62; the IG proxy benefited more from rates and carry. MSD Investment tightened to T+240 bp from T+270 bp initial talk, while Shutterfly still needed a 12% yield and covenant concessions, keeping credit selection ahead of broad beta.

Allocation Read

4. Allocation Implications

Stay staged in 5Y-10Y, high-quality carry and cash; wait for confirmation before adding 20Y+ or HY beta.

Duration positioning should stay modestly constructive but staged. The preferred zone is 5Y-10Y because carry and price convexity have improved, while 30Y at 5. 01% remains in the pressure zone. Long-end ETFs need a cleaner move below 4. 95% in 30Y before size should be increased.

Curve expression is better than simply extending maturity. With 2s10s near +40 bp and 5s30s near +75 bp, front-end hike risk and long-end term premium coexist. A soft CPI supports 5Y-10Y or a modest 5s30s flattener; a hot CPI argues against concentrating exposure in 2Y-5Y.

Credit and ETF exposure should favor high-quality carry. LQD outperformed HYG and MSD Investment showed that IG supply can clear when compensation is adequate. Shutterfly illustrates that lower-quality issuers still need very high yields and tighter creditor protections, so spread beta is not free income.

Cash and bills still have value. Treasury CMT levels around 3. 79%-3. 90% in the 3M-1Y area provide income while preserving optionality before CPI and FOMC. Portfolios already holding long-duration ETFs do not need to extend immediately while 30Y remains above 5%.

Watchlist

5. Watchlist

The key triggers are CPI, 10Y 4. 45/4. 60, 30Y 4. 95/5.

The first trigger is U. S. CPI. If headline is below +0. 5% m/m and core is below +0. 3% m/m, 10Y can test below 4. 45% and 5Y-10Y duration can move from observation to an initial add. If either line is hot, 2Y can move back toward 4. 20% and front-end hike protection returns.

The second trigger is the long end. A 30Y close below 4. 95% for two sessions would show that term-premium pressure is easing and make 20Y+ ETFs more attractive. A move back above 5. 08% would mark a failed long-end rally and argue for lower DV01.

The fourth trigger is 10Y and 30Y supply. The 3Y note already cleared at a higher 4. 192% yield; the long-end auctions will show whether real money absorbs duration near 5%. Weak indirect demand or a larger tail would delay 20Y+ ETF adds.

The seventh trigger is global central banks. A BOJ hike with slower purchase reductions would be manageable for global duration; a hawkish hike with unchanged tapering would pressure long-end rates. BoC, ECB and Asian central-bank language will show whether oil is treated as temporary or requires a higher policy premium.
